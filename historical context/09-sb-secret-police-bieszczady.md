# SB (Służba Bezpieczeństwa) in Bieszczady

## Overview

After Akcja Wisła (1947), the Urząd Bezpieczeństwa (UB, later reorganized as Służba Bezpieczeństwa / SB) maintained a heavy presence in the Bieszczady region. Their primary mission: ensure the deportations stuck and suppress any revival of Ukrainian/Lemko identity.

## Activities

- **Surveillance networks** — Dense web of agents and informants across the region.
- **Infiltration** — SB agents embedded in local communities. Locals recruited as informants through coercion, blackmail, or promises.
- **Hunting UPA remnants** — Collaborated with military (KBW) to track and eliminate remaining partisan forces.
- **Suppressing returns** — Monitored and prevented displaced Lemkos/Ukrainians from returning to their homeland.
- **Targeting cultural preservation** — Lemko organizations, Greek Catholic/Orthodox church activities, and any attempts at commemoration were surveilled and disrupted.
- **Eliminating local elites** — Clergy, teachers, and educated figures sent to Jaworzno internment camp to prevent organized resistance.

## Methods

- Agent recruitment through pressure, blackmail, or reward.
- Physical surveillance and patrols.
- Interception of correspondence.
- Intimidation, arrest, and detention.
- Control of information — ensuring no public acknowledgment of Akcja Wisła atrocities.
