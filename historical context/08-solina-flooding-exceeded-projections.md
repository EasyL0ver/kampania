# Solina Dam — Flooding Exceeded Projections

## Overview

Multiple sources indicate that the actual flooding caused by the Solina reservoir exceeded original engineering projections, particularly during the final filling phase in 1967.

## Key Facts

- **September 1967:** Full filling of the reservoir began. Intense rainfall during this period pushed water levels beyond what hydrologists had predicted.
- **Larger flood zone:** Locals reported areas being submerged that were not part of the original displacement plan.
- **Unprepared exhumations:** Graves and structures that hadn't been dealt with in time were swallowed by the rising water. Skulls and bones continued to surface long after the flooding (*"Długo jeszcze potem wypływały czaszki, kości"*).
- **PRL cover-up:** The regime presented the dam as a triumph of socialist engineering. Any admission of miscalculation or inadequate preparation was suppressed.
