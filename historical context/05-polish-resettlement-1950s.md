# Post-1947 Polish Resettlement of Bieszczady

## Overview

After Operation Vistula emptied the Bieszczady of its Lemko, Boyko, and Ukrainian population, the region was nearly deserted for several years. Beginning in the early 1950s, the communist state organized resettlement campaigns to repopulate the area with Polish colonists.

## Who Came

- **Repatriates from Kresy** — Poles displaced from eastern territories annexed by the USSR.
- **Farmers** — From central and southern Poland, seeking land.
- **Adventurers and drifters** — The so-called "Bieszczady pioneers," people seeking freedom or escape. The region had a reputation as the "Polish Wild West."
- **Fugitives** — Criminals, deserters, people hiding from the law.
- **State-directed workers** — Sent to staff forestry operations, PGR state farms, and infrastructure projects.

## Conditions

- Buildings inherited from deported populations were often ruined or decayed.
- Land was neglected, overgrown after years of abandonment.
- Infrastructure nearly nonexistent — no paved roads, no reliable utilities.
- Fear of remaining UPA partisan bands persisted into the early 1950s.
- Communities were artificial — strangers from different regions with no shared history or social fabric.
- The state marketed Bieszczady as a "promised land" (*ziemia obiecana*) but reality was grim.

## Relationship to Former Lemko Villages

Settlers often built next to or directly on former Lemko village sites. They lived among the ruins:
- Decaying wooden churches (*cerkwie*), sometimes repurposed as storage.
- Overgrown cemeteries with Cyrillic inscriptions.
- Foundations, root cellars, old orchards still bearing fruit.
- Personal belongings left behind in the 1947 rush.

The previous inhabitants were a ghostly presence — everywhere visible, nowhere acknowledged. The state did not discuss who had lived there or what had happened to them.

## Timeline

- **1947–early 1950s:** Region nearly empty. Some military/border guard presence.
- **Early 1950s:** First organized settlement waves arrive.
- **Late 1950s–1960s:** Communities slowly stabilize. Infrastructure projects begin (roads, dam).
- **1960s:** Solina Dam construction begins — some of these newly settled communities face displacement themselves.
