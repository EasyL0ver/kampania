## Krywe — A Real Template

Krywe is a vanished Bieszczady village in the San valley, at the foot of Otryt. First mentioned 1526.

### Demographics (1921 census)
- 74 houses
- 443 Rusins (Bojkos — on the Łemko-Bojko ethnic border)
- 9 Poles
- 20 Jews

### What happened
- Part of residents deported to Ukraine in 1946
- Remaining population forcibly resettled via Akcja Wisła in 1947
- **Village buildings were burned to erase traces**
- Village emptied completely

### The Cerkiew
- Cerkiew św. Paraskewii — built 1842, on a hilltop called Diłok
- First cerkiew on site mentioned in 1589
- After 1947: roof stripped of metal sheeting, interior vandalized, building set on fire
- **The fire did not destroy it completely** — ruins survive to this day
- Bell tower next to the church (post-1852)
- **A chest containing UPA security services archives was found hidden inside the cerkiew ruins in the 1980s**
- The hilltop with cerkiew, bell tower and cemetery dominates the empty valley below — "unreal impression"

### The Cemetery
- Neglected cemetery north of the cerkiew
- Only a few headstones remain with barely readable inscriptions
- Several earthen graves

### Post-War Use
- PGR (State Agricultural Enterprise) from Czarna Dolna operated on the land
- **Ośrodek Pracy Więźniów (Prisoner Work Camp)** existed until 1978
- After camp closure: PGR from Lutowiska ran land recultivation
- Bridge over San built, collapsed 1999
- Later: Kombinat Rolno-Przemysłowy Igloopol, then private hands

### Physical Remains Today
- Cerkiew ruins — best preserved ruined cerkiew in all Bieszczady
- Bell tower
- Cemetery fragments
- Along Krywiec stream: **hidden wells and cellars** overgrown in undergrowth
- Foundations and building outlines visible in grass
- Manor house foundations
- Tartak (sawmill) remains

### Cultural Note
- 1934 academic study placed Krywe on the Łemko-Bojko transition zone
- The village had: school, steam sawmill, manor, folwark (farm estate), mills

## Documented Violence Against Ukrainians (1944-1947)

From apokryfruski.org database — documented killings in the Bieszczady region:

### Patterns
- Military units (KBW, WP) and militia conducting killings during Akcja Wisła operations
- AK/WiN units attacking Ukrainian civilians in 1944-46
- Methods: shooting, drowning (bodies thrown in rivers), burning buildings with people inside
- Victims included women, children, elderly
- Bodies frequently disposed in water (rivers, streams)
- "Stójki" (checkpoints) where civilians were stripped, robbed, killed
- Killings often triggered by suspicion of UPA connection — real or imagined

### Baligród Area (closest to our setting)
- Documented killing of a Ukrainian teacher by soldiers: forced to jump from a bridge, shot while running
- Pattern of checkpoint violence along the San river
- AK units from neighboring villages running armed checkpoints
