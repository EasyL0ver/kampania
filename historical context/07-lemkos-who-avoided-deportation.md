# Lemkos Who Avoided Deportation

## Overview

While Operation Vistula displaced ~150,000 people, a small number of Lemkos managed to remain in or near the Bieszczady region. They survived through:

- **Hidden identity** — suppressing Lemko culture, language, and religion for decades.
- **Mixed families** — marriages with Poles provided cover; the "Polish side" of the family was emphasized.
- **Document changes** — altered names, switched from Greek Catholic to Roman Catholic rites.
- **Help from neighbors** — some Polish families sheltered or vouched for Lemko neighbors.
- **Hiding in forests** — some fled into the mountains during the roundups and returned after the military left.

## The Price of Staying

- Complete suppression of cultural identity — language, traditions, religion all buried.
- Children often didn't know their own heritage.
- Constant fear of discovery and delayed deportation.
- Social pressure to assimilate fully into Polish identity.
- Many families only began reconnecting with their Lemko roots after 1989.
