# Lemko Beliefs and Folk Magic

Research compiled from ethnographic sources on Lemko folk beliefs, death rites, and supernatural traditions relevant to the scenario.

## Wells as Liminal Places

In Lemko and Carpathian folklore, wells and springs are **gateways between the natural and supernatural worlds**. They are inhabited by water spirits called **vodnyk** (vodyanik) who must be respected and occasionally appeased with offerings.

Taboos around wells:
- Drawing water at night angers the spirits
- Speaking loudly near wells is forbidden
- Polluting a well brings misfortune or illness
- Protective rituals and spoken charms performed when digging, blessing, or using wells
- Sprigs of **wormwood** and **mugwort** placed near wells to ward off evil

Water taken from wells on holy feast days (especially Theophany/Epiphany) was believed to have healing and protective powers.

**Scenario relevance:** The well in %OLD_VILLAGE% fits the vodnyk archetype perfectly. Hag's herbs around the well echo protective folk practice. The villagers' fear of the well has deep cultural roots — it's not just superstition, it's doctrine.

## The Unquiet Dead

Deaths under violent or unusual circumstances (murder, suicide, accidents) create **restless or malevolent spirits**. These unquiet dead:
- Bring misfortune to the family or entire village
- Demand special precautions and rites to be appeased
- Can return through water sources and liminal spaces
- Are especially active during seasonal thinning of the veil (holidays, transitions)

A "good death" (natural, peaceful, at old age) allows safe transition. A "bad death" (violent, untimely, cursed) produces wandering spirits.

**Scenario relevance:** The 1947 massacre victims are textbook unquiet dead — violent deaths, no burial rites, no prayers, bodies in/near the well. Twenty years of unresolved spiritual debt.

## Death Rites and the 40-Day Period

### Preparation
- Body washed and dressed in plain white (sometimes unhemmed) clothes
- Belt tied around the body for protection
- Mirrors covered to prevent spirit from becoming trapped
- Clocks stopped at the moment of death

### The Vigil (Varta)
- Body watched through the night by relatives and neighbours
- Protects against evil spirits
- Ensures the soul doesn't linger dangerously close to the living

### The Panakhyda (Memorial Service)
- Prayer service for the repose of the dead
- Performed immediately after death, before burial, at graveside
- Then on the **3rd, 9th, and 40th day** after death, and every anniversary
- Includes incense, candles, hymn "Vičnaja Pamjat'" (Eternal Memory)
- The soul lingers near the living world for **40 days** — prayers during this period help it find peace

### Kolyva (Memorial Food)
- Dish of boiled wheat with honey and raisins
- Wheat symbolises resurrection (John 12:24 — grain must die to bear fruit)
- Distributed to mourners in memory of the deceased
- Almsgiving encouraged in memory of the dead

**Scenario relevance:** None of these rites were performed for the massacre victims. No washing, no vigil, no panakhyda, no 40-day prayers. The dead received nothing. Babcia's "ritual" at the well may be a corrupted, half-remembered panakhyda — or genuine folk knowledge surfacing through dementia.

## Folk Healers (Znakhary / Babky)

Village healers — often older women called **znakhary** or **babky** — used:
- Incantations spoken over water to "read away" sickness
- Holy water from sacred springs
- Herbal remedies (wormwood, mugwort, rue, juniper)
- Iron amulets, embroidered charms
- Ritual bread (krachun) for protection during liminal times

These healers blended Christian prayers with older pagan practices. They were the first line of defense against spiritual threats.

**Scenario relevance:** Both Babcia and Hag fit the babka archetype. Babcia has the knowledge but a broken mind. Hag has the practice but uses it for penance, not healing. Between them, the players can piece together what needs to be done — if they listen.

## Nature Spirits and Demons

The Lemko spiritual landscape includes:
- **Vodnyk** — water spirits in wells and rivers
- **Lisovyk** — forest spirits (wood nymphs)
- **Domovyk** — house spirits
- **Chort/Czort** — mischievous or malevolent demons
- **Ancestral spirits** — honoured during holidays with food offerings at wells and crossroads

The boundary between worlds thins during Christmas, Epiphany, and Pentecost. Spirits (benevolent or malevolent) can cross over during these times.

**Scenario relevance:** The well doesn't need to be literally supernatural. In the Lemko worldview, a mass grave in a water source with no memorial rites performed is a **spiritual catastrophe** — a hole in the world that will leak evil until it's sealed properly.

## Vampires and Revenants

Carpathian folk belief includes fear of the dead returning as vampires or revenants, especially:
- Those who died unnatural deaths
- Those not properly buried
- Those denied memorial rites

Wells and water sources are seen as both sacred and dangerous — potential return points for spirits.
