# The Solina Dam — Construction Timeline (1960–1968)

## Overview

The Solina Dam on the San River in the Bieszczady Mountains is Poland's tallest dam (82 m) and created Poland's largest artificial lake — Jezioro Solińskie (472 million m³). Designed by Feliks Niczke / Energoprojekt Warszawa.

## Early History

- **1921:** First concept for damming the San River, proposed by Prof. Karol Pomianowski.
- **1936–1937:** Initial geological and hydrological surveys. WWII halts everything.
- **1955–1960:** Post-war planning resumes. A smaller test dam built at **Myczkowce**.

## Construction Phases

### Phase 1 — Site Preparation (1960–1964)

- **1960:** Main construction begins. Dam site chosen between **Horbek Mountain** and **Jawor**.
- A workers' village built to house ~2,000 construction workers (later converted to holiday accommodation).
- Heavy earthworks, foundation digging, initial concrete pouring.
- **Major social impact begins:**
  - ~3,000 residents displaced from the flood zone.
  - Several hundred houses and outbuildings demolished.
  - Orthodox churches demolished or blown up.
  - Cemeteries "liquidated" — graves exhumed and relocated (often inadequately; remains found later).

### Phase 2 — Dam Construction (1964–1968)

- Main dam body (corpus) built: **820,000 m³ of concrete**, over **2 million tonnes**.
- 82 meters tall, 664 meters long.
- Hydropower plant installed: 2 classic + 2 reversible turbines, **200 MW** total output.
- 57 km of new roads constructed.
- **1967–1968:** Reservoir gradually fills. Final remains of villages, buildings, and landscapes submerged.

### Completion

- **20 July 1968:** Dam officially opened and operational.

## Villages Destroyed / Flooded

| Village | Status |
|---|---|
| Solina (the original village) | Entirely flooded |
| Teleśnica Sanna | Entirely flooded |
| Horodek | Entirely flooded |
| Sokole | Entirely flooded |
| Chrewt | Entirely flooded |
| Zawóz | Entirely flooded |
| Wołkowyja | Partially flooded |

Some sources cite 8–9 villages total.

## Notable Details

- When water levels drop, ruins of buildings and infrastructure resurface — foundations, walls, roads.
- Churches were demolished before flooding; cemeteries were supposed to be fully exhumed but this was done carelessly in many cases.
- The workers' settlement became a small community of its own, later repurposed for tourism.
