# Tadek Gajda

**Type:** Named character — sibling (brother)
**Freudian role:** [Ego](../story-facts/freudian-triangle.md) — the failing mediator, cracking under the weight of what he can't reconcile

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1925
- **Age in 1967:** 42

## Role

One of four siblings — brother to Zbigniew Gajda, Helena Rzepka, and Janina Gajda. Part of a family unit in %NEW_VILLAGE%.

## Heritage

Lemko descent — hidden. See Zbigniew Gajda for details on the siblings' shared background.

## Relationship to Edward Barnaś

No direct hatred. Tadek Gajda's feelings toward Edward Barnaś's family are complicated by his love for Hania Barnaś rather than ethnic or political grudge.

## Relationships

- Siblings: Zbigniew Gajda, Helena Rzepka, Janina Gajda
- **Deeply in love with Hania Barnaś** — but she didn't return it, preferring Emil Rzepka. Now she's dead by his hand.

## The Lynch

One of the three drunk men who attacked Hania Barnaś. **Participated in the rape** along with Stanisław Rezeń. Driven by rejection — Hania Barnaś chose Emil Rzepka over him. Her death was unintended but resulted from the violence.

## In 1967

A **rocker and alcoholic**. The guilt, the rejection, the violence — Tadek Gajda has drowned it all in booze. A wreck of a man. Volatile, unpredictable, possibly the weakest link in the cover-up — the one most likely to crack under pressure or let something slip when drunk.

Sleeping with **Barbara Kopacz** (who also sleeps with his nephew Marek Gajda). He is the **actual father of Pawełek Kopacz**, but nobody knows — not even Barbara Kopacz herself. Another secret piled on top of all the others.

## The Flood & The Committee

**Tadek Gajda doesn't care.** Government people, flood assessment, census — none of it registers. He's too deep in the bottle and too far gone to engage with bureaucracy. He won't be hostile, won't be helpful. Just absent. Another drunk ignoring the officials.

The players will likely dismiss him early. That's the trap — the man who looks like nothing is the weakest link in the cover-up and the easiest to crack once they realize he knows something.

## The Well's Influence

The well amplifies Tadek Gajda's **guilt and self-destruction.** He was already the weakest vessel — 13 years of drinking to forget. The well makes the forgetting stop working.

- The drinking increases but stops numbing. He drinks the same amount and stays painfully, horribly lucid. The one thing that kept him functional — the ability to drown it — fails.
- Fragments leak out. Not confessions — worse. Half-sentences at the wrong moment. Hania Barnaś's name muttered in his sleep. *"She didn't deserve it"* to whoever's sitting next to him. He doesn't remember saying it. Others do.
- His sleep fills with the sound of the well — water dripping, echoing, rising. He wakes up hoarse, as if he's been shouting. The drinking crew notices. Marek Gajda notices.
- The volatility spikes. He swings between maudlin weeping and sudden rage. Picks fights he'd normally avoid. Grabs people too hard. The violence under the surface — the same violence from 1954 — is closer to the skin.

The well doesn't need to break Tadek Gajda. He was already broken. It just needs to make the cracks **audible**.
