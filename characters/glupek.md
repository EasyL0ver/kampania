# %GLUPEK%

**Type:** Named character — child of [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md)

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1950
- **Age in 1967:** 17
- **Lives in:** [%CIOTKA%'s house](../places/new-village.md#ciotkas-house) — with [%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md)

## Character

Physically massive (six foot), strong, brain-damaged — **not congenital, caused by oxygen deprivation during the lynch.** No impulse control. Doesn't intend harm but doesn't know his own strength: a hug that cracks ribs, a shove into a wall, panic that becomes flailing fists. Silent, unpredictable. For players, terrifying — a large figure who shows up where they don't expect, stares, follows. Every encounter is tense.

## Background

Son of [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md). During the lynch (1954), after [%JAGNA%](jagna.md), [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md), and [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md)'s wife were killed, [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md) attempted to strangle %GLUPEK% — then 4 years old — to eliminate all witnesses. **[%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md) intervened and saved his life,** but permanent brain damage was done. Too young to remember the event or his family. The only member of [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md)'s family left alive — bears the physical evidence of the crime in his damaged mind. Everyone who looks at him is reminded of what they did.

## The Danger

[%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md) has spent 13 years managing him — keeping him calm, away from triggers, keeping the village from pushing him too far. The committee arriving threatens 13 years of careful management by strangers who don't understand what they're poking.

## Relationships

- **[%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md)** — the only mother he knows. She feeds him, shelters him, protects him. He doesn't understand why she cries sometimes. Doesn't know she saved his life.
- **[%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md)** — the [church](../places/new-village.md#church) is his refuge. [%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md) treats him with patience, gives him small tasks, lets him sit in the quiet. Doesn't understand faith, but understands safety.
- **[%BUTCHER%](butcher.md)** — instinctive fear. No conscious memory — just a body that knows. When [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md)'s dogs bark, he hides. When [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md) passes on the road, he goes still. The village notices. Nobody explains it.

## Well Influence

The well draws %GLUPEK% **physically and mentally.** He was supposed to die there — unfinished business, the survivor [the well](../story-facts/the-well.md) never received. As it strengthens, it calls him home.

- Wanders toward [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../places/old-village.md) more often. Escapes [%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md)'s watch. Found standing at the forest's edge, staring toward the ruins. Can't explain why.
- Agitation grows: rocking, hitting himself, crying without cause. The village reads it as his condition worsening — it's not. Something is waking up.
- **The well gives him back the truth** — not in words (he doesn't have the language), but in fragments, feelings. Starts flinching from [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md) and [%WUJAS%](secondary-characters.md) too — not just [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md). Pulls away from [%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md) without understanding why (she was there that night — his body is learning to distrust her). Stares at his own hands. Stares at the well.
- At the well, he stands still for a long time. Agitation stops. Something in him recognises the place — not the memory, but the gravity. He was supposed to be down there. The well shows him where his family went.

**The well doesn't give %GLUPEK% dreams — it gives him knowledge he can't process.** The truth arriving in a mind without the architecture to hold it. A 17-year-old brain-damaged boy discovering that the woman who raised him was there the night his family was murdered — one of the story's most dangerous variables.

## Flood Attitude

Doesn't understand the committee, the census, or the flood. Strangers make him nervous — he'll hide behind [%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md) or retreat to the [church](../places/new-village.md#church). **But he needs to be in the census to survive.** Documenting him means answering questions: whose son, why in [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md)'s house, where's his father? Every line on the form leads back to the lynch. The boy who can't understand the flood is the one whose survival depends on paperwork that could expose everything.

## For Players

%GLUPEK% is evidence and vulnerability in one body. Approaching him requires patience and [%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md)'s permission. The [church](../places/new-village.md#church) and [%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md) are the safest intermediaries. His instinctive reactions to the lynch perpetrators are the most visceral clue the players can witness — a body that remembers what a mind cannot.

## Open Questions

- Does %GLUPEK% have fragmentary memories or instinctive reactions to attackers beyond [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md)?
- How do the villagers treat him — pity? Guilt? Avoidance?
