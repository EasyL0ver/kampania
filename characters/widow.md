# Wanda Mazur

**Type:** Named character — grieving widow

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** ~1912
- **Age in 1967:** ~55
- **Heritage:** Polish settler
- **Lives in:** Small house near the church — alone
- **Settled:** After 1954 — arrived with husband Tadeusz Mazur to work the PGR farm
- **Husband:** Tadeusz Mazur (dead, ~1965). Grain silo accident — sent in to clear a blockage, suffocated under the grain. Michał Pytlak's ignorance of the danger, not malice.

## Character

Quiet, devout, harmless. Grieving widow living on phantom wages from a dead man's PGR ledger entry. She doesn't know her husband's death was covered up.

## Appearance

Small and thinning — the kind of woman who seems to take up less space each year. Faded floral headscarf tied tight under the chin, always the same brown wool coat regardless of season. Hands raw and red from washing, nails bitten short. Face deeply lined, pale eyes watery and slightly unfocused. Moves slowly, deliberately, as if afraid of disturbing someone. Speaks barely above a whisper — short sentences, often trailing off. When she does talk, her voice has a slight tremor. Smells faintly of tallow candles and damp wool.

## Opinions

- **[ks. Władysław Pająk](priest.md)** — Her lifeline. The only person who speaks to her with warmth most days. She attends every service partly for God, mostly for the brief moment after when he asks how she's managing.
- **[Michał Pytlak](foreman.md)** — Her husband's former boss. She bears him no grudge — accidents happen on farms. She's grateful the pension still comes. Doesn't know his negligence killed Tadeusz, doesn't know the death was covered up.
