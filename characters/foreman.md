# Michał Pytlak

**Type:** Named character — farm overseer
**Real name placeholder:** Michał Pytlak

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** ~1910
- **Age in 1967:** ~57
- **Family:** Married to Zofia Pytlak (cook). Father of Staszek Pytlak (age 5).
- **Arrived:** After 1954 — knows nothing about the lynch.

## Role

Runs Zbigniew Gajda's PGR farm day-to-day. Manages the workers, organizes labor, reports problems upward. Competent, pragmatic, loyal to Zbigniew Gajda through years of employment.

## Heritage

Not from here originally. A settler like the others, but arrived later — after the village was already built, after the secrets were already buried. He has no Lemko connection, no guilt, no stake in the cover-up. His world is the farm, the land, and the work.

## The Secret

**Covers up a death on the PGR.** About two years ago, Tadeusz Mazur suffocated in a grain silo — sent in to clear a blockage, the grain swallowed him in seconds. Michał Pytlak went in after him, nearly died himself. Nobody knew grain could do that. Zbigniew Gajda ordered the death never reported (no inspections). Tadeusz stays on the ledger, his wages go to Wanda Mazur as a "widow's pension." She doesn't know. The village doesn't know the death was never filed. Only two men carry this: Zbigniew Gajda and Michał Pytlak. See `story-facts\foreman-embezzlement.md` for full mechanics.

## In 1967

A man who built something with his hands. The farm works because Michał Pytlak makes it work. He knows every field, every fence, every drainage ditch. He's spent years turning rough land into something productive. This is *his* — not on paper, but in sweat.

**The wolves are bleeding him.** Attacks started weeks ago — a sheep here, a sheep there. He reinforced the pens. [Ryszard Dudka](neighbour.md) has been hunting them, failing. The losses mount. When [Zbigniew](wojewoda.md) authorizes [Stanisław Rezeń](butcher.md) to deal with it, Pytlak is grateful — he doesn't know village politics, doesn't know about the lynch. He sees a competent man with dogs who can solve his problem. See [The Wolf Hunt](../scenes/events/event-wolf-hunt.md).

**He manages the anti-flood measures.** Sandbags, drainage ditches, barriers, water diversion — Michał Pytlak is the one organizing it all. When the flood comes, he's the man with the shovel, not the clipboard. He directs the workers, assigns positions, decides where the sandbags go. This is what he does — he manages land, and right now the land is under attack.

## The Flood & The Committee

**Michał Pytlak wants to stay and fight.** While Zbigniew Gajda plans evacuation and Marek Gajda counts reimbursement money, Michał Pytlak says no. We sandbag. We dig channels. We save the farm. We don't run.

This isn't ideology or stubbornness for its own sake. It's a man who built something real looking at a committee with clipboards telling him it's going to be destroyed. He knows the land. He knows where the water will go. He thinks — genuinely believes — that if the village works together, they can survive the flood.

**He's the counterpoint to everyone else's resignation.** Where Stefania Kopacz accepts death and Marek Gajda plans escape and Zbigniew Gajda plans orderly retreat — Michał Pytlak is the only voice saying *we can beat this*. He's wrong, probably. The dam is coming regardless. But he's the only one fighting.

**This puts him at odds with the committee.** The players are here to assess, document, prepare for evacuation. Michał Pytlak sees them as wasting time — every hour on census paperwork is an hour not spent digging drainage. Every conversation about relocation is a conversation that isn't about survival.

**He'll organize the workers himself.** Józef Nowak, Piotr Wiśniewski, whoever will listen — sandbags, ditches, barriers. He'll do it with or without permission. The farm overseer who's been quietly stealing grain turns out to be the only man in the village who cares enough to fight for it.

## Relationships

- **Zbigniew Gajda** — Loyalty and respect, but growing tension. Michał Pytlak thinks Zbigniew Gajda is giving up too easily. The sołtys who always has a plan suddenly wants to surrender to the water.
- **Barbara Kopacz** — She works on his farm. Professional, distant, correct.
- **Zofia Pytlak** — His wife, his ally. She shares his stubbornness about staying.

## The Well's Influence

The well gives Michał Pytlak something nobody else gets: **inhuman fortitude.** He doesn't sleep. He doesn't stop. He works the sandbags until his hands bleed and then works more. He carries loads men half his age can't lift. He stands in knee-deep floodwater for hours directing the effort and doesn't get sick.

He's 57 years old and he's outworking everyone in the village. It's not adrenaline. It's not stubbornness. It's something else — a strength that has no business being in a man his age, that comes from nowhere and doesn't run out.

The well has no guilt to amplify in him, no wound to reopen. So it gives him the one thing that fits: the power to fight the water. But the gift has a cost — it keeps him away from [Zofia Pytlak](zofia.md), the one person who sees what's happening to him. She begs him to stop. He can't hear her. The well won't let him.

**If players intervene** — help Zofia pull him back, break the well's grip — he comes home. He's human again. Zofia lives. But the flood defense collapses without him. The village floods. The "Foreman saves village" ending is lost.

**If nobody intervenes** — the well keeps him standing until the work is done. Then it lets go. The inhuman strength drains away. What's left is a broken 57-year-old man with a bad knee, standing in his kitchen, looking at his dead wife. He saved the village. He'll sit in that kitchen for the rest of the game and nobody will reach him.

The other villagers notice. They don't say anything. But they watch the old man haul sandbags in the dark and something in the back of their minds says *that's not natural*.
