# %FOREMAN%

**Type:** Named character — farm overseer
**Real name placeholder:** %STEFAN%

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** ~1910
- **Age in 1967:** ~57
- **Family:** Married to %ZOFIA% (cook). Father of %STASZEK% (age 5).
- **Arrived:** After 1954 — knows nothing about the lynch.

## Role

Runs %WOJEWODA%'s PGR farm day-to-day. Manages the workers, organizes labor, reports problems upward. Competent, pragmatic, loyal to %WOJEWODA% through years of employment.

## Heritage

Not from here originally. A settler like the others, but arrived later — after the village was already built, after the secrets were already buried. He has no Lemko connection, no guilt, no stake in the cover-up. His world is the farm, the land, and the work.

## The Secret

**Embezzles from the PGR** — phantom workers, skimmed supplies, padded expenses. %ZOFIA% helps inflate kitchen orders. %WOJEWODA% doesn't know. Small-scale, steady, careful. Not a fortune — just enough to live a little better than he should. See `story-facts\foreman-embezzlement.md` for full red herring mechanics.

## In 1967

A man who built something with his hands. The farm works because %FOREMAN% makes it work. He knows every field, every fence, every drainage ditch. He's spent years turning rough land into something productive. This is *his* — not on paper, but in sweat.

**He manages the anti-flood measures.** Sandbags, drainage ditches, barriers, water diversion — %FOREMAN% is the one organizing it all. When the flood comes, he's the man with the shovel, not the clipboard. He directs the workers, assigns positions, decides where the sandbags go. This is what he does — he manages land, and right now the land is under attack.

## The Flood & The Committee

**%FOREMAN% wants to stay and fight.** While %WOJEWODA% plans evacuation and %JUNIOR% counts reimbursement money, %FOREMAN% says no. We sandbag. We dig channels. We save the farm. We don't run.

This isn't ideology or stubbornness for its own sake. It's a man who built something real looking at a committee with clipboards telling him it's going to be destroyed. He knows the land. He knows where the water will go. He thinks — genuinely believes — that if the village works together, they can survive the flood.

**He's the counterpoint to everyone else's resignation.** Where %BABCIA% accepts death and %JUNIOR% plans escape and %WOJEWODA% plans orderly retreat — %FOREMAN% is the only voice saying *we can beat this*. He's wrong, probably. The dam is coming regardless. But he's the only one fighting.

**This puts him at odds with the committee.** The players are here to assess, document, prepare for evacuation. %FOREMAN% sees them as wasting time — every hour on census paperwork is an hour not spent digging drainage. Every conversation about relocation is a conversation that isn't about survival.

**He'll organize the workers himself.** %JOZEK%, %PIOTR%, whoever will listen — sandbags, ditches, barriers. He'll do it with or without permission. The farm overseer who's been quietly stealing grain turns out to be the only man in the village who cares enough to fight for it.

## Relationships

- **%BARBARA%** — She works on his farm. Professional, distant, correct.
- **%ZOFIA%** — His wife, his partner in the embezzlement, his ally. She shares his stubbornness about staying.

## The Well's Influence

The well gives %FOREMAN% something nobody else gets: **inhuman fortitude.** He doesn't sleep. He doesn't stop. He works the sandbags until his hands bleed and then works more. He carries loads men half his age can't lift. He stands in knee-deep floodwater for hours directing the effort and doesn't get sick.

He's 57 years old and he's outworking everyone in the village. It's not adrenaline. It's not stubbornness. It's something else — a strength that has no business being in a man his age, that comes from nowhere and doesn't run out.

The well has no guilt to amplify in him, no wound to reopen. So it gives him the one thing that fits: the power to fight the water. Whether this is the well rewarding the only man trying to hold the land together, or the well keeping him upright so the drama plays out longer — nobody knows. %FOREMAN% doesn't question it. He just keeps working.

The other villagers notice. They don't say anything. But they watch the old man haul sandbags in the dark and something in the back of their minds says *that's not natural*.
