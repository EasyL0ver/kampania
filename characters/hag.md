# Paraskewia Chyłak

**Type:** Named character — Lemko survivor, forest hermit

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** ~1927
- **Age in 1967:** ~40
- **Heritage:** Lemko
- **Lives in:** [Hag's cabin](../locations/hags-cabin.md) — alone
- **Settled:** Never left — hid in the forest since the 1947 massacre

## Character

Survived the 1947 massacre. Her forbidden love triggered the soldiers' arrival. Has been performing Lemko death rites at [the well](../story-facts/the-well.md) for 20 years — the well's keeper and containment.

## Appearance

Looks sixty, is forty. The forest has carved her down to sinew and bone — thin as a birch sapling, all sharp angles. Skin weathered to the colour of old bark, deeply lined around the eyes and mouth. Hair iron-grey, long, usually hidden under a dark headscarf tied peasant-style. Hands like roots — knotted, cracked, stained with herb-juice and soil under the nails. Wears layers of patched wool and linen that blend with the forest floor — browns, greys, faded greens. Moves silently, instinctively — twenty years of hiding have made her footfall lighter than a deer's. Eyes dark and watchful, never fully at rest. When she speaks — rarely — her voice is a dry rasp, unused for days at a time. Slips between Polish and Lemko Rusyn mid-sentence without noticing.

## Opinions

- **[Stanisław Rezeń](butcher.md)** — Knows exactly who he is. The local boy from the next valley who helped the soldiers in 1947. Has watched his footprints at the well for twenty years. Hates him with the slow, patient hatred of a woman who has nothing left but vigil. He is the well's corruption — she is its ward. One day the balance will break.
