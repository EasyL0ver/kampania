# Paraskewia Chyłak

**Type:** Named character — Lemko survivor, forest hermit

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** ~1927
- **Age in 1967:** ~40
- **Heritage:** Lemko
- **Lives in:** [Hag's cabin](../locations/hags-cabin.md) — alone
- **Settled:** Never left — hid in the forest since the 1947 massacre

## Character

Survived the 1947 massacre. Her forbidden love triggered the soldiers' arrival. Has been performing Lemko death rites at [the well](../story-facts/the-well.md) for 20 years — the well's keeper and containment.

## Appearance

- **Clothes:** Layers of patched wool and linen in forest colours — browns, greys, faded greens; blends with the undergrowth
- **Hair & face:** Iron-grey hair, long, hidden under a dark headscarf tied peasant-style; deeply lined face, dark watchful eyes
- **Carriage:** Moves silently — twenty years of hiding made her footfall lighter than a deer's; appears suddenly, as if the forest spat her out

Speaks rarely, a dry rasp unused for days at a time. Slips between Polish and Lemko Rusyn without noticing.

## Opinions

- **[Stanisław Rezeń](butcher.md)** — I know exactly who he is. The local boy from the next valley who helped the soldiers in 1947. I've watched his footprints at the well for twenty years. I hate him with the slow, patient hatred of a woman who has nothing left but vigil. He is the well's corruption — I am its ward. One day the balance will break.
