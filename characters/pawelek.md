## The Well's Influence

Pawełek Kopacz is 4. He doesn't have guilt, trauma, or buried secrets. The well has nothing to amplify in him — but it still calls. Children are curious by nature, and the well is *interesting*. Shiny. The water makes sounds.

As the well grows stronger, Pawełek Kopacz wanders toward %OLD_VILLAGE%. Nobody notices — he's a small boy in a chaotic flood, and Barbara Kopacz can't watch him every second. He finds the well. He drinks the water.

**He falls ill.** High fever, convulsions, delirium. It's 1967, the region is flooded, and floodwater contaminates everything — wells, streams, puddles. A child drinking bad water during a flood is the most ordinary tragedy imaginable. Nothing supernatural required.

But in his fever-dreams he mutters about things a 4-year-old shouldn't know — "the water under the ground," "the old woman," whispers he heard near the well. The medical explanation is airtight. The fever-babbling is what keeps the players (and the village) unsettled.

**For Marek Gajda, this can be the moment.** The boy he's been avoiding, the responsibility he's been dodging — now Pawełek Kopacz is burning with fever and nobody is stepping up. If Marek Gajda chooses to act, this is where the avoidance ends. Not because he's ready. Because the boy is sick and someone has to be there.
