# kpt. Henryk Ćwiek

**Type:** Named character — deceased KBW officer
**Freudian role:** [Superego](../story-facts/freudian-triangle.md) of the 1947 massacre — institutional authority, destroyed by the violence it unleashed

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** ~1915
- **Died:** 1947 (age ~32) — killed during the [%OLD_VILLAGE% massacre](../story-facts/old-village-massacre.md)
- **Rank:** Kapitan, Korpus Bezpieczeństwa Wewnętrznego (KBW)

## Character

Career military. A man shaped by orders — gave them, followed them, believed in the chain. Not sadistic, not merciful. Professional. The kind of officer who clears a village the way he'd clear a road — with efficiency and without philosophical doubt. The state says these people go, they go. That's the job.

Not evil in any interesting way. The banality of authority. The Superego doesn't need to be cruel — it just needs to say *this is what must be done* and mean it completely.

## The 1947 Massacre

Commanded the KBW unit assigned to clear Lemko villages in the Bieszczady sector during Akcja Wisła. [Edward Barnaś](soldier.md) was a soldier in his unit. [Stanisław Rezeń](butcher.md) was recruited as a civilian auxiliary — a local who knew the terrain, the paths, which houses were where.

Arrived at [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../places/old-village.md) expecting a routine deportation. The villagers refused to leave. Situation escalated. Zieliński gave the order to use force — the Superego authorizing the Id.

**Killed during the resistance.** Dmytro Kosach — [Paraskewia Chyłak](hag.md)'s UPA lover, a trained guerrilla fighter hiding in the village — shot Ćwiek dead when the soldiers arrived. His death didn't create chaos — it created **fury**. The soldiers, watching their captain fall, turned on the village with the blind rage of men who've just seen their commander killed.

What followed was retribution, not disorder. [Edward Barnaś](soldier.md) was carried along by the fury — the Ego swept up in collective rage. [Stanisław Rezeń](butcher.md) found his purpose — the Id freed from restraint, discovering that the violence felt *right*. Everyone's id broke free that day. The difference is Rezeń never put his back.

The massacre was total because a dead captain demanded blood.

## The Cover-Up

Ćwiek's death made the cover-up non-negotiable — but not for the reasons one might expect. The cover-up protects the soldiers from **their own chain of command**, not from justice. What happened was a military failure:

- They didn't detect the UPA presence before entering the village
- They lost their commanding officer during a routine deportation
- After Ćwiek died, nobody gave orders — the massacre was **uncontrolled**, not authorized
- They killed the entire village — women, children, elderly — not as counter-insurgency but as soldiers avenging their captain

The state wanted villages **cleared**, not massacred. Orderly deportation, train cars, paperwork. Every man in that unit faced courts-martial for incompetence, not war crimes. So:

- File the village as "evacuated successfully"
- Report Ćwiek as KIA in a separate UPA engagement, or simply MIA
- The village is supposed to be empty anyway — nobody comes looking for deported Lemkos
- His body disposed of alongside the villagers — another body in the ground

**The double irony:** years later, [por. Witold Skowron](officer.md) and the state learn about the massacre and bury it too — for completely different reasons (political embarrassment, state crime). Two cover-ups stacked, protecting different people from different consequences, overlapping perfectly.

## Thematic Weight

The first Superego to fall. His death at [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../places/old-village.md) creates a **structural vacuum** that echoes for twenty years:

- **1947–1954:** No Superego contains the Id. Rezeń spends seven years with the drive and nothing holding it. Edward's Ego manages alone — builds a family, represses everything. Fragile stability.
- **1954:** [Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md) steps into the Superego role — personal, local authority replacing institutional command. Tells Rezeń where to live, what not to drink, who to avoid.
- **1967:** Zbigniew's containment erodes under the well's influence. The second Superego begins to fail — the same structural position, the same outcome, twenty years later.

**The Superego is always the first to fall.** Ćwiek dies. Then Zbigniew cracks. The pattern repeats because the Id is patient and the Ego is fragile.

## For Players

Not directly discoverable — no one in 1967 knows his name or talks about him. But traces exist:

- Military records (if players dig into Akcja Wisła operations in the region)
- [por. Witold Skowron](officer.md) may have access to classified files mentioning a "lost" officer
- The fact that a KBW unit operated here *and lost its commander* is a buried state secret alongside the massacre itself
- If found, his story reframes the massacre: not a cold, ordered execution but a situation that collapsed when authority died
