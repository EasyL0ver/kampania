## The Flood & The Committee

**Initially compliant.** %BARBARA% does what she's told. Government people want names and property? Fine. She answers questions, keeps her head down, doesn't make trouble. One more set of officials changes nothing.

**She refuses to state %PAWEŁEK%'s father.** The census asks. She won't answer. Not for the government, not for the players, not for anyone. This is the one line she's held for years and no amount of bureaucratic pressure will move it.

**When she learns the flood is coming — she panics, then gets angry.** The government is going to drown them out. Her house — the only thing she has, built new by %WOJEWODA% — is going underwater. She has a 4-year-old son, no money, nowhere to go, and these people with clipboards are here to write it all down before the dam swallows it.

She's not angry about secrets or history. She's angry about survival. A single mother in 1960s Poland just learned that the state is taking the one piece of ground she has. That rage is simple, real, and has nothing to do with the buried horrors the players are chasing.

## Faith

%BARBARA% is a **devout Catholic** — the church is her anchor. In a life defined by poverty, dependency, and silence, her faith is the one thing that's hers. %PRIEST%'s word carries absolute weight with her. She won't defy him on matters of faith — not for the committee, not for the players, not for anyone.

**This matters for the ritual.** If the players need %BABCIA% brought to the well for the Lemko rite, %BARBARA% is the only one who can take her. But she won't do it without %PRIEST%'s explicit blessing. A pagan ceremony at a haunted well is heresy in her eyes. See `story-facts\the-ritual.md`.

## The Well's Influence

The well amplifies %BARBARA%'s **protective instinct.** She has no guilt, no direct connection to the crimes. But she has %PAWEŁEK% and %BABCIA% — and as the village deteriorates, every instinct tells her to retreat.

- She pulls away from %WUJAS%. His behaviour is getting worse and she doesn't want it near her son. The drinking, the outbursts, the fragments he mutters — she doesn't understand them, but she knows danger.
- She pulls away from %JUNIOR%. The sołtys's family feels unsafe in a way she can't articulate. The warmth she felt is replaced by wariness.
- She pulls away from the farm. Finds reasons not to go. Keeps %PAWEŁEK% and %BABCIA% inside. Her small house becomes a shell she's closing around her family.
- She notices %BABCIA% changing — the singing, the restlessness, the sharpened memory — and it frightens her. Her grandmother is becoming someone she doesn't recognise, speaking a language that sounds like ghosts.

For the players, this is a problem: %BARBARA% controls access to %BABCIA%, and %BARBARA% is withdrawing. The more the village scares her, the harder she is to reach — and the more the players need what's inside her house.
