# Barbara Kopacz

**Type:** Named character — villager

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1939
- **Age in 1967:** 28
- **Heritage:** Half-Lemko (mother is [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md)) — raised Polish, devout Catholic
- **Lives in:** [Barbara Kopacz's house](../places/new-village.md#barbaras-house) — with [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) (her mother), [Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md)

## Character

Poor, dependent, devout Catholic. Single mother to [Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md). Works on [Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md)'s farm. Keeps her head down — silence is her survival strategy. The one line she holds absolutely: she will never name [Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md)'s father.

## Background

Arrived in [%NEW_VILLAGE%](../places/new-village.md) **after the lynch.** Knows nothing about the massacre, [Edward Barnaś](soldier.md)'s family, or [the well](../story-facts/the-well.md). [Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md) built her a small house out of guilt — he suspects [Marek Gajda](junior.md) is the father. He's wrong — it's [Tadek Gajda](secondary-characters.md) — but Barbara Kopacz isn't correcting anyone. She doesn't know herself which of the two is [Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md)'s father.

## Relationships

- **[Tadek Gajda](secondary-characters.md)** — sexual relationship. Actual father of [Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md) (unknown to everyone, including Barbara Kopacz).
- **[Marek Gajda](junior.md)** — sexual relationship. [Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md) wrongly believes he is the father.
- **[Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md)** — her son. Paternity unknown even to her.
- **[Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md)** — her mother. Lemko. Barbara was raised Polish, doesn't speak the language, doesn't practice the customs. But the blood is there.
- **[Ryszard Dudka](neighbour.md)** — closest friend, literal neighbour. Helps with the house, brings firewood, watches the boy. She trusts him completely. She and [Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md) are the only uncomplicated good in his life.
- **[ks. Władysław Pająk](secondary-characters.md)** — his word carries absolute weight. She won't defy him on matters of faith.

## Faith

Devout Catholic — the church is her anchor. [ks. Władysław Pająk](secondary-characters.md)'s word is law for her. **This matters for [the ritual](../story-facts/the-ritual.md):** if players need [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) brought to [the well](../story-facts/the-well.md) for the Lemko rite, Barbara Kopacz is the only one who can take her — but she won't without [ks. Władysław Pająk](secondary-characters.md)'s explicit blessing. A pagan ceremony at a haunted well is heresy in her eyes.

## The Danger — Players' Confidant

Barbara Kopacz seems safe — arrived after the lynch, knows nothing, sympathetic single mother. Players will trust her instinctively. **She is a sieve.** She shares her day casually with [Ryszard Dudka](neighbour.md) over the fence — *"Those government people were asking about the old village today…"* Everything the players tell her reaches the one man sitting on 13 years of guilt about exactly what they're uncovering. She breaks her friend by being honest; he breaks the village because she was.

**Players cause [the lynch](../story/ending-punishment-lynch.md) by trusting the wrong person.** Her darkness isn't cruelty — she doesn't know what information is dangerous.

## Flood Attitude

Initially compliant — answers questions, keeps head down. **Refuses to state [Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md)'s father** on the census — no amount of bureaucratic pressure will move this. When she learns the flood is coming, she panics then gets angry. Her house — the only thing she has — is going underwater. A single mother with a 4-year-old, no money, nowhere to go. That rage is simple and real, nothing to do with the buried horrors the players are chasing.

## Well Influence

The well amplifies her **protective instinct.** No guilt, no connection to the crimes — but as the village deteriorates, every instinct tells her to retreat.

- Pulls away from [Tadek Gajda](secondary-characters.md) — his drinking, outbursts, and muttered fragments feel dangerous near her son.
- Pulls away from [Marek Gajda](junior.md) — the sołtys's family feels unsafe in ways she can't articulate.
- Pulls away from the farm. Keeps [Pawełek Kopacz](secondary-characters.md) and [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) inside. Her house becomes a shell closing around her family.
- Notices [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) changing — singing, restlessness, sharpened memory — and it frightens her. Her grandmother is becoming someone she doesn't recognise, speaking a language that sounds like ghosts.

## For Players

Barbara Kopacz controls access to [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md), and she is withdrawing. The more the village scares her, the harder she is to reach — and the more the players need what's inside her house. Gaining her trust requires patience; scaring her locks [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) away.
