# Barbara Kopacz

**Type:** Named character — villager

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1939
- **Age in 1967:** 28
- **Heritage:** Half-Lemko (mother is [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md)) — raised Polish, devout Catholic
- **Lives in:** [Barbara Kopacz's house](../scenes/locations/barbaras-house.md) — with [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) (her mother), [Pawełek Kopacz](pawelek.md)

## Character

Poor, single mother to [Pawełek Kopacz](pawelek.md). Works on [Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md)'s farm. Warm, open, instinctively generous — the kind of person who puts the kettle on before asking why you've come. Genuinely curious about people. She asks questions — not pointed, not probing, just interested. *"What are you really here for?" "What did pan Gajda say?" "Did you go to the old village? What's it like?"* She remembers what you said last time and follows up. Players will answer because it feels like conversation, not interrogation. Everything she hears goes over the fence to Ryszard by evening. The one line she holds absolutely: she will never name [Pawełek Kopacz](pawelek.md)'s father.

## Background

Arrived in %NEW_VILLAGE% **after the lynch.** Knows nothing about the massacre, [Edward Barnaś](soldier.md)'s family, or [the well](../story-facts/the-well.md). [Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md) built her a small house out of guilt — he suspects [Marek Gajda](junior.md) is the father. Barbara suspects the same — she slept with both Marek and [Tadek Gajda](wujas.md) and believes Marek is more likely. She's wrong — the real father is Tadek.

## Relationships

- **[Tadek Gajda](wujas.md)** — one-time encounter. The real father of [Pawełek Kopacz](pawelek.md), but neither of them knows.
- **[Marek Gajda](junior.md)** — ongoing relationship. Barbara believes he is the father. So does [Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md). Both are wrong.
- **[Pawełek Kopacz](pawelek.md)** — her son. She thinks she knows who the father is. She doesn't.
- **[Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md)** — her mother. Lemko. Barbara was raised Polish, doesn't speak the language, doesn't practice the customs. But the blood is there.
- **[Ryszard Dudka](neighbour.md)** — closest friend, literal neighbour. Helps with the house, brings firewood, watches the boy. She trusts him completely. She and [Pawełek Kopacz](pawelek.md) are the only uncomplicated good in his life.
- **[ks. Władysław Pająk](priest.md)** — the village priest. She goes to church because everyone does. He's respected, but she's not devout — faith is background, not identity.

## The Danger — Players' Confidant

Barbara Kopacz doesn't *seem* safe — she *is* safe. That's the problem. She's genuinely kind, genuinely curious, genuinely interested in the committee members as human beings. Players will confide in her because she earns it — she listens, she cares, she remembers. **She is a sieve.** She shares her day casually with [Ryszard Dudka](neighbour.md) over the fence — *"Those government people were asking about the old village today…"* Everything the players tell her reaches the one man sitting on 13 years of guilt about exactly what they're uncovering. She breaks her friend by being honest; he breaks the village because she was.

**Players cause [the lynch](../story/ending-punishment-lynch.md) by trusting the wrong person.** Her darkness isn't cruelty — she doesn't know what information is dangerous.

## Flood Attitude

She answers census questions warmly, volunteers extra information, makes the committee feel welcome. **Refuses to state [Pawełek Kopacz](pawelek.md)'s father** on the census — no amount of pressure moves this, but she stays sweet about it: *"I'm sorry, I just can't."* When she learns the flood is coming, she's scared — her house, the only thing she has, is going underwater. A single mother with a 4-year-old, no money, nowhere to go. But even scared, she's still Barbara. She makes tea. She asks if *you're* alright. She worries about everyone else while her own world is ending.

## Well Influence

The well has almost nothing to work with in Barbara. No guilt, no connection to the crimes, no buried violence. She's one of the few people in the village the well can't reach. What the well *does* is darken the world around her — [Tadek Gajda](wujas.md) gets worse, the village gets tenser, [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) starts changing. Barbara notices all of it because she notices everything. She worries. She talks about it — to the players, to Ryszard over the fence. Her openness doesn't close. It becomes more dangerous as the things she's observing get darker.

- Notices [Tadek Gajda](wujas.md) drinking more, muttering, avoiding her
- Notices [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) changing — singing, restlessness, sharpened memory. It unsettles her but she adapts. She's been adapting her whole life
- Worries about [Pawełek Kopacz](pawelek.md) playing too far from the house
- Stays warm. Stays open. The village is falling apart around her and she's still making tea

## For Players

Barbara Kopacz is the easiest door in the village to open — and the most dangerous to walk through. Her warmth is real, constant, and unconditional. She never withdraws, never closes up, never becomes hostile. She controls access to [Stefania Kopacz](babcia.md) but shares that access generously. The danger isn't getting in — it's that everything players say to her reaches [Ryszard Dudka](neighbour.md) by evening. The warmer she is, the more they tell her. The more they tell her, the worse it gets.

## Actions

### Census interview
- **Requires:** Committee authority (first visit)
- **Cost:** 1 action (part of a house visit)
- **Outcome:** Barbara answers everything cheerfully — name, age, household members, employment. She volunteers details you didn't ask for, makes small talk, apologises for the mess. She's the easiest census interview in the village. **One wall: she will not name Pawełek's father.** The warmth doesn't disappear — but something behind her eyes closes. She goes quiet, her jaw sets, and she waits for you to move on.
- **Gives:** [barbara-refuses-father](../clues/clues.md#barbara-refuses-father)
- **Opportunities:**
  - **Read:** Her refusal isn't stubbornness — it's fear. Protection. Something specific and alive is behind that silence.
  - **Law:** The omission is formally notable — a census form with no father listed is a bureaucratic flag. You know this means something in the paperwork chain.
  - **Observation:** Three household members listed, one income (farm labour). The economics don't add up — someone else is helping. **Gives:** [barbara-has-help](../clues/clues.md#barbara-has-help)

### Push about the father
- **Requires:** **Intimidate** or **Law**, OR [barbara-has-help](../clues/clues.md#barbara-has-help) + **Sweettalk** (*"Someone built you this house. Someone is looking after you. Is it the father?"*)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Outcome:** She cracks. Names **Marek Gajda** — she believes it's true. Begs the players not to write it down. *"Please. He doesn't even know. His father would — please."* If they record it, Wojewoda sees the census form. She's warm again next visit regardless.
- **Gives:** [marek-is-paweleks-father](../clues/clues.md#marek-is-paweleks-father).

### Ask about the village
- **Requires:** Any visit (she volunteers this stuff freely)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Outcome:** Barbara is a social map of the village — but this isn't a monologue. It's a **conversation.** She gives a little, then asks a question back. She's genuinely curious about the committee, about what they're doing, about what they've found. The information exchange feels natural, warm, mutual. Players won't notice they're giving as much as they're getting.
  - *"Pan Gajda is very helpful — he'll sort anything out for you. He's in the office most days. He has a phone, you know, if you need it."* Then: *"What do you need the phone for? Are you calling Warsaw?"*
  - *"His brother Tadek is fun, always joking. He's at the store a lot, or visiting friends."* Then: *"Have you met him? What did you think?"*
  - *"Pan Rezeń keeps to himself, he's out past the edge. He has dogs — beautiful dogs."* Then: *"You haven't been out there, have you? Why would you go there?"*
  - *"Ryszard next door is wonderful. He brings firewood, watches Pawełek. I don't know what I'd do without him."*
  - *"Pani Zofia at the farm kitchen — oh, she's lovely. She feeds everyone. You should go see her."*
  - *"Pan Pytlak works so hard. He's been very busy lately."* Then: *"Is the farm in trouble? Is that why you're here?"*
  - *"Pani Rzepka at the store is very proper, very organised. Her husband is an artist — he paints! Isn't that wonderful?"*
  - *"Father Pająk is so kind. Everyone goes to him. The church is beautiful — the Gajda family keeps it lovely."*
  - *"The old village? Oh, there's nothing there. Just old stones. We don't go — there's no reason to."* Then: *"Why do you ask? Did someone mention it?"*
  - *"Edek is a sweet boy. Big! Janina takes such good care of him. He's always been that way."*
  - Every answer she gives ends with a question. Not suspicious — interested. She collects information the way she collects kindness: naturally, without effort, without agenda.
- **Read:** Everything she says is kind. Notice what she *doesn't* say. She doesn't mention the butcher's name until asked. She skips over Matrona's personality. She says Tadek is "fun" like someone who's learned to frame a problem as a feature.
- **Streetwise:** She's handing you the village's entire nervous system wrapped in politeness. Every kind word is a data point. "Very busy" = cracking. "Keeps to himself" = dangerous. "Always been that way" = nobody questions it. She's the best intelligence source in the village and she has no idea. **Also notice:** she's fishing. Every question she asks you is information she'll share with Ryszard tonight.
- **Warning:** **Everything you tell Barbara reaches the Neighbour.** The sieve works both ways — she gives generously, and reports generously. Don't reveal this to the players. The GM should note what the players say to Barbara — it determines what Ryszard knows.
- **Gives:** [wojewoda-has-only-phone](../clues/clues.md#wojewoda-has-only-phone), [siblings-fund-the-church](../clues/clues.md#siblings-fund-the-church). With **Streetwise**: full social map of the village (no single clue — operational intelligence).
