# Stanisław Rezeń

**Type:** Named character — evil with self-awareness, symbolic Lemko rage
**Freudian role:** [Id](../story-facts/freudian-triangle.md) — raw drive, compulsion without conscience

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1921
- **Age in 1967:** 46
- **Heritage:** **Not Lemko.** Polish — from a neighbouring village in Bieszczady. Not a distant resettled stranger. A local who knew these mountains, these paths, these people. That's what makes 1947 worse.
- **Lives in:** [Stanisław Rezeń's house](../locations/butchers-house.md) — alone (with dogs), edge of village
- **Settled:** ~1948 — local from neighbouring village, drifted in early

## Character

Charismatic, no inhibitions. The most alive person in any room. Village outcast, contained by [Wojewoda](wojewoda.md) at the edge. Participated in the 1947 massacre and the 1954 lynch. [The well](../story-facts/the-well.md)'s longest vessel.

## Relationships

- **[Edek Barnaś](glupek.md)** — the worst thing about Rezeń and the boy is that Rezeń is *nice* to him. Arm around the shoulder. Pinches his cheek. Slips him food — a piece of sausage, a crust. Ruffles his hair like a favourite uncle. Talks to him like a man — crude, familiar, playful: *"Synku, a ty już miałeś kobietę?"* Sonny, you had a woman yet? To a brain-damaged 17-year-old who can't understand the question. Laughs at his own joke. The village squirms. Janina, if present, goes rigid. **For players:** the casual *"synku"* and the easy familiarity raise questions — why is this man so comfortable with Ciotka's son? Why does she hate it so much? It cracks the surface of the census story without saying anything explicit. Jovial, warm, familiar. The village watches this and their skin crawls, but what can they say? He's being kind to the boy. That's worse than cruelty — because the hand on Edek's shoulder is the hand that strangled him at four. And Edek's body knows. He goes rigid at the touch. Doesn't pull away — can't, the damaged brain reads the warmth and the terror simultaneously and freezes. Smiles back sometimes, an empty reflex, while every muscle locks. Rezeń doesn't notice or doesn't care. He enjoys the boy's company the way a man enjoys a dog that flinches. Nobody in the village will ever say what's wrong with this picture. They just look away faster.
- **[Janina Gajda](ciotka.md)** — she stopped him killing the infant. Never forgiven. But the silence isn't mutual — it's one-sided. She avoids him. He doesn't avoid her. He catcalls her in public — crude, loud, playful: *"Janka, kiedy mnie odwiedzisz?"* When are you visiting me? Or worse, with others watching: *"Taka ładna kobieta i sama, to grzech."* Such a pretty woman, all alone — it's a sin. Grins. The village looks away. She walks faster, doesn't respond, locks her door. **For players:** it reads as a man harassing a woman — creepy, aggressive, maybe there's history there. Sexual history. Maybe he's the father? It's another false trail that cracks Ciotka's clean census story. The truth is simpler and worse: he's punishing her. Thirteen years of punishment for saving the boy he was strangling.
- **[Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md) & [Tadek Gajda](secondary-characters.md)** — co-conspirators, decayed bond. [Zbigniew Gajda](wojewoda.md) keeps him at arm's length; [Tadek Gajda](secondary-characters.md) drinks to forget him. Useful as a threat, unwanted as a neighbour. He accepts it with the cold patience of a man who knows where the bodies are.
- **[Paraskewia Chyłak](hag.md)** — doesn't know she exists. He's noticed signs: debris re-piled on the well after he cleared it, candle wax in the cerkiew, faint incense on nights nobody should be there. Chalks it up to the ruins being strange. Someone is fighting him for the well, and he doesn't know it. **She** knows exactly who he is — the local boy from the next valley who helped the soldiers in 1947. She's been watching his footprints at the well for 20 years.

## Actions

### Confront Rezeń (after confrontation)
- **Requires:** Tracked the dogs from the [well](../locations/the-well.md) OR already suspect him
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **Skills:** **Intimidate**, **Read**
- **Outcome:** He doesn't deny it. Doesn't confirm it either. Doesn't care. The knife turns in his hand. He's calmer than he's been in days — the compulsion briefly satisfied, like feeding an addiction. *"The witch? She was at the well. She shouldn't have been at the well."* If pressed: *"I didn't go looking for her."* True. He went to the well. She was there. Everything else was the compulsion.
  - **Read:** He's not lying. He's not guilty. He doesn't experience what he did as wrong. The well wanted feeding. He fed it. The satisfaction is visible — and it's already fading. The craving is coming back.
