# %CIOTKA%

**Type:** Named character — sibling (sister)

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1912
- **Age in 1967:** 55
- **Heritage:** Lemko descent — hidden. See [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md) for siblings' shared background.
- **Lives in:** [%CIOTKA%'s house](../places/new-village.md#ciotkas-house) — with [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md)

## Character

Devout, fearful, deeply honest when her guard drops. Eldest sibling but weakest in the family hierarchy. The moral heart of the tragedy — the only person who showed mercy the night of the lynch. Lives in the dead man's house, raising his brain-damaged son, surrounded by the people who did it. Her faith may hold her together — or torment her most.

## Background

One of four siblings — sister to [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md), [%WUJAS%](secondary-characters.md), and [%MATRONA%](matrona.md). No direct hatred or personal grudge against [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md).

## The Lynch

**Saved [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md)'s life.** When [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md) was strangling the toddler, she intervened and stopped him. The child survived — but the strangling had already caused brain damage.

## In 1967

A devout, single woman living in **[%SOLDIER%](soldier.md)'s house** — the best plot of land in the village, now occupied by the woman who saved his son. Raises [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md) as her own.

## Relationships

- **[%GLUPEK%](glupek.md)** — her life's penance. Raised him from infancy — he's 17 now, massive, brain-damaged, trusting. She doesn't see [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md)'s son; she sees *her* boy. Love is real, fierce, tangled with guilt. Every day in his father's house is both punishment and purpose.
- **[%BUTCHER%](butcher.md)** — silent terror. She stopped him killing [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md); he's never forgiven it. They don't speak, don't look at each other. She locks her door at night and keeps [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md) close. Lived in fear of him for 13 years.
- **[%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md)** — her confessor, the only person outside the family she trusts. Over 13 years of confessions, she has told him **everything** — not all at once, not deliberately, but piece by piece the whole picture spilled out. The rape, the killings, who did what, where the bodies went. **%CIOTKA% is the reason [%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md) knows.** [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md)'s confessions are guarded, [%MATRONA%](matrona.md)'s are masterful misdirection — but %CIOTKA% is too honest, too broken, too desperate for absolution to edit herself. She doesn't realize how much she's given him. Their relationship reinforces each other's silence: she goes for comfort, he gives it, and neither acknowledges that what passes between them is a full account of a murder.
- **The siblings** — obedience through fear. Saving [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md) was a betrayal of the pact. [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md) treats her with cold authority, [%MATRONA%](matrona.md) keeps her in line with looks and silences, [%WUJAS%](secondary-characters.md) can barely face her. She stays quiet because quiet is the price of keeping her boy safe.

## Well Influence

The well amplifies her **fear and paralysis:**

- Stops leaving the house — the world outside feels threatening in ways she can't name
- Clings to [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md) with increasing desperation. Senses something pulling him toward [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../places/old-village.md), fights it with proximity
- Prayers become frantic, repetitive, compulsive — not devotion but terror. God feels further away
- Flinches from her siblings — the family feels like a trap closing
- Dreams about **the night she saved [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md)** — except she doesn't make it in time. Reaches for the baby and her hands pass through him. Wakes up and checks on the boy. Every night. The well is telling her: *you can't protect him forever.*

## Flood Attitude

The census terrifies her. Government people writing names, houses, land — paper trails that can't be answered cleanly:

- Whose house is this? ([%SOLDIER%](soldier.md)'s — how did she get it?)
- Who is [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md)'s father? (No safe answer.)
- Why does a single woman have the best plot? (The owner was murdered.)

She will lie — practiced, quiet, consistent lies from 13 years of practice. **Breaking through requires convincing her the truth won't destroy [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md).** He's all she cares about. If players can make her believe speaking won't get him taken away or hurt, she'll crack.

## For Players

%CIOTKA% holds the most honest account of the lynch available — but only yields it if she believes [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md) will be safe. Her [%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md) connection is key: he knows everything because of her. Approaching through faith or genuine care for [%GLUPEK%](glupek.md) is the way in.
