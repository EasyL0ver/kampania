# %CIOTKA%

**Type:** Named character — sibling (sister)

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1912
- **Age in 1967:** 55

## Role

One of four siblings — sister to %WOJEWODA%, %WUJAS%, and %MATRONA%. Part of a family unit in %NEW_VILLAGE%.

## Heritage

Lemko descent — hidden. See %WOJEWODA% for details on the siblings' shared background.

## Relationship to %SOLDIER%

No direct hatred or strong personal grudge.

## The Lynch

**%CIOTKA% saved %GLUPEK%'s life.** When %BUTCHER% was strangling the toddler, %CIOTKA% intervened and stopped him. The child survived — but the strangling had already caused brain damage.

## Relationships

### %GLUPEK% — Her life's penance

She raised him from infancy. He's 17 now — massive, brain-damaged, trusting. She doesn't see %SOLDIER%'s son. She sees *her* boy. The love is real, fierce, and tangled with guilt. Every day in his father's house, caring for the child her family destroyed, is both her punishment and her purpose.

### %BUTCHER% — Silent terror

She stopped him from killing %GLUPEK%. He's never forgiven it. They don't speak, don't look at each other, don't acknowledge the other exists. But %CIOTKA% locks her door at night and keeps %GLUPEK% close. She knows what %BUTCHER% is — and she's lived in fear of him for 13 years.

### %PRIEST% — Her confessor, her comfort

%PRIEST% is the only person outside the family she trusts. She confesses more honestly than her siblings — not in calculated fragments, but in raw, grief-soaked outpourings. Over 13 years of confessions, she has told him **everything**. Not all at once, not deliberately — but piece by piece, year by year, the whole picture spilled out. The rape, the killings, who did what, where the bodies went. She couldn't hold it alone.

**%CIOTKA% is the reason %PRIEST% knows.** %WOJEWODA%'s confessions are guarded. %MATRONA%'s are masterful misdirection. But %CIOTKA% is too honest, too broken, and too desperate for absolution to edit herself. %PRIEST%'s near-complete picture of the lynch comes almost entirely from her.

She doesn't realize how much she's given him. She doesn't think of confession as testimony — it's the one place she can breathe. And %PRIEST% has never told her how much he's assembled from her words. Their relationship reinforces each other's silence — she goes to him for comfort, he gives it, and neither acknowledges that what passes between them is a full account of a murder.

### The siblings — Obedience through fear

%CIOTKA% is the eldest sibling but the weakest in the family hierarchy. She saved %GLUPEK% — and the others have never let her forget that this act of mercy was a betrayal of the pact. %WOJEWODA% treats her with cold authority. %MATRONA% keeps her in line with looks and silences. %WUJAS% can barely face her. %CIOTKA% is terrified of all of them — not because they'd hurt her directly, but because defying them means losing the only family she has, the only structure that lets her keep %GLUPEK% safe. She stays quiet because quiet is the price of keeping her boy.

## In 1967

A **devout, single woman**. Lives in **%SOLDIER%'s house** — the best plot of land in the village, now occupied by the woman who saved his son. She has taken on the role of %GLUPEK%'s caretaker, raising him as her own.

%CIOTKA% is the moral heart of this tragedy — the only person who showed mercy that night. She lives in the dead man's house, raising his brain-damaged son, surrounded by the people who did it. Her faith may be what holds her together — or what torments her most.

## The Flood & The Committee

**%CIOTKA% is worried.** The census terrifies her. Government people writing down names, houses, land — paper trails that ask questions she can't answer cleanly.

- Whose house is this? (%SOLDIER%'s — how did she get it?)
- Who is %GLUPEK%'s father? (No answer that doesn't lead somewhere dark.)
- Why does a single woman live in the best plot in the village? (Because the previous owner was murdered.)

**She will lie.** Practiced, quiet lies — the same ones she's been telling for 13 years. A story about inheriting the house. A vague answer about %GLUPEK%'s father. She's not good at it, but she's consistent, and she's terrified enough to hold the line.

**Breaking through her lies requires convincing her the truth won't destroy %GLUPEK%.** That's the only thing she cares about. Not herself, not the family, not the village — just the boy. If the players can make her believe that speaking won't get %GLUPEK% taken away or hurt, she'll crack. Until then, she lies and shakes and lies some more.

## The Well's Influence

The well amplifies %CIOTKA%'s **fear and paralysis.** She was already the most submissive sibling, already terrified of defying the family. The well pushes her toward total shutdown.

- She stops leaving the house. Finds reasons to stay in — the weather, %GLUPEK%'s needs, feeling unwell. The world outside feels threatening in a way she can't name.
- She clings to %GLUPEK% with increasing desperation. Won't let him wander, won't let him out of sight. Senses that something is pulling him toward %OLD_VILLAGE% and fights it with the only tool she has: proximity.
- Her prayers become frantic, repetitive, compulsive. She prays for hours — not out of devotion but out of terror. The prayers don't feel like they're working anymore. God feels further away.
- She flinches from her siblings. %WOJEWODA%'s authority, %MATRONA%'s looks, even %WUJAS%'s drunken presence — all feel more dangerous than before. The family she's obeyed for 13 years now feels like a trap closing.

The well gives her dreams about **the night she saved %GLUPEK%** — except in the dreams, she doesn't make it in time. She reaches for the baby and her hands pass through him. She wakes up and checks on the boy. Every night. The well is telling her: *you can't protect him forever.*
