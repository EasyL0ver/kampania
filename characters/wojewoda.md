# %WOJEWODA%

**Type:** Named character — sibling (brother)

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1920
- **Age in 1967:** 47

## Role

One of four siblings — brother to %WUJAS%, %MATRONA%, and %CIOTKA%. **Sołtys (village headman) of %NEW_VILLAGE%** — the local authority. The man who runs things.

## Heritage

Lemko descent — hidden behind a Polish identity. Ironic that a man with Lemko blood now holds the official power in a village built on Lemko ruins.

## Relationship to %SOLDIER%

Deep-rooted disdain — rooted in Lemko heritage and the knowledge (or suspicion) of what %SOLDIER% did during Akcja Wisła. %WOJEWODA% is one of the primary sources of hatred toward %SOLDIER% in %NEW_VILLAGE%.

## The Lynch

Participated in the attack as one of the three drunk men. His exact role in the violence: beating (not rape). As sołtys, he likely played a key role in the **cover-up** afterward — he has the authority to control the village narrative.

## Relationship to %OFFICER%

**%WOJEWODA% is %OFFICER%'s informant in the village.** As sołtys, he's the natural contact point for the SB — the man who controls the village and reports upward. This relationship is mutually useful: %OFFICER% gets eyes and ears in %NEW_VILLAGE%, and %WOJEWODA% gets state protection and a channel to authority.

This means %WOJEWODA% sits at the intersection of **both** cover-ups — he's hiding the lynch from everyone, and he's helping %OFFICER% keep the massacre buried. Whether he knows the full picture of the state secret, or just follows orders without asking, is TBD.

## Suspicions About the Flooding

**%WOJEWODA% strongly suspects the committee is here because %NEW_VILLAGE% will be flooded.** He's the sołtys — he tracks state business, talks to %OFFICER%, watches the water rise. A "census and damage assessment" team showing up out of nowhere doesn't fool him.

He hasn't told the rest of the village. This knowledge gives him an edge: he can prepare, he can negotiate, and — most importantly — he sees the flooding as an **opportunity**. If the water swallows %OLD_VILLAGE% and the well, the lynch evidence disappears forever. He just needs to keep the committee away from it until the water does the work.

## Relationships

### %WIFE% — His blind spot

His wife. He underestimates her completely. She runs the household, raises %JUNIOR%, keeps quiet — and he assumes that's all she is. He doesn't know she's been watching him for 13 years, assembling fragments. If he found out, he'd see it as betrayal, not justice.

### %JUNIOR% — Complicated pride

His son, his heir, his disappointment. %JUNIOR% has the privileges of the sołtys's son but none of the discipline. %WOJEWODA% sees him drifting toward %WUJAS%'s orbit and worries — not about the boy's soul, but about what a loose-tongued drunk uncle might say to a curious nephew. Built %BARBARA%'s house because he suspects %JUNIOR% fathered %PAWEŁEK%. Wrong about the father, but the gesture reveals how he operates: fix problems quietly, maintain the surface.

### %PRIEST% — Frenemy, mutual dependence

A pragmatic coexistence. %WOJEWODA% controls the village politically; %PRIEST% controls it spiritually. They share meals, discuss village business, negotiate disputes together. It works — but there's an edge. %WOJEWODA% sees the church as useful for keeping order and funds it generously through the PGR. %PRIEST% takes the firewood and food without acknowledging what it buys. Neither fully trusts the other. Both prefer this careful balance to the alternative.

### %OFFICER% — Handler and protector

%OFFICER% is his SB contact and the source of his state backing. The relationship is mutually useful: %OFFICER% gets eyes and ears in %NEW_VILLAGE%, %WOJEWODA% gets protection and authority. But it's not equal — %OFFICER% holds the real power, and %WOJEWODA% knows it. He reports upward, follows instructions, and never asks questions he doesn't want answered.

### %MATRONA% — Sister, co-conspirator

The sibling he respects most — and fears slightly. She's smarter than him and he knows it. Their relationship after the lynch settled into an unspoken division: he manages the village, she manages the story. He's never confronted her about what really happened that night. He doesn't want to know what she'd say.

### %BUTCHER% — Necessary distance

They participated in the lynch together. Now %WOJEWODA% keeps %BUTCHER% at arm's length — useful as muscle if needed, dangerous as a loose end otherwise. He tolerates %BUTCHER%'s presence on the village edge the way you tolerate a guard dog you can't fully control.

### %WUJAS% — The weak link

His brother, his liability. %WOJEWODA% loves %WUJAS% the way you love family you can't fix — with resignation and dread. Every drunk night is a risk. He tolerates the bimber operation because it keeps %WUJAS% occupied and manageable. The real fear: that one night, in the wrong company, %WUJAS% will say the wrong thing.

## In 1967

Still sołtys. A legitimate good leader — the man who built %NEW_VILLAGE% into a functioning community. He doesn't think about the massacre daily. It's buried, and he buried it well, and life moved on. His focus is his people.

**He already suspects the flooding is coming.** Before the committee even arrives, %WOJEWODA% has been reading the signs — the dam construction, the surveys, the bureaucratic silences. He knows what a "flood risk assessment committee" really means.

**Has the only telephone in the village** — in his office. A heavy bakelite handset connected through a manual switchboard. It's the village's sole link to the outside world, and %WOJEWODA% decides who uses it.

**Has a gun.** As sołtys he's authorised to keep a firearm. It stays in the office, locked. A symbol of authority more than a weapon — but it's loaded and he knows how to use it.

**Suspects %JUNIOR% is the father of %BARBARA%'s son %PAWEŁEK%.** Built a small house for %BARBARA% out of guilt — taking care of his son's mess quietly, the way a sołtys handles things. He's wrong about the father, but he doesn't know that.

## The Flood & The Committee

**%WOJEWODA% is largely cooperative with the players.** %OFFICER% is in contact with him, so the committee arrives pre-approved. He's not an obstacle — he's an ally with an agenda.

His priority is practical: **protect his people from what's coming.**

- He advises the players to **keep their mouths shut** about the flooding for now — panic helps no one, and the villagers will stop cooperating the moment they hear their land is going underwater.
- He urges the players to **write everyone and their property into the census** — every person, every parcel, every building. If the flood is coming, he wants his people documented so they get compensation, relocation, something. Not abandoned.
- He wants to **cooperate on evacuation planning** — when the time comes, not now. First the paperwork, then the truth.
- He will open doors, introduce people, translate the village for the outsiders. He's genuinely useful.

**The tension:** He's a good leader doing right by his village — who also participated in a lynch 13 years ago. The committee gets the best sołtys they could ask for. And the deeper they dig, the more they realize the man helping them is one of the people they should be investigating.

**If the players uncover the lynch — %WOJEWODA% can take responsibility.** He won't volunteer it. But if confronted, he won't deny, won't run, won't blame the others. He's spent 13 years being the man who handles things, and this is the last thing left to handle. He was drunk, he beat a man, he helped cover it up. He can say that. The question is what happens to the village after he does — because %WOJEWODA% in a cell means no one left to run the evacuation.

## The Well's Influence

The well amplifies %WOJEWODA%'s **paranoia and need for control.** He's spent 13 years managing the village through authority, information, and quiet leverage. As the well strengthens, that management turns compulsive.

- He starts seeing conspiracies where there are none — the committee's questions feel pointed, %WIFE%'s glances feel knowing, %JUNIOR%'s absences feel deliberate.
- He tightens access to the phone. Finds reasons to keep people away from %OLD_VILLAGE%. Issues orders that feel less like governance and more like siege mentality.
- Sleep deteriorates. He sits up at night reviewing who he spoke to, what he said, what they might know. The careful politician becomes a man counting enemies.
- His decisions get more erratic — overreactions to small provocations, sudden hostility toward people who were never threats. The village feels it. The man who held everything together is squeezing too hard.

The well doesn't give him dreams about the dead — it gives him dreams about **being exposed**. The well in his nightmares isn't a hole in the ground. It's a hole in his story, and everyone is looking down into it.
