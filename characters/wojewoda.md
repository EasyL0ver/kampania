# %WOJEWODA%

**Type:** Named character — sibling (brother) / sołtys

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1920
- **Age in 1967:** 47
- **Heritage:** Lemko (hidden behind Polish identity)
- **Lives in:** [%WOJEWODA%'s house](../places/new-village.md#wojewodas-house) — with [%WIFE%](wife.md), [%JUNIOR%](junior.md); [%WUJAS%](secondary-characters.md) crashes here
- **Armed:** Sołtys's pistol (locked in office, loaded)

## Character

The man who runs things. **Sołtys (village headman)** — legitimate good leader who built [%NEW_VILLAGE%](../places/new-village.md) into a functioning community. Pragmatic, authoritative, controlled. Doesn't share burdens. Ironic that a man with Lemko blood holds official power in a village built on Lemko ruins.

## Background / Role

One of four siblings — brother to [%WUJAS%](secondary-characters.md), [%MATRONA%](matrona.md), and [%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md). Deep-rooted disdain for [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md) — rooted in Lemko heritage and knowledge of what [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md) did during Akcja Wisła. Primary source of hatred toward [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md) in [%NEW_VILLAGE%](../places/new-village.md).

## The Lynch

Participated as one of three drunk men. His role: beating (not rape). As sołtys, played key role in the **cover-up** — authority to control the village narrative.

## In 1967

- **Has the only telephone in the village** — bakelite handset, manual switchboard, in his office. Village's sole link to the outside world. He decides who uses it.
- **Suspects flooding before the committee arrives** — tracks state business, talks to [%OFFICER%](officer.md), watches water rise. A "census and damage assessment" team doesn't fool him. Hasn't told the village.
- **Suspects [%JUNIOR%](junior.md) fathered [%PAWEŁEK%](secondary-characters.md).** Built [%BARBARA%](barbara.md) a small house out of guilt — handling his son's mess quietly. Wrong about the father, but the gesture reveals how he operates.
- Flooding is an **opportunity**: if water swallows [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../places/old-village.md) and [the well](../story-facts/the-well.md), the lynch evidence disappears forever. Just needs to keep the committee away until the water does the work.

## The Flood & The Committee

**Largely cooperative.** [%OFFICER%](officer.md) is in contact — committee arrives pre-approved. Not an obstacle, an ally with an agenda.

Priority: **protect his people.**

- Advises players to **keep quiet about flooding** — panic stops cooperation.
- Urges **thorough census** — every person, parcel, building documented for compensation and relocation.
- Wants to **cooperate on evacuation planning** — when the time comes, not now.
- Opens doors, introduces people, translates the village for outsiders. Genuinely useful.

**The tension:** good leader doing right by his village — who participated in a lynch 13 years ago. The deeper players dig, the more they realize the man helping them is someone they should be investigating.

**If confronted about the lynch — will take responsibility.** Won't volunteer it. But won't deny, run, or blame others. He was drunk, he beat a man, he helped cover it up. He can say that. The question: %WOJEWODA% in a cell means no one left to run the evacuation.

## Well Influence

Amplifies **paranoia and need for control.** 13 years of managing through authority; now it turns compulsive.

- Sees conspiracies — committee's questions feel pointed, [%WIFE%](wife.md)'s glances knowing, [%JUNIOR%](junior.md)'s absences deliberate.
- Tightens access to phone. Finds reasons to keep people from [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../places/old-village.md). Orders feel less like governance, more like siege mentality.
- Sleep deteriorates. Sits up reviewing who he spoke to, what he said, what they might know. Careful politician becomes a man counting enemies.
- Decisions get erratic — overreactions, sudden hostility toward non-threats. The village feels it. The man who held everything together is squeezing too hard.
- **Dream:** [the well](../story-facts/the-well.md) isn't a hole in the ground — it's a hole in his story, and everyone is looking down into it.

## Flood Attitude

Sees flooding before it's announced. Opportunity and threat: water destroys evidence, but also destroys everything he's built. Pragmatic — protect his people first, secrets second.

## For Players

The most cooperative NPC at first — genuinely useful guide and ally. His agenda (thorough census, quiet evacuation prep) aligns with the committee's. The reveal that he's one of the perpetrators is devastating because he's been *helpful*. Unlike [%MATRONA%](matrona.md), if confronted he can face it.
