# %WOJEWODA%

**Type:** Named character — sibling (brother)

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1920
- **Age in 1967:** 47

## Role

One of four siblings — brother to %WUJAS%, %MATRONA%, and %CIOTKA%. **Sołtys (village headman) of %NEW_VILLAGE%** — the local authority. The man who runs things.

## Heritage

Lemko descent — hidden behind a Polish identity. Ironic that a man with Lemko blood now holds the official power in a village built on Lemko ruins.

## Relationship to %SOLDIER%

Deep-rooted disdain — rooted in Lemko heritage and the knowledge (or suspicion) of what %SOLDIER% did during Akcja Wisła. %WOJEWODA% is one of the primary sources of hatred toward %SOLDIER% in %NEW_VILLAGE%.

## The Lynch

Participated in the attack as one of the three drunk men. His exact role in the violence: beating (not rape). As sołtys, he likely played a key role in the **cover-up** afterward — he has the authority to control the village narrative.

## Relationship to %OFFICER%

**%WOJEWODA% is %OFFICER%'s informant in the village.** As sołtys, he's the natural contact point for the SB — the man who controls the village and reports upward. This relationship is mutually useful: %OFFICER% gets eyes and ears in %NEW_VILLAGE%, and %WOJEWODA% gets state protection and a channel to authority.

This means %WOJEWODA% sits at the intersection of **both** cover-ups — he's hiding the lynch from everyone, and he's helping %OFFICER% keep the massacre buried. Whether he knows the full picture of the state secret, or just follows orders without asking, is TBD.

## Suspicions About the Flooding

**%WOJEWODA% strongly suspects the committee is here because %NEW_VILLAGE% will be flooded.** He's the sołtys — he tracks state business, talks to %OFFICER%, watches the water rise. A "census and damage assessment" team showing up out of nowhere doesn't fool him.

He hasn't told the rest of the village. This knowledge gives him an edge: he can prepare, he can negotiate, and — most importantly — he sees the flooding as an **opportunity**. If the water swallows %OLD_VILLAGE% and the well, the lynch evidence disappears forever. He just needs to keep the committee away from it until the water does the work.

## In 1967

Still sołtys. The man the committee will deal with first — the official face of %NEW_VILLAGE%. He controls access, information, and the villagers. He has the most to lose if the truth comes out — not just the lynch, but his position, his hidden heritage, everything.

**Has the only telephone in the village** — in his office. A heavy bakelite handset connected through a manual switchboard. It's the village's sole link to the outside world, and %WOJEWODA% decides who uses it.

**Suspects %JUNIOR% is the father of %BARBARA%'s son %PAWEŁEK%.** Built a small house for %BARBARA% out of guilt — taking care of his son's mess quietly, the way a sołtys handles things. He's wrong about the father, but he doesn't know that.
