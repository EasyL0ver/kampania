# %MATRONA%

**Type:** Named character — sibling (sister)

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1922
- **Age in 1967:** 45

## Role

One of four siblings — sister to %WOJEWODA%, %WUJAS%, and %CIOTKA%. Part of a family unit in %NEW_VILLAGE%.

## Heritage

Lemko descent — hidden. See %WOJEWODA% for details on the siblings' shared background.

## Relationships

- **%PAINTER%** — husband (married after the lynch)
- Siblings: %WOJEWODA%, %WUJAS%, %CIOTKA%

### %PRIEST% — Her unwitting shield

%MATRONA% has cultivated the deepest relationship with %PRIEST% of any villager. She is **devoutly, performatively, and possibly genuinely religious** — attends every service, organizes church events, placed %KRYSTIAN% as altar boy. Their conversations about faith and morality are warm, almost a friendship.

Her confessions are masterpieces of misdirection — real guilt, carefully wrapped so %PRIEST% sees a struggling soul rather than the architect of a murder. He will instinctively defend her to anyone who asks. She's made the village priest her shield without him ever knowing it.

### %WIFE% — Quiet contempt

%MATRONA% watches %WIFE% carefully. She knows her sister-in-law suspects something — the sideways glances, the too-careful questions. %MATRONA% keeps %WIFE% at arm's length with the same controlled warmth she applies to everything. Polite, familial, impenetrable. If she ever decided %WIFE% was a real threat, she'd act — but so far %WIFE% has been too powerless to worry about.

## Relationship to %SOLDIER%

**Deep, layered hatred.** On the surface — personal betrayal: %PAINTER% chose %JAGNA% over her. But beneath that: %SOLDIER% took the best land, his family thrived while hers hid their Lemko identity, and his teenage daughter humiliated her in front of the whole village. The affair was the last insult in a long chain.

## The Lynch — The True Architect

The village believes %BUTCHER% was the coldest head that night. **They're wrong. It was %MATRONA%.**

She didn't stumble to her brothers in tears after catching the lovers. She went to them **with intent**. She got them drinking. She fed the rage. She aimed them at %JAGNA% like a weapon.

When %SOLDIER% was dead and the drunk men were panicking, it was %MATRONA% — **sober, clear-eyed** — who said to get rid of the problem. She didn't spell it out. She didn't have to. %BUTCHER% picked it up and carried it out. The wife and the baby. She never gave a direct order. She just made the suggestion and let the worst man in the room do the rest.

She never touched anyone. Every hand that night was male. That's not an accident — it's her design.

## In 1967

Married to %PAINTER%. Respectable. The grieving sister-in-law, the good wife who forgave her fiancé's betrayal. A woman wronged who chose grace.

In reality: the architect of a massacre, hiding behind 13 years of performance. She's had longer to practice her story than anyone. She's the **hardest NPC to crack** — not because she won't talk, but because she'll talk plenty. She'll give the players a version of events that's almost true, with herself carefully edited out of every scene that matters.

**%PAINTER% is her insurance.** She's kept him broken, dependent, and terrified for 13 years. He knows the truth — he heard her voice that night — and she's made sure he'll never speak it. His guilt over the affair, his gratitude that she "forgave" him, his fear of what she's capable of — she wields all of it.

## The Flood & The Committee

**%MATRONA% follows %WOJEWODA%'s lead.** She's his henchman — compliant, useful, cooperative on the surface. If %WOJEWODA% says help the committee, she helps the committee. She'll host them, feed them, answer their questions about the village with practiced warmth. She's the perfect welcoming face.

**But she watches for depth.** The flood assessment, the census, the logistics — all fine. The moment the players start digging into village history, into %OLD_VILLAGE%, into what happened — she notices before anyone else. She won't obstruct openly. She'll redirect. Change the subject. Offer something more interesting. Mention how busy the players must be with the flood work.

If redirection fails, she escalates quietly — a word to %WOJEWODA% that the committee is asking strange questions. A concerned suggestion to %PRIEST% that these outsiders don't understand village life. She never confronts. She manages.

**This is where she and %WOJEWODA% diverge.** If the players uncover the lynch, %WOJEWODA% is willing to take responsibility — he can face what he did. %MATRONA% cannot. Admitting the lynch means admitting she *designed* it. %WOJEWODA% can survive as a man who did something terrible while drunk. %MATRONA% cannot survive as the sober woman who ordered a family killed. She will fight the truth harder than anyone because she has the most to lose.

## Redemption

%MATRONA%'s redemption is the hardest in the story. She'd have to admit not just complicity but **authorship**. She designed the murder of a family, including a child, and spent 13 years letting others carry the guilt. Her path to redemption requires accepting that she is the worst person in this village — worse than the drunk men, worse than %BUTCHER% — because she was sober and she chose it.

## The Well's Influence

The well erodes %MATRONA%'s **performance.** She's spent 13 years maintaining a flawless mask — the pious wife, the good sister, the woman who forgave. The well doesn't shatter her the way it shatters %WUJAS%. It creates micro-cracks.

- A comment too sharp in conversation — a flash of contempt she'd normally suppress. A moment where the real woman surfaces before the mask snaps back.
- Intrusive thoughts she can't control. She catches herself staring at %PAINTER% with something that isn't the rehearsed warmth. She loses the thread of a conversation with %PRIEST% mid-sentence.
- She over-corrects. Becomes more devout, more generous, more publicly perfect — which reads as desperate to anyone paying attention. The performance becomes louder, which makes it look more like a performance.
- Her control over %PAINTER% tightens — more surveillance, more pressure, more reminders of what he owes her. She senses him slipping and responds with the only tool she has: fear.

The well doesn't give her dreams about the dead. It gives her dreams about **%JAGNA%** — alive, young, looking at her. Not accusatory. Just looking. %MATRONA% can face anything except being *seen* by the girl she had killed.

## The Only Paths to Her

- **%PAINTER%** — if the players free him from her control. He heard her voice that night.
- **%BUTCHER%** — if he remembers the sober woman giving orders. The coldest head in the room may remember there was a colder one.
- **%CIOTKA%** — if still alive, she was there. She may know who really gave the order to kill the baby.
