# %MATRONA%

**Type:** Named character — sibling (sister) / true architect of the lynch

## Vital Statistics

- **Born:** 1922
- **Age in 1967:** 45
- **Heritage:** Lemko (hidden) — see [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md) for shared sibling background
- **Lives in:** [%MATRONA% & %PAINTER%'s house](../places/new-village.md#matrona--painters-house) — with [%PAINTER%](secondary-characters.md), [%EWA%](secondary-characters.md), [%KRYSTIAN%](secondary-characters.md)

## Character

Devoutly, performatively, and possibly genuinely religious. Attends every service, organizes church events, placed [%KRYSTIAN%](secondary-characters.md) as altar boy. Controlled warmth on the surface, impenetrable beneath. The **hardest NPC to crack** — not because she won't talk, but because she'll talk plenty with herself carefully edited out of every scene that matters.

## Background / Role

One of four siblings — sister to [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md), [%WUJAS%](secondary-characters.md), and [%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md). Married [%PAINTER%](secondary-characters.md) after the lynch. Presents as the grieving sister-in-law who forgave her fiancé's betrayal. In reality: the architect of a massacre, hiding behind 13 years of performance.

## Relationship to %SOLDIER%

**Deep, layered hatred.** Surface: personal betrayal — [%PAINTER%](secondary-characters.md) chose [%JAGNA%](jagna.md) over her. Beneath: [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md) took the best land, his family thrived while hers hid their Lemko identity, his teenage daughter humiliated her publicly. The affair was the last insult in a long chain.

## The Lynch — The True Architect

The village believes [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md) was the coldest head that night. **Wrong. It was %MATRONA%.**

- Went to her brothers **with intent** after catching the lovers. Got them drinking. Fed the rage. Aimed them at [%JAGNA%](jagna.md) like a weapon.
- When [%SOLDIER%](soldier.md) was dead and the drunk men panicked, it was %MATRONA% — **sober, clear-eyed** — who said to get rid of the problem. Never spelled it out. [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md) picked it up — the wife and the baby.
- Never gave a direct order. Never touched anyone. Every hand that night was male. **Not accident — design.**

## In 1967

- **[%PAINTER%](secondary-characters.md) is her insurance.** Kept him broken, dependent, terrified for 13 years. He heard her voice that night. His guilt over the affair, gratitude for her "forgiveness," fear of what she's capable of — she wields all of it.
- Follows [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md)'s lead with the committee — cooperative, welcoming, the perfect face.
- **Watches for depth.** The moment players dig into village history or [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../places/old-village.md), she notices first. Won't obstruct openly — redirects, changes subject, mentions how busy the committee must be.
- If redirection fails, escalates quietly: a word to [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md), a concern to [%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md) about outsiders. Never confronts. Manages.

## Divergence from %WOJEWODA%

If the lynch is uncovered, [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md) can take responsibility — a man who did something terrible while drunk. **%MATRONA% cannot.** Admitting the lynch means admitting she *designed* it, sober. She will fight the truth harder than anyone because she has the most to lose.

## Redemption

The hardest in the story. Would have to admit not just complicity but **authorship** — designed the murder of a family including a child, spent 13 years letting others carry the guilt. Requires accepting she's worse than the drunk men, worse than [%BUTCHER%](butcher.md) — because she was sober and she chose it.

## Relationships

- **[%PAINTER%](secondary-characters.md)** — husband (married after the lynch). Her insurance — broken, dependent, terrified.
- **[%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md)** — brother. She's his henchman; he manages the village, she manages the story.
- **[%WUJAS%](secondary-characters.md)** — brother.
- **[%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md)** — sister.
- **[%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md)** — her unwitting shield. Confessions are masterpieces of misdirection — real guilt wrapped so he sees a struggling soul, not a murderer. He will instinctively defend her.
- **[%WIFE%](wife.md)** — quiet contempt. Knows her sister-in-law suspects something. Keeps her at arm's length with controlled warmth. If [%WIFE%](wife.md) became a real threat, she'd act — but so far too powerless to worry about.

## Well Influence

Erodes %MATRONA%'s **performance.** Doesn't shatter her like [%WUJAS%](secondary-characters.md) — creates micro-cracks:

- Comments too sharp — flashes of contempt she'd normally suppress. The real woman surfaces before the mask snaps back.
- Intrusive thoughts. Catches herself staring at [%PAINTER%](secondary-characters.md) without rehearsed warmth. Loses thread of conversation with [%PRIEST%](secondary-characters.md) mid-sentence.
- Over-corrects: more devout, more generous, more publicly perfect — reads as desperate to anyone paying attention.
- Control over [%PAINTER%](secondary-characters.md) tightens — more surveillance, pressure, reminders of what he owes her.
- **Dream:** not the dead but **[%JAGNA%](jagna.md)** — alive, young, just looking at her. Not accusatory. Just *seen*. %MATRONA% can face anything except being seen by the girl she had killed.

## Flood Attitude

Follows [%WOJEWODA%](wojewoda.md)'s lead — cooperate, help, comply. The flood itself matters less to her than keeping the story intact.

## For Players

Presents a version of events that's almost true, with herself edited out. Players need indirect paths to crack her.

## The Only Paths to Her

- **[%PAINTER%](secondary-characters.md)** — if freed from her control. He heard her voice that night.
- **[%BUTCHER%](butcher.md)** — if he remembers the sober woman giving orders. The coldest head may remember there was a colder one.
- **[%CIOTKA%](ciotka.md)** — if still alive. Was there; may know who really ordered the killing of the baby.
