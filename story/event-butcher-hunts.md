# %BUTCHER% Hunts

**Type:** Story event — escalating threat / player-dependent

## Trigger

%BUTCHER% is watching. He's been watching since the committee arrived. The moment he believes the players **know too much** — about the lynch, the well, the family — he decides to solve the problem the same way he solved it in 1954. No witnesses.

What counts as "too much":
- Players asking about %SOLDIER%'s family directly
- Players visiting the well or showing interest in %OLD_VILLAGE%
- Players talking to %WUJAS% when he's drunk and loose
- %CIOTKA%'s death — the secret is already out, and the players were there
- Any direct confrontation with %WOJEWODA% or %BUTCHER% about the lynch

## How He Operates

%BUTCHER% is not impulsive. He was the coldest head in the room in 1954 — the one who said "no witnesses" while the others were still in shock. He hasn't changed.

- **He watches first.** Tracks what the players do, who they talk to, where they go.
- **He uses the isolation.** The road is flooded. No backup is coming. The forest is his territory, the village edge is his domain.
- **He has the dogs.** They're not pets — they're an extension of him. Unsettling, aggressive, useful.
- **He picks off stragglers.** A player alone at night, near %OLD_VILLAGE%, on the village edge — that's an opportunity.
- **He doesn't announce himself.** No threats, no warnings. The first sign that %BUTCHER% is hunting is when something goes wrong.

## Escalation

The threat isn't binary — it builds:

1. **Observation** — %BUTCHER%'s dogs appear near places the players visit. He's seen in the distance, watching.
2. **Intimidation** — Something goes missing. A door left open. A dead animal on the doorstep. Messages without words.
3. **Action** — Direct physical threat. A player cornered. A nighttime approach. The road is flooded and nobody's coming.

## The Players' Options

- **Avoid triggering him** — stay surface-level, don't dig. Safe, but they learn nothing.
- **Confront him** — dangerous, but %BUTCHER% alone against multiple players is different from %BUTCHER% with two drunk accomplices in the dark.
- **Turn the village against him** — he's already an outcast. If the truth comes out, nobody will protect him.
- **Use %WOJEWODA%** — the sołtys can control %BUTCHER%. But that means trusting the sołtys.

## Open Questions

- Does %BUTCHER% act alone or try to recruit %WOJEWODA%?
- At what point does %WOJEWODA% decide %BUTCHER% is a bigger threat than the players?
- Can the players earn %BUTCHER%'s trust — or is he beyond reach?
- What's his endgame if the water rises and the village is evacuated? He can't kill everyone.
