# %PAWEŁEK% Falls Mysteriously Ill

**Type:** Story event — atmospheric / clue delivery / moral choice

## Trigger

As the well grows stronger, %PAWEŁEK% (age 4) is drawn to %OLD_VILLAGE%. He's not haunted — he's curious. The water is shiny, it makes sounds. He drinks from the well.

It's 1967, the region is flooded. Floodwater contaminates everything — wells, streams, puddles. A child drinking bad water is the most ordinary tragedy imaginable.

By evening, he's burning with fever, convulsing, delirious. The sickness comes on fast and fierce. The medical explanation is airtight — but his fever-dreams are what unsettle everyone.

## What Happens

%BARBARA%, frantic and helpless, moves between the priest and the village healer. Some say the child should be taken to the town doctor. Others insist the real sickness isn't in the body—it's what the water brought.

%PAWEŁEK% lies fevered, muttering in fragments:
- He speaks about "the water under the ground" 
- He cries out about "the old woman" (possibly the %HAG%)
- He describes sounds, whispers he heard near the well
- His fever dreams are vivid, terrifying, incoherent

The delirious ramblings spike the village's paranoia. **What does the contaminated water know? What does %PAWEŁEK% know?**

## What The Village Believes

Different interpretations circulate:
- **The well victims are poisoning the flood** — the dead want the living to drink their curse, to suffer their sickness
- **%PAWEŁEK% is the first** — if one child falls ill from the floodwater, how many others will follow? Is the whole village poisoned?
- **%PAWEŁEK% stumbled onto something** — he went where he shouldn't have, drank from the wrong place, came too close to the well
- **The sickness is spiritual, not physical** — the contamination isn't just disease, it's the land itself rejecting what it contains

## What The Players Can Do

**Medical angle:**
- Find clean water and medicine (nearly impossible in flood conditions)
- Consult with %PRIEST% about whether a doctor exists in the flooded region
- Determine exactly where %PAWEŁEK% drank from—this reveals geography, access points, areas players shouldn't go
- Investigate: are other children showing symptoms?

**Investigation angle:**
- Interview %PAWEŁEK% when lucid enough (he's 4—fragmented information)
- Question %BARBARA% about where he was playing when he drank
- Map the floodwater's flow—where is it most contaminated? Where did it come from?
- Discover if the floodwater has indeed mixed with soil/remains from the well area

**Social angle:**
- %BARBARA% is vulnerable, desperate, will confess things under duress
- Other mothers become fearful—is the sickness spreading? Is any water safe?
- The village may demand the players find a solution or accept blame if more children fall ill
- Some may see this as divine judgment on %BARBARA% (unmarried mother) or the village itself

## Possible Resolutions

- **Recovery through care** — %PAWEŁEK% survives with proper hydration and rest. The village credits prayer or folk remedy. A small victory that builds trust in the players.
- **Recovery with knowledge** — As he recovers, %PAWEŁEK% (or %BARBARA%, guilt-ridden) reveals more detail: a location, a smell, something he saw. A clue about the well?
- **Escalation** — Other children start showing symptoms. The illness spreads. The village becomes desperate, dangerous, looking for someone to blame.
- **Supernatural interpretation** — Despite obvious medical cause, the village refuses to see it. The illness "proves" the well is cursed, the dead are rising, the land is poisoned. Hysteria follows.
- **Player intervention determines outcome** — Saving %PAWEŁEK% earns genuine gratitude and opens %BARBARA% to trust. Neglecting him spreads distrust and fear. How the players handle %BARBARA%'s desperation matters.

## Connection Points

- **%BARBARA%'s collapse** — A sick child breaks her. She'll reveal who %PAWEŁEK%'s father is if it might help save him.
- **%WUJAS% and %JUNIOR%'s response** — How does the biological father react? Guilt? Avoidance? This reveals character.
- **%HAG%'s knowledge** — Does she recognize waterborne sickness? Does she know which waters are truly poisoned? What price for her help?
- **%PRIEST%'s limits** — Prayer alone won't cure dysentery. Where does his faith end and desperation begin?
- **The well** — %PAWEŁEK%'s sickness is proof that something is genuinely wrong with the water. The well's location and contents become undeniable.
- **Village blame** — Outsiders? %BARBARA%? The curse? The village seeks a target for its fear.

## Open Questions

- How quickly does %PAWEŁEK% recover? (Timeline affects village panic)
- Do other children fall ill? (Epidemic vs isolated incident)
- Can the players save him, or is medical care simply unavailable?
- What does %PAWEŁEK% (or %BARBARA%) reveal about where he drank from?
- Does this push %BARBARA% to finally name his father?
- Does the village blame the well, or blame the players for arriving and "bringing sickness"?
