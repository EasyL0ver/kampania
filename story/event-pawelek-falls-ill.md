# Pawełek Kopacz Falls Mysteriously Ill

**Type:** Story event — atmospheric / clue delivery / moral choice

## Trigger

As the well grows stronger, Pawełek Kopacz (age 4) is drawn to %OLD_VILLAGE%. He's not haunted — he's curious. The water is shiny, it makes sounds. He drinks from the well.

It's 1967, the region is flooded. Floodwater contaminates everything — wells, streams, puddles. A child drinking bad water is the most ordinary tragedy imaginable.

By evening, he's burning with fever, convulsing, delirious. The sickness comes on fast and fierce. The medical explanation is airtight — but his fever-dreams are what unsettle everyone.

## What Happens

Barbara Kopacz, frantic and helpless, moves between the priest and the village healer. Some say the child should be taken to the town doctor. Others insist the real sickness isn't in the body—it's what the water brought.

Pawełek Kopacz lies fevered, muttering in fragments:
- He speaks about "the water under the ground" 
- He cries out about "the old woman" (possibly the Paraskewia Chyłak)
- He describes sounds, whispers he heard near the well
- His fever dreams are vivid, terrifying, incoherent

The delirious ramblings spike the village's paranoia. **What does the contaminated water know? What does Pawełek Kopacz know?**

## What The Village Believes

Different interpretations circulate:
- **The well victims are poisoning the flood** — the dead want the living to drink their curse, to suffer their sickness
- **Pawełek Kopacz is the first** — if one child falls ill from the floodwater, how many others will follow? Is the whole village poisoned?
- **Pawełek Kopacz stumbled onto something** — he went where he shouldn't have, drank from the wrong place, came too close to the well
- **The sickness is spiritual, not physical** — the contamination isn't just disease, it's the land itself rejecting what it contains

## What The Players Can Do

**Medical angle:**
- Find clean water and medicine (nearly impossible in flood conditions)
- Consult with ks. Władysław Pająk about whether a doctor exists in the flooded region
- Determine exactly where Pawełek Kopacz drank from—this reveals geography, access points, areas players shouldn't go
- Investigate: are other children showing symptoms?

**Investigation angle:**
- Interview Pawełek Kopacz when lucid enough (he's 4—fragmented information)
- Question Barbara Kopacz about where he was playing when he drank
- Map the floodwater's flow—where is it most contaminated? Where did it come from?
- Discover if the floodwater has indeed mixed with soil/remains from the well area

**Social angle:**
- Barbara Kopacz is vulnerable, desperate, will confess things under duress
- Other mothers become fearful—is the sickness spreading? Is any water safe?
- The village may demand the players find a solution or accept blame if more children fall ill
- Some may see this as divine judgment on Barbara Kopacz (unmarried mother) or the village itself

## Possible Resolutions

- **Recovery through care** — Pawełek Kopacz survives with proper hydration and rest. The village credits prayer or folk remedy. A small victory that builds trust in the players.
- **Recovery with knowledge** — As he recovers, Pawełek Kopacz (or Barbara Kopacz, guilt-ridden) reveals more detail: a location, a smell, something he saw. A clue about the well?
- **Escalation** — Other children start showing symptoms. The illness spreads. The village becomes desperate, dangerous, looking for someone to blame.
- **Supernatural interpretation** — Despite obvious medical cause, the village refuses to see it. The illness "proves" the well is cursed, the dead are rising, the land is poisoned. Hysteria follows.
- **Player intervention determines outcome** — Saving Pawełek Kopacz earns genuine gratitude and opens Barbara Kopacz to trust. Neglecting him spreads distrust and fear. How the players handle Barbara Kopacz's desperation matters.

## Connection Points

- **Barbara Kopacz's collapse** — A sick child breaks her. She'll reveal who Pawełek Kopacz's father is if it might help save him.
- **Tadek Gajda and Marek Gajda's response** — How does the biological father react? Guilt? Avoidance? This reveals character.
- **Paraskewia Chyłak's knowledge** — Does she recognize waterborne sickness? Does she know which waters are truly poisoned? What price for her help?
- **ks. Władysław Pająk's limits** — Prayer alone won't cure dysentery. Where does his faith end and desperation begin?
- **The well** — Pawełek Kopacz's sickness is proof that something is genuinely wrong with the water. The well's location and contents become undeniable.
- **Village blame** — Outsiders? Barbara Kopacz? The curse? The village seeks a target for its fear.
