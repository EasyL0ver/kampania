# The Report — The State's Ending

**Type:** Story ending — the final scene, plays after everything else

## What It Is

The game is over. The flood, the ritual, the lynch or its prevention — all of it is done. The players leave the village. %OFFICER% meets them. They hand over their report.

**He reads it in the car.**

## The Choice

Somewhere during the five days, the players may have discovered the 1947 Lemko massacre — the state's crime, buried under the dam. Akcja Wisła. The original sin that predates the lynch, the well, everything.

%OFFICER% has made it clear — implicitly or explicitly — that the state wants this buried. The dam is infrastructure *and* a grave. The flooding isn't just about hydroelectric power. It's about making the evidence disappear forever.

The players write the report. They choose what goes in.

**If the massacre is not in the report:** %OFFICER% reads, nods, closes the folder. The car drives away. The state's secret stays buried. The dam will finish the job.

**If the massacre is in the report:** %OFFICER% reads. He stops. He looks up at the players. He reaches for his gun.

**If the players called %PROFESSOR%:** %OFFICER% reaches for the gun — and stops. He knows. The players told %PROFESSOR% during the game, by phone. The truth is already outside this car. Killing them changes nothing. The gun goes back. The car drives in silence. The players live — and so does the truth.

**If the players didn't call %PROFESSOR%:** The gun. THE END.

The players may not even realise the phone call saved their lives. They may not connect it until the ride home — or never. But the GM knows.

## What It Means

The players spent five days fighting for a village. They may have saved the living, honoured the dead, delivered justice to the guilty. They may have played perfectly. And none of it matters to the state. The state has one question: **did you write down what we told you not to write down?**

The gun is the answer to every player who thought the truth would set them free. The truth sets no one free. The truth gets you shot in a car on a mountain road in 1967 Poland.

## For the GM

This scene plays last. Always. Regardless of which endings the players achieved. The ritual, the engineering, the justice — all of it happened. And now this.

**Do not telegraph it.** The report is just paperwork. The car ride is just logistics. %OFFICER% reading is just bureaucracy. The gun is the only moment that matters, and it should come from nowhere — the way the state always comes.

**Do not resolve it.** The story ends on the gun. Not on the shot. Not on what happens after. The players don't get to know if they live or die. They get to know that they told the truth and the state responded. That's the last thing they feel.

**If the massacre isn't in the report** — the ending is quiet. Almost anticlimactic. The car drives away. The players are safe. And they know, for the rest of their lives, what they chose not to write.
