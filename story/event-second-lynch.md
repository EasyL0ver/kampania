# The Second Lynch — Zbigniew Gajda Turns the Village

**Type:** Story event — worst case / player-dependent

## Trigger

Zbigniew Gajda decides the committee is a bigger threat than the flooding. This happens when:
- The players have openly uncovered the lynch or the massacre
- The well has surfaced remains and the players have seen them
- Tadek Gajda has confessed and Zbigniew Gajda knows it
- The players have contacted por. Witold Skowron over the phone with damning information
- The village's silence has collapsed beyond repair

Zbigniew Gajda doesn't reach for a knife. He reaches for what he's always had — **authority**. He turns the village against the players.

## How He Does It

Zbigniew Gajda is the sołtys. He's kept this community together for 17 years. He knows how to move people. His pitch to the village:

- *"These outsiders are here to destroy us. They want to flood our homes and blame us for crimes that never happened."*
- *"They're state people — the same state that took the Lemkos, and now they're coming for us."*
- *"If they leave this village with that ledger, none of us get compensation. None of us get new homes. We get nothing."*

The ledger — the very thing that gave the players power — becomes the thing the village wants to take from them. History repeats: strangers who threaten the village's survival are dealt with the way Bieszczady has always dealt with problems.

## What It Looks Like

Not a mob with torches. A quiet closing of ranks:
- Doors that were open are now shut.
- The committee's lodging is no longer available.
- Tools go missing. Food stops appearing.
- People stop talking. Eyes follow the players everywhere.
- And then, one night — the same thing that happened in 1954. Drunk men, darkness, no witnesses. The road is flooded. Nobody's coming.

## Can the Players Prevent It?

Yes — multiple ways:
- **Never push hard enough to trigger it.** Stay surface-level, keep the village calm.
- **Secure allies.** Irena Gajda, ks. Władysław Pająk, Janina Gajda (if alive), Ryszard Dudka — people who won't follow Zbigniew Gajda blindly.
- **Use the phone.** If por. Witold Skowron knows what's happening, Zbigniew Gajda knows backup is eventually coming. He won't lynch state officials if the state knows they're in danger.
- **Split Zbigniew Gajda from Stanisław Rezeń.** The sołtys is political — he organizes the mob but doesn't want blood on his hands. If Stanisław Rezeń is neutralized, Zbigniew Gajda loses his enforcer.
- **Turn the village.** If enough people know the truth, Zbigniew Gajda's narrative falls apart. He can't rally a mob against outsiders when half the village wants to confess.

## Thematic Weight

The village that lynched Edward Barnaś's family is about to lynch the committee. Same method, same silence, same well waiting to receive the bodies. The only difference is whether the players have made enough human connections to break the cycle.

## Open Questions

- Does Stanisław Rezeń lead the physical violence again, or does Zbigniew Gajda find new hands?
- Where does Helena Gajda stand? Marek Gajda?
- Is this preventable in every playthrough, or can it be the ending?
- If the players survive but the lynch was attempted — what does por. Witold Skowron find on day 5?
