# %BUTCHER% Kills %HAG% — The Point of No Return

**Type:** Story event — OPTIONAL climax / turning point

**This event is not guaranteed.** It happens only if %BUTCHER%'s escalation and %HAG%'s open appearance coincide without player intervention. The players may prevent it by reaching %HAG% first, by confronting %BUTCHER%, or by events that keep them apart. If it doesn't happen, the story still works — just differently.

## What This Is

A *possible* turning point. %BUTCHER% finds %HAG% at the well, kills her, and throws her body in. The last person performing rites for the dead is silenced. The well's containment breaks. Everything after this is different.

## Setup

Two things converge:

1. **%HAG% appears openly.** The intensifying rain drives her out of hiding. She believes her 20 years of ritual are failing — the dead are angrier than ever, the well's influence is spreading. She stops sneaking in at night and begins performing her rites openly, desperately, at %OLD_VILLAGE%. Fires, chanting, the works. See `story\event-hag-appears.md`.

2. **%BUTCHER% is escalating.** The committee is poking around. The rain is worse. His dreams are worse. The compulsion that's been pulling him to the well for 13 years is screaming now. He goes to %OLD_VILLAGE% — and for the first time, someone else is there.

## The Encounter

%BUTCHER% arrives at the well and finds %HAG% — the "ghost" of %OLD_VILLAGE%, the thing villagers whisper about. A real woman. Ancient, wild, kneeling at the well, performing rites over the dead he put there.

He doesn't know she's Lemko. He doesn't know she's been containing the well. He doesn't understand her prayers. He sees a stranger at **his** well, touching **his** dead, in the place where the compulsion brings him every night. She's in the way. She's wrong. She's trying to close what needs to stay open.

**She sees him too.** She sees the man she's been fighting for years — the one who clears the debris, who feeds the well, who tears the stitches out. The repetition compulsion given a face. She's 77 years old and she's not afraid. She's been expecting this. The well was always going to win eventually.

## The Killing

It's not elaborate. %BUTCHER% is a sociopath, not a dramatist. He hits her. She falls. He throws her in the well.

The same motion. The same solution. The compulsion completing itself — another body in the hole, another silence enforced, another voice stopped. He doesn't know what he's done in the symbolic sense. He just knows the woman at the well is gone and the well is his again.

He walks home. The dogs are waiting at their perimeter. They still won't cross it.

## What Breaks

**Everything.**

%HAG%'s 20 years of rites were the only thing keeping the well's influence in check. With her death — and worse, with her body *in* the well — the containment shatters completely.

### The Dreams Explode

- **The players' dreams become unbearable.** No longer atmospheric dread — now it's direct. The dead aren't waiting anymore. They're reaching. The well isn't in %OLD_VILLAGE% — it's everywhere. Under their beds, in the floor, in the centre of %NEW_VILLAGE%. The water is already at their ankles.
- **%BUTCHER%'s dreams don't stop — they get worse.** Killing %HAG% doesn't satisfy the compulsion. It feeds it. Like an addiction where every hit makes the next craving worse. He becomes more erratic, more violent, more drawn to the well. The man who was cold and methodical is becoming something rawer.
- **Other villagers start dreaming.** People who never had the dreams before — %CIOTKA%, %WIFE%, %WUJAS%, even %WOJEWODA%. The well's reach has expanded. The whole village feels it, even if they don't understand what changed.

### The Village Unravels

The mood shifts overnight. People are irritable, frightened, sleep-deprived. Arguments flare. The drinking gets worse. %WUJAS% has a breakdown. %CIOTKA% won't let %GLUPEK% out of her sight. The rain feels personal now — not weather, but intent.

The villagers who avoided %OLD_VILLAGE% out of superstition now avoid it out of something closer to terror. They can feel it. Something changed. Something got worse.

### The Investigation Accelerates

For the players, the shift is unmistakable. The dreams were bad before. Now they're screaming. Whatever was in the well is no longer contained — and the players don't know why. If they go back to %OLD_VILLAGE%, they'll find:

- %HAG%'s ritual supplies scattered — candles, incense, bread, icons
- Signs of violence — blood on the well's rim, disturbed earth
- %HAG% is gone. Her cabin in the forest is empty. Her rites have stopped.
- The well feels different. Worse. Open. Hungry.

Connecting %HAG%'s disappearance to %BUTCHER% requires investigation — but his sudden calm, his clean sleep, his dogs' changed behaviour are all tells.

## Timing

This should happen at the story's tipping point — likely **day 3 or early day 4** — late enough that the players have met %HAG% (or at least know about her), early enough that the aftermath reshapes the final days. The exact trigger depends on when %HAG% appears openly and when %BUTCHER%'s escalation brings him to %OLD_VILLAGE% at the same time.

**If the players have already spoken to %HAG%**, her death is devastating — they've lost a key witness and the only person who understood the well.

**If the players haven't found her yet**, they arrive at %OLD_VILLAGE% to find evidence of someone who was there — rites, supplies, blood — and a mystery they're already too late to solve.

## Consequences for Endgame

- **The well is now uncontained.** The final days play out under escalating supernatural pressure — dreams, mood, the village cracking.
- **%BUTCHER% is accelerating.** The most dangerous man in the village is getting worse in lockstep with the water level. Everyone deteriorates — but he deteriorates at the well's pace. This synchronisation is a major clue.
- **A new body in the well.** If the players eventually examine the well, they'll find recent remains alongside decades-old ones. %HAG%'s death is fresh evidence of ongoing murder — not a 13-year-old cold case.
- **The rites can't be resumed.** Nobody else knows them. Nobody else has the knowledge, the language, the 20 years of relationship with the dead. What %HAG% was doing is gone forever.
- **%BUTCHER% killed again — and the compulsion is hungrier.** He fed the well, and now it wants more. The compulsion isn't satisfied — it's escalating. The players are now on a clock — not just the flood, but %BUTCHER% emboldened by a fresh kill and driven by a craving that's only getting worse.

## For the GM

This is the story's fulcrum. Before this event, the investigation is tense but manageable — secrets, lies, red herrings, slow pressure. After this event, something has broken at a level the players can *feel*. The atmosphere shifts from mystery to dread. The well is no longer a symbol. It's a force.

Never show the killing directly — the players find the aftermath. The horror is in the reconstruction: the scattered rites, the blood, the empty cabin, and one man in the village who's getting worse faster than anyone else.
