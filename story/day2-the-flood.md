# Day 2 — The Flood

**Type:** Story event — inciting crisis

## What Happens

por. Witold Skowron was supposed to return on day 2 to check on the committee. He doesn't show up. The rain that's been falling since the players arrived intensifies overnight into a **massive downpour**. By morning, the road out of %NEW_VILLAGE% is gone — washed out, submerged, impassable.

The committee is **stranded**. No car, no road, no backup. The only phone is in Zbigniew Gajda's office.

## What the Players Experience

- They wake to the sound of rain hammering the roof — louder than yesterday, and it was loud yesterday.
- The road they drove in on is underwater. Mud everywhere. The landscape is transforming.
- por. Witold Skowron doesn't arrive. No word. No explanation.
- The villagers take it in stride — floods happen here. But the players are outsiders. They've never been cut off like this.
- Zbigniew Gajda is calm. Helpful. "These things happen. The road will clear in a few days. You can continue your work."

## What It Means

- The committee's **state authority dissolves**. Their mandate means nothing without the state behind them. They're just strangers now.
- **Zbigniew Gajda becomes the highest authority.** He's the sołtys, this is his village, and nobody's coming to overrule him.
- **The phone is the lifeline.** If the players want to contact por. Witold Skowron, they need Zbigniew Gajda's permission — or they need to get into his office without it.
- **The village dynamic shifts.** The villagers were cooperative when the committee represented compensation and state power. Now? These are just mouths to feed during a flood.
- **The water is rising everywhere** — the river, the groundwater, the well in %OLD_VILLAGE%. The 5-day clock is now a survival clock too.

## Zbigniew Gajda's Reaction

Outwardly: concerned, hospitable, reassuring. "Stay as long as you need."

Inwardly: this is a gift. The committee is cut off from por. Witold Skowron. No reports going out. If they've found anything inconvenient, it stays in the village. And every day the rain continues, the well fills higher.
