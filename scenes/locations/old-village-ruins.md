# %OLD_VILLAGE% — Homestead Ruins

**Type:** Location (explorable)
**Location:** Scattered across [%OLD_VILLAGE%](old-village-ruins.md) — stone foundations and chimneys, all that remains of wooden *chyża* homes.
**Present:** Nobody.
**Available:** After reaching %OLD_VILLAGE%.
**Cost:** 1 action per area searched

## Setup

The wooden buildings were burned in 1947. Stone survived. What fire couldn't finish, twenty years of forest did.

**Current state (1967):**
- Stone foundations 1–2 feet above ground level
- Chimneys partially intact
- Interiors flooded or filled with debris
- Underground root cellars and storage pits still intact — often water-filled, dangerous to descend into
- Pottery shards, rusted tools, fragments of daily life scattered on surface
- **Overgrown orchards** still bearing apples and plums — eerie witness to human abandonment

**Hazards:**
- Hidden cellar holes
- Unstable stones
- Flooding in cellars

## Opportunities

- **Fragments of daily life** — pottery, tools, household items. A village that was living one day and dead the next.
- **Fruit trees** — still producing after twenty years. Nature doesn't know the people are gone.
- **Root cellars** — if players descend (dangerous), may contain preserved items from 1947
- **Scale** — counting foundations gives village size. Cross-reference with [cemetery](old-village-cemetery.md) graves: the numbers don't match.
- **Burn marks** — evidence the destruction was deliberate, not natural decay. → Seeds [old-village-was-burned](../../clues/clues.md#old-village-was-burned)

## GM Notes

The ruins are atmosphere and scale. They tell players how big this village was — and how completely it was erased. The fruit trees are the detail that stays with people: apple blossoms in a ghost town.

Also includes remains of **storage/utility buildings** (possibly mill, community house) and **animal pens** (post holes, deteriorated wood, scattered animal bones). These are primarily atmospheric — little of investigative value remains.
