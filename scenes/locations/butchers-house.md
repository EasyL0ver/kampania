# Butcher's House

**Type:** Location (revisitable)
**Location:** %NEW_VILLAGE% — edge of village, closest house to the forest path toward [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md).
**Present:** [Stanisław Rezeń](../../characters/butcher.md) (variable — often gone), dogs (always — 3 lean mutts)
**Available:** Any time — dogs are a barrier.
**Cost:** 1 action per visit

## Setup

Last house before the trees. Dark timber, no paint, no curtains. Maintained but joyless.

Out back: butchering station. Block scarred deep, iron hooks on a crossbar, drainage channel stained black. Behind it — a dirt mound, dog-sized. Too careful for rubbish. The other dogs won't go near it. He used to have five dogs. Now three. Nobody asked.

The dogs don't bark. Low growls, bodies low. One limps. Another flinches when a hand rises too fast. These aren't guard dogs. These are animals that have learned to be still around their owner.

Rezeń, if home, appears at the door before being knocked on. Cigarette in mouth — always, lighting the next off the last. Fingers yellow-brown, doorframe burned in a dozen spots. He fidgets while talking — picks at a scab on his forearm, scratches himself, turns a small knife over and over in his free hand. A body that can't be still. His friendliness is slightly off — too eager, watches faces too closely, smiles at things that aren't funny.

Worn footpath from behind the house into the trees. Mud always fresh.

## Opportunities

- **The path** — beaten track into the forest. Leads to [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md) and [the well](../../story-facts/the-well.md). The most direct route in the game. → Seeds [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well)
- **The dogs' perimeter** — they follow him to the tree line and stop. Won't cross. The man goes where the animals refuse to follow. → Seeds [dogs-wont-cross](../../clues/clues.md#dogs-wont-cross)
- **No bottles** — no alcohol anywhere. Stopped cold in 1954. Why that year? → Seeds suspicion about 1954
- **Sleep deprivation** — dark eyes, slow movements. Floorboards near the window worn smooth in an oval — pacing, every night, for years. Neighbours hear him thrashing. → Seeds the well's dreams
- **The butchering schedule** — block used more often than livestock explains. Villagers who bring him a pig say he takes too long. Works behind closed doors. Alone. Returns the meat perfectly cut. Nobody watches him work anymore. → Horror atmosphere
- **The dogs** — flinching, limping, freezing when he reaches near them. → This man practices cruelty on living things
- **The mound** — dog-sized, out back. Remaining dogs avoid it. *"Didn't he used to have more dogs?"* Nobody answers. → Atmosphere

## Actions

### Census visit — interview Stanisław
- **Requires:** Committee authority
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **Outcome:** Answers on the doorstep. Won't let them inside. Name, occupation, duration — all correct, all useless. He asks questions back: where are they staying, how long, what does the census need? He's mapping them. Players who notice will be unsettled. Players who don't will think he's unfriendly.
  - Standing close, players smell him. Smoke, iron, dog. And underneath: damp earth, something sour.
- **Leads to:** Dead end for information. He doesn't mention the Barnaś family. Doesn't react to the name.

### Observe from a distance
- **Requires:** Time and patience.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Outcome:** Leaves at dawn or dusk, walks the forest path, returns hours later. Sometimes at night, half-dressed, barefoot. Dogs watch him go and don't follow. Comes back with mud on his hands, knuckles scraped. The routine has been going on for years. Villagers have stopped noticing.
- **Leads to:** → [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well). Following him is a separate action and a risk.

### Follow him into the forest
- **Requires:** He's left the house.
- **Cost:** 1 action (and real danger)
- **Outcome:** Path worn into the ground. Past where his dogs stopped. Into [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md). He goes to the well. Sits on the rim. Clears debris from the mouth — branches, dirt. Leans over the edge and listens. If he notices the players, he tells them to leave. Once. Flat voice. The dogs are behind them on the path.
- **Leads to:** → [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well), [butcher-compelled-to-feed](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-compelled-to-feed). Most direct path to the well. Being caught following him is dangerous.

### Enter while he's gone
- **Requires:** Stanisław confirmed away. Dogs growl but don't attack without him.
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **⚠️ LETHAL RISK:** If he returns while players are inside, he will kill them. Dogs go silent — that's the only warning. **GM must track his absence.** Players should feel the clock.
- **Outcome:** Door isn't locked.

  **The room.** One room. Bench-bed, stove, hooks on every beam. Low ceiling. Cold air. Floor is beaten earth and rough planks. The planks near the window are worn smooth — pacing path.

  **The tools.** Knives, cleavers, bone saw, skinning blades, hooks — perfect row on the wall. Some are wrong. Blades filed into shapes no butchering manual teaches. Homemade modifications, patient metalwork. Among them: **a military-issue knife**, KBW-stamped handle. He took it from [Edward Barnaś](../../characters/soldier.md) the night of the lynch. Uses it daily. Players who've found the [KBW documents](ciotkas-house.md) will recognise it.

  **Under the stove.** Wooden box. Animal bones — not scraps. Arranged. Small skulls, jawbones, vertebrae in rows. Decorative knife marks. Practice cuts. Some skulls are dog-sized.

  **The bed.** Bench with a blanket, twisted and damp. Body-shaped impression in the mattress. Scratch marks on the wall beside it — fingernail grooves in timber.

  **The drawer** ⚠️. Shallow, in the bench frame. A girl's undergarments. Folded. Kept. No woman has lived here. He took them from [Hania Barnaś](../../characters/jagna.md) the night he raped her. He keeps them because they arouse him. **Content warning** — GM should gauge the table. Can be softened to a ribbon or necklace.

  **The smell.** Old blood. More than animal butchering explains. Ground into the floorboards and walls.

  **The silence.** Dogs outside have gone quiet. Settled, or coming back? Every moment inside costs time.

- **Leads to:** Knife proves connection to the Soldier. Drawer proves what he did to the girl. → Seeds [butcher-compelled-to-feed](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-compelled-to-feed)

### Confront about the well
- **Requires:** Players know about the well
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **Outcome:** Doesn't deny it. Doesn't explain. *"I go where I want."* If players push about **1954** — something shifts. Not fear. *Interest.* The dogs stand. *"You should leave."* Last civil thing he'll say.
- **Leads to:** This man is dangerous, aware, and under no one's control.

## GM Notes

**This is the horror location.** The well is abstract. This house is where evil lives and sharpens its knives. Make players uncomfortable — smells, silence, time pressure.

**The dogs are a barometer.** Early game: growl at strangers. Mid-game: pace, whine, won't sleep. Late game: afraid of him. When the dogs leave the property — it's almost too late.

**The path is the most useful discovery.** Leads directly to %OLD_VILLAGE% and the well. Bypasses the forest search entirely.

**Don't let players fight him.** They will lose. If caught in his house, the scene is survival. If they escape, he considers them enemies. He is patient.

**The containment arc:** Early — away, at the well, disciplined. Mid — showing up in village, pleasant, wrong. Late — discipline breaks, sharpened knives, nothing holding him back.
