# Butcher's House

**Type:** Location (revisitable)
**Location:** %NEW_VILLAGE% — edge of the village, set apart. Closest house to the forest path toward [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md). Nobody between him and the trees. The village ends here. Something else begins.
**Present:** [Stanisław Rezeń](../../characters/butcher.md) (variable — often gone to forest), dogs (always — 3-4 large, lean mutts)
**Available:** Any time — but the dogs are a physical barrier. Approaching uninvited is a statement.
**Cost:** 1 action per visit

## Setup

The last house before the trees. Not abandoned — worse. *Maintained.* Dark timber oiled against rot, roof patched with military precision, no paint, no flowers, no curtain in any window. Every window is a black rectangle. Even in daylight, the interior absorbs light.

Out back: the butchering station. A heavy wooden block scarred so deeply the cuts have cuts. Iron hooks on a crossbar — too many for one man's work. A drainage channel gouged into the dirt, stained so dark it looks like the earth itself is bruised. The smell hits players before they see it: old blood, smoke, dog, and underneath — something mineral. Stone. Standing water. A smell that doesn't belong to any animal.

The dogs notice players before they reach the fence. They don't bark. Low growls, lips pulled back, bodies low to the ground. Not aggression — *assessment*. Three or four lean mutts with the eyes of animals that have learned to read danger from the thing that feeds them. Players who've met [Edek](../../characters/glupek.md) know what these dogs sound like from inside Ciotka's house — the sound that makes a six-foot man hide behind a stove.

Stanisław Rezeń, if home, appears at the door before being knocked on. He doesn't invite players in. Conversation happens on the threshold, him leaning on the frame, blocking the interior with his body. He fills the doorway. Behind him: darkness, and the faint smell of well-water. Polite enough. Short answers. He has the patience of a man who's been waiting for someone to come asking — and has already decided what happens when they do.

A worn footpath runs from behind the house into the trees — not toward the village, toward the forest. The mud is always fresh. No grass grows on it anymore.

## Opportunities

- **The path** — a beaten track from his back door straight into the forest. Used daily, for years. The earth is packed so hard it's almost stone. Players who follow it will find it leads to [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md) and [the well](../../story-facts/the-well.md). The most direct route in the game — because he's walked it a thousand times. → Seeds [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well)
- **The dogs' perimeter** — watch the dogs when he leaves for the forest. They follow him to the tree line and stop. Won't cross. Pace and whine at the boundary like animals at the edge of a fire. The man goes where the animals refuse to follow. → Seeds [dogs-wont-cross](../../clues/clues.md#dogs-wont-cross)
- **No bottles** — in a village where everyone drinks, this house has no alcohol. Not hidden — absent. No empties, no stash, no bimber smell. A man who stopped cold in 1954 and never started again. Why that year? → Seeds suspicion about 1954
- **Sleep deprivation** — dark eyes, slow movements, the face of a man who hasn't slept properly in years. The floorboards near the window are visible through the glass — worn smooth in an oval pattern. Pacing. Thirteen years of pacing. The dogs whine at night because they hear him thrashing — neighbours at the village edge have heard it too. → Atmosphere, seeds the well's dreams
- **The butchering schedule** — he butchers animals for the village. Regular work. But the block out back is used more often than any livestock schedule explains. What is he cutting when there's nothing to cut? The drainage channel runs with water some mornings when no animal was slaughtered the night before. → Horror atmosphere

## Actions

### Census visit — interview Stanisław
- **Requires:** Committee authority (and nerve)
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **Outcome:** He answers on the doorstep. Won't let them inside. Name: Stanisław Rezeń. Occupation: butcher, odd jobs. Family: none. Previous residence: *"came with the settlers."* That's the cover story — he's actually from a neighbouring village and was here before the settlers arrived. Duration: since 1950. Every answer is factually close enough and tells players nothing. No lies to catch because he offers nothing to lie about. But he's not passive — he's studying them. Asks questions back: where are they staying, how long, what exactly does the census need? He's mapping their presence the way a predator maps movement. Players who recognise this will be unsettled. Players who don't will think he's just unfriendly.
  - **The smell** — standing this close, players can smell him. Smoke, iron, dog. And underneath: damp earth. Cold air. He smells like a cellar that hasn't been opened in years.
- **Leads to:** Dead end for information — but the interaction itself is a clue. This man is assessing threat, not answering questions. He doesn't mention the Barnaś family. He doesn't react to the name. The absence is the evidence.

### Observe from a distance
- **Requires:** Time and patience. Don't approach — watch.
- **Cost:** Free (can be done while visiting nearby locations)
- **Outcome:** His routine: leaves at dawn or dusk, walks the forest path, returns hours later. Sometimes at night — half-dressed, barefoot, moving like a sleepwalker. The dogs watch him go and don't follow. He comes back with mud on his hands. Some mornings his hands are raw, knuckles scraped, nails black with dirt — he's been digging. At what? The routine has been going on for years — villagers who notice have stopped noticing. That's the horror. They've normalised this.
- **Leads to:** → [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well). Following him is a separate action — and a risk.

### Follow him into the forest
- **Requires:** He's left the house. Players must be willing to follow a dangerous man into the woods at night.
- **Cost:** 1 action (and real danger)
- **Outcome:** The path is clear — he's worn it into the ground over twenty years. Through the trees, past the point where his dogs stopped and stood whining. Into [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md). He goes to the well. He sits on the rim. Sometimes he clears debris — branches, dirt, anything blocking the mouth. He works quietly, methodically, like a man tending a grave. Or feeding something. He leans over the edge and listens. If he notices the players — the calm doesn't break. It *focuses*. He stands slowly. He tells them to leave. Once. His voice is flat and final. The dogs are behind them on the path.
- **Leads to:** Players witness the well's pull firsthand. → [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well), [butcher-compelled-to-feed](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-compelled-to-feed). Also the most direct path to finding [the well](../../story-facts/the-well.md) and [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md). But being caught following him is dangerous — he now sees the players as a problem. And he is patient.

### Enter while he's gone
- **Requires:** Stanisław confirmed away (at the well, doing farm work). Dogs will growl but don't attack if players are calm and slow — they're confused without him, not loyal to the house.
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **⚠️ LETHAL RISK:** If Stanisław returns while players are inside, he will kill them. Not threaten. Not confront. Not ask questions. The dogs go silent — that's the only warning. The silence means he's close. A man who tried to strangle an infant will not negotiate with intruders in his home. **GM must track his absence carefully.** Players should feel the clock with every sentence of description. Describe the light changing. The dogs shifting. A sound in the trees that might be wind.
- **Outcome:** The door isn't locked. It opens inward with a sound like a held breath releasing.

  **The room.** One room. Bench-bed against the wall, stove in the corner, hooks on every beam. The ceiling is low — players will stoop. The air is cold. Colder than outside, even in summer. The walls seem to hold the chill the way a cave does. The floor is beaten earth in some places, rough planks in others. The planks near the window are worn smooth in an oval — the pacing path. Thirteen years of the same circuit, every night, until the wood itself has been polished by bare feet.

  **The tools.** Butchering tools hang on the wall in a perfect row — knives, cleavers, a bone saw, skinning blades, hooks. Cleaned and oiled with the care of a man who loves his instruments. Each one has a place. Each place has a shadow where the tool hangs. Among them: **a military-issue knife** — different steel, different make, KBW-stamped handle. It doesn't belong in this row. It belongs in a soldier's belt. He took it from [Edward Barnaś](../../characters/soldier.md) — the man he'd served alongside in 1947, the man he killed in 1954 — because it was a good knife. He uses it daily. Players who've found the [KBW service documents](ciotkas-house.md) in Ciotka's backyard will recognise the type. A dead soldier's knife hanging in the row like it always belonged there.

  **The bed.** A bench with a blanket, twisted and damp with sweat. The shape of a body is pressed into the thin mattress — the same position every night, curled, facing the wall. Scratch marks on the wall beside it — fingernail grooves in the timber. Not words. Not counting. Just the marks of a man clawing at something in his sleep.

  **The drawer** ⚠️. A shallow drawer built into the bench-bed frame. Inside: a girl's undergarments. Folded. Kept. No woman has ever lived in this house. No daughter, no wife, no visitor in 13 years. He took them from [Hania Barnaś](../../characters/jagna.md) the night he raped her. He keeps them because they arouse him. That's it. No symbolism, no ritual, no deeper meaning. A deviant's keepsake. **Content warning** — GM should gauge the table before introducing this detail. It can be softened to a personal item (ribbon, necklace) if the group needs it.

  **The smell.** It's been building since they entered but now it's undeniable. Beneath the smoke and dog and iron — old blood. More old blood than any amount of animal butchering explains. Ground into the floorboards, into the bench frame, into the walls. The house smells like the butchering station out back — because to him, there's no difference between the two.

  **The silence.** No clock. No creaking. The dogs outside have gone quiet. Is that because they've settled — or because he's coming back? GM: let the players decide how long they stay. Every moment inside is a choice. Every detail they examine costs time they might not have.

- **Leads to:** The knife proves the connection to the Soldier. The drawer proves what he did to the girl. The scratch marks and the pacing groove prove something is eating him alive — guilt or compulsion, players decide. → Seeds [butcher-compelled-to-feed](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-compelled-to-feed)

### Confront about the well
- **Requires:** Players know about the well, have seen him there or know he visits
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **Outcome:** He doesn't deny it. He also doesn't explain. No confusion, no vulnerability — cold assessment. He's deciding whether these people are a threat worth the mess. *"I go where I want."* If players push, he gives them nothing — the patience of a man who knows exactly what he's done and made peace with it the way you make peace with breathing. Not a decision. A fact.
  - If players push about **1954** specifically — something shifts. Not fear. Not guilt. *Interest*. Like they've said something in a language he didn't think anyone else spoke. The dogs stand. *"You should leave."* This isn't a warning. It's the last civil thing he'll say. What comes after isn't anger. It's the same calm he brought to the well in 1954 and 1947. The calm of a man doing what he's good at.
- **Leads to:** Players understand this man is dangerous, aware, and under no one's control but his own. The well's influence is there — but HE doesn't see it as a problem. The compulsion feels like choice to him. That's what makes it terrifying.

## GM Notes

**This is the horror location.** Not the well, not the old village — this house. The well is abstract. The old village is ruins. This house is where the evil *lives*, eats, sleeps, and sharpens its knives. Players should feel physically uncomfortable here. Describe temperature drops. Describe smells. Describe the silence getting heavier. Make them aware of time passing.

**The dogs are a barometer.** Early game: they guard the house, growl at strangers, stay close. Mid-game: they pace, whine, refuse to sleep. Late game: they won't follow Stanisław into the village anymore — the containment has reversed. The dogs used to guard his territory. Now they're afraid of him. When the dogs leave the property entirely — it's almost too late.

**The path is the most useful discovery here.** It leads directly to %OLD_VILLAGE% and the well. Players who find it bypass the forest-search entirely. The Butcher has blazed the trail for them — twenty years of footsteps.

**Don't let players fight him.** Not because it's unfair — because they will lose. He's the strongest man in the village, armed, experienced with killing, has dogs, and feels nothing about it. If players are caught in his house, the scene is survival, not combat. The only question is whether they get out before the door opens. If they do, he now considers them enemies — and he is patient. He has been patient for twenty years.

**The containment arc:** Early game — he's away, at the well, contained by his own practical discipline. Mid-game — he starts appearing in the village, calm, pleasant, *wrong*. Late game — the dogs won't follow, the discipline breaks, and the most dangerous man in the village is walking among people with sharpened knives and nothing holding him back. His house emptying IS the danger signal.
