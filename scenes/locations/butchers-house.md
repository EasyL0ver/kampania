# Butcher's House

**Type:** Location (revisitable)
**Location:** %NEW_VILLAGE% — edge of the village, set apart. Closest house to the forest path toward [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md). Nobody between him and the trees.
**Present:** [Stanisław Rezeń](../../characters/butcher.md) (variable — often gone to forest), dogs (always — 3-4 large, lean mutts)
**Available:** Any time — but the dogs are a physical barrier. Approaching uninvited is a statement.
**Cost:** 1 action per visit

## Setup

The worst house in the village. Not ruined — maintained with cold efficiency, like a man maintaining himself. Dark timber, no paint, no flowers, no curtain in the window. A butchering station out back — wooden block, hooks, drainage channel in the dirt. Smells of dogs, smoke, and old blood.

The dogs notice players before they reach the fence. Low growls, not barking — trained or instinctive. They don't attack without cause. They assess. Players who've met Edek know what these dogs sound like from the inside of Ciotka's house — the sound that makes him hide.

Stanisław Rezeń, if home, appears at the door before being knocked on. He doesn't invite players in. Conversation happens on the threshold, him leaning on the frame, blocking the interior with his body. Polite enough. Short answers. He has the patience of a man who's spent 13 years waiting for someone to come asking.

A worn footpath runs from behind the house into the trees — not toward the village, toward the forest. The mud is always fresh.

## Opportunities

- **The path** — a beaten track from his back door straight into the forest. Used daily. Players who follow it will find it leads to [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md) and [the well](../../story-facts/the-well.md). The most direct route in the game — because he's walked it a thousand times. → Seeds [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well)
- **The dogs' perimeter** — watch the dogs when he leaves for the forest. They follow him to the tree line and stop. Won't enter the forest. Pace and whine at the boundary. The man goes where the animals refuse to follow. → Seeds [dogs-wont-cross](../../clues/clues.md#dogs-wont-cross)
- **No bottles** — in a village where everyone drinks, this house has no alcohol. Not hidden — absent. No empties, no stash, no bimber smell. A man who stopped cold in 1954 and never started again. Why that year? → Seeds suspicion about 1954
- **The stones** — small grey stones on the windowsill. In a jar by the stove. In his coat pockets. River-smooth, well-worn. Players won't recognise them until they've seen the well — then they'll know. He's been bringing pieces of it home. Not as trophies. He doesn't know why he picks them up. → Seeds [butcher-compelled-to-feed](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-compelled-to-feed)
- **Sleep deprivation** — dark eyes, slow movements. The bed, if players see the interior, is barely used — a blanket on a bench, twisted and damp. The floorboards near the window are worn smooth from pacing. The dogs whine at night because they hear him thrashing. → Atmosphere, seeds the well's dreams

## Actions

### Census visit — interview Stanisław
- **Requires:** Committee authority (and nerve)
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **Outcome:** He answers on the doorstep. Won't let them inside. Name: Stanisław Rezeń. Occupation: butcher, odd jobs. Family: none. Previous residence: came with the settlers. Duration: since 1950. Every answer is factually correct and tells players nothing. No lies to catch because he offers nothing to lie about. But he's not passive — he's studying them. Asks questions back: where are they staying, how long, what exactly does the census need? He's mapping their presence the way a predator maps movement. Players who recognise this will be unsettled. Players who don't will think he's just unfriendly.
- **Leads to:** Dead end for information — but the interaction itself is a clue. This man is assessing threat, not answering questions. He doesn't mention the Barnaś family. He doesn't react to the name. The absence is the evidence.

### Observe from a distance
- **Requires:** Time and patience. Don't approach — watch.
- **Cost:** Free (can be done while visiting nearby locations)
- **Outcome:** His routine: leaves at dawn or dusk, walks the forest path, returns hours later. Sometimes at night — half-dressed, barefoot, moving like a sleepwalker. The dogs watch him go and don't follow. He comes back with mud on his hands and stones in his pockets. The routine has been going on for years — villagers who notice have stopped noticing.
- **Leads to:** → [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well). Following him is a separate action — and a risk.

### Follow him into the forest
- **Requires:** He's left the house. Players must be willing to follow a dangerous man into the woods at night.
- **Cost:** 1 action (and real danger)
- **Outcome:** The path is clear — he's worn it into the ground. Through the trees, past the point where his dogs stopped. Into [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md). He goes to the well. He sits. Sometimes he clears debris — stones, fallen branches, anything blocking the mouth. He works quietly, methodically. This isn't confusion — it's ownership. The well is his. He maintains it the way he maintains his knives. If he notices the players — the calm evaporates. He doesn't ask why they're there. He tells them to leave. Once. The dogs are behind them on the path.
- **Leads to:** Players witness the well's pull firsthand. → [butcher-visits-the-well](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-visits-the-well), [butcher-compelled-to-feed](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-compelled-to-feed). Also the most direct path to finding [the well](../../story-facts/the-well.md) and [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../../places/old-village.md). But being caught following him is dangerous — he now sees the players as a problem.

### Enter while he's gone
- **Requires:** Stanisław confirmed away (at the well, doing farm work). Dogs will growl but don't attack if players are calm and slow.
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **⚠️ LETHAL RISK:** If Stanisław returns while players are inside, he will kill them. Not threaten. Not confront. The dogs go silent — that's the only warning. A man who strangled an infant without hesitation will not negotiate with intruders in his home. **GM must track his absence carefully.** Players should feel the clock.
- **Outcome:** Spartan interior. One room, bench-bed, stove, hooks on the wall. Butchering tools cleaned and oiled — knives, cleavers, a bone saw. Among them: **a military-issue knife** ⚠️ — different steel, different make, KBW-stamped handle. Better than anything a village butcher would buy. He took it from [Edward Barnaś](../../characters/soldier.md) the night of the lynch because it was a good knife. Uses it daily. Players who've found the [KBW service documents](ciotkas-house.md) in Ciotka's backyard will recognise the type — a soldier's knife in a butcher's tool rack. The windowsill stones. A worn patch of floor under the window where he paces at night. No photographs, no letters, no personal effects. A life stripped to function.
  - **Under the bench:** a pair of boots caked in grey-white mud that doesn't match anything near the village. Well-bottom mud. He's been going deeper than standing at the rim.
  - **Smell:** beneath the smoke and dog — something faint. Stone and standing water. The house smells like the well when the air is still.
- **Leads to:** The boots are physical evidence he's been IN the well, not just near it. The smell is atmospheric but unnerving — the well is seeping into his home. → Seeds [butcher-compelled-to-feed](../../clues/clues.md#butcher-compelled-to-feed)

### Confront about the well
- **Requires:** Players know about the well, have seen him there or know he visits
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **Outcome:** He doesn't deny it. He also doesn't explain. No confusion, no vulnerability — cold assessment. He's deciding whether these people are a threat. *"I go where I want."* If players push, he gives them nothing — the patience of a man who knows exactly what he's done and has had 13 years to make peace with it. He's not haunted by guilt. He's managed by discipline.
  - If players push about **1954** specifically — something shifts. Not fear. Recognition. He knows they know. The dogs stand. *"You should leave."* This isn't a warning. It's the last civil thing he'll say.
- **Leads to:** Players understand this man is dangerous, aware, and under no one's control but his own. The well's influence is there — but HE doesn't see it as a problem. The compulsion feels like choice to him. That's what makes it terrifying.

## GM Notes

**This house is a warning.** Everything about it says *stay away* — the dogs, the isolation, the man himself. Players who come here are choosing danger. Reward them with atmosphere and evidence, not easy answers.

**The dogs are a barometer.** Early game: they guard the house, growl at strangers, stay close. Mid-game: they pace, whine, refuse to sleep. Late game: they won't follow Stanisław into the village anymore — the containment has reversed. The dogs used to guard his territory. Now they're afraid of him.

**The stones are the subtlest clue in the house.** Grey well-stones on the windowsill mean nothing until players have been to the well. Then: he's been taking pieces of it home for years. Like feeding in reverse — or nesting.

**The path is the most useful discovery here.** It leads directly to %OLD_VILLAGE% and the well. Players who find it bypass the forest-search entirely. The Butcher has blazed the trail for them.

**Don't let players fight him.** Not because it's unfair — because they will lose. He's the strongest man in the village, armed, has dogs, and has killed before without hesitation. If players are caught in his house, the scene is survival, not combat. The only question is whether they get out. If they do, he now considers them enemies — and he is patient.

**The containment arc:** Early game — he's away, at the well, contained by his own discipline. Mid-game — he starts appearing in the village, calm, pleasant, wrong. Late game — the dogs won't follow, the discipline breaks, and the most dangerous man in the village is walking among people with sharpened knives and nothing holding him back. His house emptying IS the danger signal.
