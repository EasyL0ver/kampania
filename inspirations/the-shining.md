# The Shining — Stanley Kubrick (1980)

**Type:** Atmosphere / isolation inspiration (minus the supernatural)

## What to Take

- **Isolation breeds madness.** The Overlook Hotel = %NEW_VILLAGE% cut off by rain and mud. Nowhere to go, walls closing in.
- **Architecture of dread.** Empty corridors, locked rooms, spaces that feel wrong. The abandoned buildings of %OLD_VILLAGE%, %SOLDIER%'s house where %CIOTKA% now lives, the well.
- **The past bleeds through.** The hotel has ghosts — our village has history seeping out of every wall, every overgrown path, every Cyrillic gravestone.
- **Slow psychological deterioration.** Not a sudden break — a gradual unraveling as pressure mounts. The rain doesn't stop. The water is rising. People start cracking.
- **The caretaker who was always there.** Jack "has always been the caretaker." %SOLDIER% came back to the place of his crime. %BUTCHER% still lives on the edge. The village can't escape its own past.
- **The child who sees.** Danny sees what adults can't or won't. %GLUPEK% — brain-damaged, non-verbal, but *present*. Does he sense things? React to places? Flinch near certain people?
