# Dogville — Lars von Trier (2003)

**Type:** Social dynamics / collective cruelty inspiration

## What to Take

- **A small community reveals its true nature.** Behind the politeness and routine lies exploitation, cruelty, and self-justification. %NEW_VILLAGE% is Dogville — everyone is complicit, everyone has a reason.
- **The outsider as catalyst.** Grace arrives and the village shows what it really is. The committee (players) arrives and the same thing happens.
- **Incremental evil.** Nobody starts as a monster. Each step is small, reasonable, justified. The beating was deserved. The rape was drunken. The murder was necessary. The cover-up was practical. Each person only did *their* small part.
- **Economic power as control.** Grace becomes trapped through dependency. %BARBARA% works %WOJEWODA%'s farm, lives in his house, sleeps with his family. She's Dogville's Grace — dependent, used, silenced.
- **Collective guilt disguised as community.** The village acts as one organism. Everyone knows. Nobody speaks. The silence isn't an agreement — it's the structure.
- **The transparency of evil.** Dogville's set has no walls — the audience sees everything. In our story, the players slowly realize the walls between secrets are just as thin. Everyone can see through everyone else. They just choose not to look.
- **No mercy in the ending.** Dogville doesn't forgive. Our story shouldn't either.
