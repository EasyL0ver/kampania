# Dogville — Lars von Trier (2003)

**Type:** Social dynamics / collective cruelty inspiration

## Overview

A woman on the run, Grace, takes refuge in a tiny Rocky Mountain town. The townsfolk agree to hide her — at first out of goodness, then for favors, then for exploitation. What starts as charity curdles into abuse, rape, and slavery. The entire community is complicit. The film is staged on a bare set with no walls — the audience sees everything the characters pretend not to.

## Atmosphere & Themes

- **Incremental evil.** Nobody starts as a monster. Each step is small, reasonable, justified. Each person only did *their* small part.
- **Economic power as control.** Grace becomes trapped through dependency — labor, housing, the goodwill of people who own her survival.
- **Collective guilt disguised as community.** The village acts as one organism. Everyone knows. Nobody speaks. The silence isn't an agreement — it's the structure.
- **The outsider as catalyst.** An arrival from outside forces the community to reveal what it really is.
- **The transparency of evil.** The set has no walls — the audience sees everything. The characters choose not to look.
- **No mercy in the ending.** Dogville doesn't forgive.
