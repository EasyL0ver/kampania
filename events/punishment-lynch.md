# The Punishment Ending — Ryszard Dudka's Lynch

**Type:** Story ending — one of the possible resolutions

## What It Is

Ryszard Dudka has had enough. Thirteen years of silence, thirteen years of hearing through the walls, thirteen years of watching Janina Gajda raise a brain-damaged boy while the people who did it walk free. The flood, the well, the dreams — it all breaks him. And when it breaks, it doesn't come out as confession. It comes out as rage.

**Ryszard Dudka organises a mob and the village lynches whoever it blames.**

## Who Goes Into the Well

This depends entirely on the game state — what the players have uncovered, who they've exposed, who the village believes is guilty, and who Ryszard Dudka points at.

**Any NPC can end up in the well. Any player can too.**

Possible targets:
- **The perpetrators** — Zbigniew Gajda, Tadek Gajda, Helena Rzepka, Stanisław Rezeń. If the truth is out and the village knows who did it, the mob goes for them. Poetic justice — the killers end up where they put their victims.
- **The wrong people** — If the truth is only half-out, the mob goes for whoever looks guilty. Marek Gajda (the false suspect with a violence record). Barbara Kopacz (the outsider nobody trusts). Paraskewia Chyłak (the witch in the forest). The village that lynched an innocent family once can do it again.
- **The players** — If the committee pushed too hard, exposed too much, and broke the village open without building any allies — Ryszard Dudka's rage doesn't distinguish between perpetrators and catalysts. The people who brought the truth are the people who destroyed the peace. Into the well.

## How It Happens

Ryszard Dudka is the easiest NPC to crack — and the most dangerous when cracked. His guilt is about inaction. When the well's influence turns that guilt into something unbearable, his response isn't confession. It's **overcorrection**. He will *act* this time. He will not lie in bed and listen.

He doesn't need much to start it:
- A few men from Tadek Gajda's drinking circle — already angry, already drunk, already looking for someone to blame for the flood.
- A target — anyone Ryszard Dudka can point at and say *they did this*.
- A night with no road out and no phone working.

It's the same mechanics as 1954. Drunk men. Darkness. No witnesses. The village has done this before. The muscle memory is still there.

## What the Players Did Wrong

The punishment ending happens when the players **uncover truth without building structure.** They exposed the lynch but didn't secure allies. They broke Tadek Gajda but didn't protect him. They told the village what happened but didn't give anyone a path forward. Truth without justice becomes vengeance.

Or — the players did nothing at all. Stayed on their census work, ignored the signs, and the well did the rest. Ryszard Dudka cracked on his own, the village boiled over on its own, and the committee is just collateral.

## The Well Gets What It Wants

Every body that goes into the well feeds it. The ritual ending (Stefania Kopacz) lays the dead to rest. The foreman ending saves the living. The punishment ending **feeds the well more dead.**

The cycle doesn't break. It repeats. New bodies in old water. The village that was built on a massacre commits another one. The well that was supposed to be a grave becomes one again.

## How the Players Can Prevent It

The lynch can be stopped. This is what separates the perfect outcome from the punishment ending — the players give the village justice without violence.

- **Crack witnesses and document everything** — Tadek Gajda, Ryszard Dudka, Janina Gajda, Emil Rzepka. Get the truth on paper. Official paper. Census paper. Government weight.
- **Use the phone** — contact por. Witold Skowron or district authorities. The information leaves the village through the wire, not through blood. Once the outside world knows, the mob has no purpose.
- **Keep Ryszard Dudka from snapping** — give him a confession path instead of a violence path. He wants to act — let him act by *testifying*, not by organising a mob. The bystander's overcorrection can be redirected.
- **Build trust with the village** — if enough people know the truth AND believe justice is coming, the mob never forms. Ryszard Dudka doesn't need to lynch anyone if he believes the committee will do its job.
- **Zbigniew Gajda helps** — he's willing to take responsibility. If the players reach him before Ryszard Dudka snaps, Zbigniew Gajda confessing publicly defuses the mob. You can't lynch a man who's already surrendered.

**If the players prevent the lynch AND complete the ritual AND support Michał Pytlak's plan — the dead are at rest, the living are saved, and the guilty face legal justice. The well is just a well. The cycle breaks.**

## Thematic Weight

The three endings form a triangle:
- **Ritual** (Stefania Kopacz) — acknowledge the dead. Healing. The spiritual answer.
- **Engineering** (Michał Pytlak) — save the living. Survival. The physical answer.
- **Punishment** (Ryszard Dudka) — destroy the guilty. Vengeance. The human answer.

Each is understandable. Each has a cost. The players choose — or fail to choose — and the village gets the ending it deserves.
