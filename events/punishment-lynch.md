# The Punishment Ending — Ryszard Dudka's Lynch

**Location:** %NEW_VILLAGE%
**Present:** [Ryszard Dudka](../characters/neighbour.md), mob (men from Tadek Gajda's drinking circle)
**Available:** Late game — after truth partially exposed but no justice structure in place

## Trigger

Ryszard Dudka has had enough. Thirteen years of silence, thirteen years of hearing through the walls, thirteen years of watching Janina Gajda raise a brain-damaged boy while the people who did it walk free. The flood, the well, the dreams — it all breaks him. And when it breaks, it doesn't come out as confession. It comes out as rage.

**Ryszard Dudka organises a mob and the village lynches whoever it blames.**

## Setup

Ryszard Dudka is the easiest NPC to crack — and the most dangerous when cracked. His guilt is about inaction. When the well's influence turns that guilt into something unbearable, his response isn't confession. It's **overcorrection**. He will *act* this time. He will not lie in bed and listen.

He doesn't need much to start it:
- A few men from Tadek Gajda's drinking circle — already angry, already drunk, already looking for someone to blame for the flood.
- A target — anyone Ryszard Dudka can point at and say *they did this*.
- A night with no road out and no phone working.

It's the same mechanics as 1954. Drunk men. Darkness. No witnesses. The village has done this before. The muscle memory is still there.

**Who goes into the well** depends entirely on the game state — what the players have uncovered, who they've exposed, who the village believes is guilty, and who Ryszard Dudka points at.

**Any NPC can end up in the well. Any player can too.**

Possible targets:
- **The perpetrators** — Zbigniew Gajda, Tadek Gajda, Helena Rzepka, Stanisław Rezeń. If the truth is out and the village knows who did it, the mob goes for them.
- **The wrong people** — If the truth is only half-out, the mob goes for whoever looks guilty. Marek Gajda (the false suspect with a violence record). Barbara Kopacz (the outsider nobody trusts). Paraskewia Chyłak (the witch in the forest).
- **The players** — If the committee pushed too hard, exposed too much, and broke the village open without building any allies.

## Actions

The lynch can be stopped. This is what separates the perfect outcome from the punishment ending — the players give the village justice without violence.

- **Crack witnesses and document everything** — Tadek Gajda, Ryszard Dudka, Janina Gajda, Emil Rzepka. Get the truth on paper. Official paper. Census paper. Government weight.
- **Use the phone** — contact por. Witold Skowron or district authorities. The information leaves the village through the wire, not through blood. Once the outside world knows, the mob has no purpose.
- **Keep Ryszard Dudka from snapping** — give him a confession path instead of a violence path. He wants to act — let him act by *testifying*, not by organising a mob.
- **Build trust with the village** — if enough people know the truth AND believe justice is coming, the mob never forms.
- **Zbigniew Gajda helps** — he's willing to take responsibility. If the players reach him before Ryszard Dudka snaps, Zbigniew Gajda confessing publicly defuses the mob.

**If the players prevent the lynch AND complete the ritual AND support Michał Pytlak's plan — the dead are at rest, the living are saved, and the guilty face legal justice. The well is just a well. The cycle breaks.**
