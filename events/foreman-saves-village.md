# Michał Pytlak Saves the Village — The Engineering Ending

**Location:** [UPA bunker](../locations/upa-bunker.md), then rock formation above village
**Present:** [Michał Pytlak](../characters/foreman.md), players
**Available:** After [foremans-flood-fight](foremans-flood-fight.md) — players agree to help with Pytlak's plan

## Trigger

Michał Pytlak has a plan to save the village from the flood. Not sandbags. Not prayers. **Explosives.** He wants to blow a channel through a rock formation to redirect the floodwater away from %NEW_VILLAGE% — a controlled detonation that sends the water somewhere it can't hurt anyone.

It's insane. It's dangerous. It might work.

## Setup

The UPA bunker in the forest contains old partisan munitions — unstable, decades-old ordnance from the 1940s. Michał Pytlak knows about the bunker (everyone who works the land around the forest does, even if nobody talks about it). He knows there are explosives in there.

Getting them out is the problem:
- The bunker is partially collapsed, flooded in sections, structurally unstable.
- The ordnance is old — possibly degraded, possibly still live. Handling it wrong kills you.
- Someone has to go in, find what's usable, and carry it out without dying.
- **The players can help with this** — or refuse. It's their risk too.

Michał Pytlak knows the terrain. He's worked this land for years — every slope, every drainage path, every place the water naturally wants to go. There's a point where a rock formation acts as a natural dam, directing floodwater toward the village. Blow it, and the water goes around instead of through.

It's not a guess. He's thought about it. He's walked the ground. He's right about the geography. The question is whether anyone trusts a 57-year-old farm overseer with stolen partisan explosives enough to let him try.

## Actions

1. **Believe him** — Michał Pytlak isn't a military engineer. He's a farmer who reads terrain. The players need to look at his plan and decide it's not suicide.
2. **Get the explosives** — Enter the UPA bunker, find usable ordnance, bring it out alive. This costs time and risk.
3. **Support the detonation** — Help place the charges, clear the area, manage the blast. Or stand back and pray.
