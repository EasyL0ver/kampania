# Holy Mass — Anti-Flood Prayer

**Location:** [The church](../locations/the-church.md)
**Present:** [ks. Władysław Pająk](../characters/priest.md), most of the village. [Helena Rzepka](../characters/matrona.md) in the front pew. [Zbigniew Gajda](../characters/wojewoda.md) near the back. [Ryszard Dudka](../characters/neighbour.md). Village women. [Krystian Rzepka](../characters/secondary-characters.md) as altar boy.
**Available:** Day 4 morning. ks. Pająk calls the mass after the flood — prayer for safety, for homes, for deliverance.

## Hook

The church bell rings — slow, deliberate, calling. Audible from anywhere in %NEW_VILLAGE%. Everyone knows what it means. Even with the rain, the sound carries.

## Setup

The whole village comes. Flood scared everyone — even the men who don't usually attend. The church is full, damp coats steaming, mud on the floor. ks. Pająk in vestments. Candles lit. The organ doesn't work (too damp). Hymns are unaccompanied.

The mass is real — ks. Pająk means it. Prayers for the village, for the water to recede, for the families. His voice steady but tired. The well is working on him too — his prayers feel hollow to him even as the village draws comfort.

**Who's here:**
- Helena Rzepka — front pew, dressed properly despite the flood. Krystian beside her in his altar boy cassock.
- Zbigniew Gajda — near the back, standing. Arms crossed. Here because not being here would be noticed.
- Ryszard Dudka — alone, middle row. Head down. Praying hard.
- Barbara Kopacz — absent if Pawełek is already sick. Present if not yet ill.
- Village women — the core attendees. Praying, crying, holding children.

**Who's NOT here:**
- **Janina Gajda is missing.** Her pew is empty. She never misses mass. Never. Not once in 13 years.
- [Tadek Gajda](../characters/wujas.md) — nobody expects him. He stopped coming years ago.
- [Edek Barnaś](../characters/glupek.md) — sometimes comes with Ciotka, sits in the back. Not today.
- [Stanisław Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md) — never comes.
- [Paraskewia Chyłak](../characters/hag.md) — obviously not.

**Composure: 0** — unless the player has faith, then it restores 1.

## Opportunities

Free — no action cost. Available to any player present.

- **The empty pew** — Ciotka's spot. Front-left, near the wall. She sits there every Sunday, every holy day, every special mass. Today it's empty.
  - **Observation:** Helena glances at it. Once. Then doesn't look again.
  - **Read:** ks. Pająk sees it too. Mid-prayer, his eyes go to the pew and stay a beat too long. He doesn't stop. But he saw.

- **The sermon** — ks. Pająk speaks about endurance, about God testing his faithful, about water as purification. Standard. But there's something underneath.
  - **Read:** He's distracted. Going through the motions — the words are right but the man behind them isn't fully there. The well is eating his certainty.
  - **Culture:** The prayer structure is standard Roman Catholic. But he pauses before the intercessions — the moment where a Lemko priest would invoke different saints. He catches himself. Old habit? Or something he's fighting?

- **Helena's performance** — Perfect posture, perfect devotion. Singing every response. The ideal parishioner.
  - **Read:** She's watching the room. Not praying — scanning. Who's here, who's not, who's talking to whom. The committee especially.
  - **Streetwise:** Front pew, best dressed, son as altar boy. She owns this space. The church is another institution she's embedded herself in.

- **Dudka praying** — Head down, lips moving. Not performing. Actually praying.
  - **Read:** Guilt. Not flood-fear — something older, heavier. A man asking God for forgiveness he doesn't believe he deserves.

- **Zbigniew at the back** — Not praying. Observing.
  - **Read:** He's counting heads. Same thing the committee does — census instinct. He knows who's here and who isn't. He noticed Ciotka's absence too. His jaw is tight.

## Actions

### Ask about Ciotka after mass
- **Requires:** Noticed the empty pew
- **Cost:** Free
- **Outcome depends on who you ask:**
  - **ks. Pająk:** Stops removing his vestments. *"She... wasn't here? No. No, she wasn't."* He already knew. He's been thinking about it since the intercessions. *"I'll check on her after."* He means it — and he'll go alone if the players don't offer. → If players don't go first, the priest finds the body.
  - **Helena:** *"She's probably not feeling well. The flood upset everyone."* Dismissive. Calm. Moves on.
  - **Zbigniew:** *"I noticed."* Two words. Then nothing. If pressed: *"I'll send someone."* He won't go himself.
  - **Any village woman:** *"Pani Gajda? That's strange. She's always here. Even when she was sick last winter she came."* Genuine concern. They'll offer to go check.

### Go to Ciotka's house
- **Requires:** Nothing — can be triggered by the empty pew, or independently
- **Cost:** 1 action
- **Outcome:** → triggers [Ciotka Found Dead](ciotka-found-dead.md)

### Talk to the priest after mass
- **Requires:** Nothing
- **Cost:** Free (brief), 1 action (deeper)
- **Outcome:** ks. Pająk is more open during/after mass than at the plebania. The sacred space softens him. He's still guarded, but the flood has shaken everyone — including him. Players who show faith or respect get further here than a cold plebania visit.
  - See [priest character file](../characters/priest.md) for full interaction tree.

## If Missed

The priest notices Ciotka's absence himself. After mass, he goes to check on her — finds the body. The discovery triggers [Ciotka Found Dead](ciotka-found-dead.md) without player involvement. The committee loses the chance to witness the village gathered, to read the room, and to be first at Ciotka's door.
