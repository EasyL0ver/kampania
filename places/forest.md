# The Forest (Bieszczady)

**Type:** Location — wilderness between %NEW_VILLAGE% and %OLD_VILLAGE%

## Overview

Dense forest covering the Bieszczady region. Separates %NEW_VILLAGE% from %OLD_VILLAGE% and the ruins. Mix of old growth and younger trees reclaiming abandoned land. Swampy areas, streams, natural clearings. Easy to get lost; navigation by sight and local knowledge.

## The UPA Bunker — Ziemianka

Deep in the forest, northwest of %NEW_VILLAGE%, hidden among trees and undergrowth.

**Description:**
- Abandoned partisan bunker built in mid-1940s
- Low-ceilinged earth dugout reinforced with rotting timber
- Tunnel network; extent unknown
- Entrance nearly invisible — camouflaged by vegetation and fallen trees

**Current state (1967):**
- Partially collapsed sections; flooded areas; rotting supports
- Unstable; dangerous to explore
- No recent human occupation apparent
- Scattered UPA artifacts: rusted weapons, ammunition, Cyrillic inscriptions on wood, rotting documents

**Hazards:**
- Structural collapse (rotting timber support)
- Flooding in lower sections
- Tunnel disorientation; easy to get lost
- Possible unexploded ordnance

**What players might find:**
- Ventilation shafts visible from hillside (signs of structure)
- Edek Barnaś near entrance or in forest (connection unclear)
- UPA weapons and insignia
- Documents (if legible/preserved)
- Evidence of shelter/refuge

## The Bimber Still

Hidden clearing off one of the forest paths, roughly halfway between %NEW_VILLAGE% and %OLD_VILLAGE%. Near a stream.

**Description:**
- Crude copper still under camouflage netting
- Surrounded by empty sugar sacks, barrels, firewood
- Small fire pit with signs of regular use
- Worn footpath leading to it — subtle but visible to a careful eye

**Current state (1967):**
- Actively in use by Tadek Gajda and his drinking crew (Szymek Kępa, Romek Głowacz, Franek Mucha)
- Smoke and fermentation smell detectable from nearby paths
- Sugar supplied by Helena Rzepka's store; Zbigniew Gajda knows and tolerates it

**What players might find:**
- The still and brewing equipment
- Footprints and worn trail from %NEW_VILLAGE%
- Sugar sacks traceable to Helena Rzepka's store
- Empty bottles, drinking debris

**Red herring value:** Looks like a major secret — hidden forest activity, nervous men, concealment. It's just bimber. See `story-facts\bimber-operation.md` for full details.

**NOT near the well or Paraskewia Chyłak's cabin** — but in the same forest. Players who "solve" the forest after finding the still may stop looking deeper.

## Paraskewia Chyłak's Cabin

Hidden deep in the forest, accessible only to those who know the way.

**Description:**
- Small wooden cabin, weathered and old
- Built or repurposed from pre-war structure (possibly shepherd's hut, forester's shelter)
- Single room; minimal furnishings; hearth/fireplace for warmth and cooking

**Current state (1967):**
- Maintained but sparse; no luxury or excess
- Roof weathered but functional; walls hold
- Barely visible from distance — hidden by trees, undergrowth

**Interior:**
- Ritual items: icons, candles, herbs, prayer materials
- Supplies: Foraged foods (dried mushrooms, berries, roots), preserved items
- Personal effects: Blankets, worn clothes, cooking implements
- No modern conveniences; no electricity, running water

**How she survives:**
- **Foraging:** Mushrooms, berries, herbs, wild game in season
- **Preserved foods:** Dried and pickled items stored through seasons
- **Minimal needs:** Hardened to cold and isolation; survives on very little
- **Trade/barter:** May occasionally exchange foraged goods for essentials from village (unlikely but possible)

**Access:**
- Path from forest but deliberately obscured
- Takes knowledge to find; players would need guidance or extensive searching
- Private; she guards it fiercely

**Story significance:** 
- Safe place for Paraskewia Chyłak to retreat; where she stores knowledge, ritual items, and memories of the massacre
- Reveals her isolation and commitment to the dead — 20 years alone in the forest, maintaining vigilance
- If players find it, they discover her full life and dedication
- Could be refuge in crisis

## Forest Geography

- **Dense vegetation:** Old-growth spruce and fir mixed with younger birch and oak
- **Streams:** Multiple small streams, some seasonal; swampy areas near low points
- **Topography:** Hilly; valleys; ravines; natural clearings
- **Path conditions:** Barely visible paths; overgrown; treacherous when wet
- **Weather:** Fog common; rain penetrates canopy; darkness under dense trees

## Access & Hazards

- **From %NEW_VILLAGE%:** Multiple overgrown paths; 1–2 hours on foot depending on route
- **From %OLD_VILLAGE%:** Forest on all sides; multiple entry points; easy to approach unseen
- **Flooding:** Heavy rain floods low areas; streams swell; swamps become impassable
- **Visibility:** Limited in fog; near-darkness under dense canopy at dusk
- **Wildlife:** Deer, boar, bears (possibly); feral dogs
- **Disorientation:** Easy to get lost; landmarks hard to distinguish

## What Players Can Find

- **Physical evidence:** Paths, shelters, food sources, water sources
- **Bimber still:** Illegal moonshine operation — looks conspiratorial, is just booze (red herring)
- **UPA artifacts:** Weapons, insignia, historical remnants
- **Animals/traces:** Evidence of game, habitation by wild or feral creatures
- **Natural hazards:** Pit traps (natural), streams, steep drops, unstable ground
- **Shelter:** Bunker could serve as refuge in emergency
- **Edek Barnaś:** May appear; his reasons for being in forest unknown

## Story Significance

The forest is a buffer between %NEW_VILLAGE% (order, control) and %OLD_VILLAGE% (secrets, history). It's where Paraskewia Chyłak moves freely; where Edek Barnaś may wander; where evidence or refuge might be found. Rising water will change the forest landscape — streams will swell, new flooding patterns emerge, known paths become impassable.

**For players:** A navigation challenge, a place to get lost, a refuge, and a gateway to %OLD_VILLAGE%'s secrets.
