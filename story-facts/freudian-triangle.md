# The Freudian Triangle

The triangle repeats across both foundational events — the 1947 massacre and the 1954 lynch — with the same structural positions but different people filling them. The Id is constant. The Ego and Superego rotate. And the Superego always falls first.

---

## 1947 — The Massacre Triangle

### Stanisław Rezeń (Butcher) — Id

Already the drive. The civilian auxiliary who "leaned in" when violence came. The soldiers noticed. They used him more. The Id was always there — the military structure just gave it permission. After 1947, Rezeń spent seven years with the awakened drive and no containing structure. A lit match in an empty room.

### Edward Barnaś (Soldier) — Ego

A soldier caught between orders from above and the horror around him. The Ego mediates — he does what he's told, feels the pull, participates. After 1947, the Ego *succeeds*: Edward builds a family, represses the violence, becomes a devoted father. He's the Ego that worked. For seven years.

He is [Tadek Gajda](../characters/wujas.md)'s mirror: same structural position, opposite outcome. Edward held it together. Tadek couldn't. Both Egos — one functional, one collapsed.

### kpt. Henryk Ćwiek (KBW Officer) — Superego

Institutional authority. Career military, chain of command, the voice that says *"clear this village."* Not cruel — professional. The Superego doesn't need to be evil; it just needs to authorize.

**Killed during the massacre** — Dmytro Kosach, [Paraskewia Chyłak](../characters/hag.md)'s UPA lover, shot him dead. His death didn't create a vacuum — it created **fury**. The soldiers turned on the village in rage, every man's Id unleashed through vengeance. The massacre was total because a dead captain demanded blood.

His death created a **seven-year vacuum** (1947–1954) — no authority containing the Id. [Zbigniew Gajda](../characters/wojewoda.md) eventually stepped in, but the gap is where Rezeń's compulsion crystallized.

See [kpt. Henryk Ćwiek](../characters/kbw-officer.md) for full character file.

## 1954 — The Lynch Triangle

### Stanisław Rezeń (Butcher) — Id

Raw drive. Compulsion without conscience. The well found a willing vessel in 1947 and never let go. He doesn't resist because he doesn't want to. The id doesn't negotiate — it wants, and acts. His arc is the id unleashed: when Zbigniew's control erodes, the leash snaps and the drive walks free.

- Compulsion to feed the well
- Predatory interest in new arrivals
- No guilt, no internal conflict — just appetite
- The well amplifies what was already there

### Tadek Gajda (Wujas) — Ego

The mediator that's failing. The ego's job is to negotiate between the id's demands and the superego's restrictions — and Tadek has been doing it with alcohol for 13 years. He loved Hania, he raped Hania, he can't make those two facts coexist. The bottle was his reality principle — numb enough to function, drunk enough to forget.

The well breaks this mechanism. The drinking stops working. He stays lucid. The ego can no longer mediate — fragments leak out, half-confessions surface, the negotiation between impulse and guilt collapses in real time. He's the **weakest link** because the ego is always the first thing to crack.

- Drinks but can't forget
- Half-sentences, sleep-talking, involuntary confessions
- Swings between maudlin guilt and sudden violence
- The cracks become audible

### Zbigniew Gajda (Wojewoda) — Superego

Authority. Control. The rules that contain the id. For 13 years, Zbigniew has managed Rezeń through power and implied threat — assigned him the edge, forbade alcohol, kept him from people. The sołtys containing the village monster the same way the superego contains the id: through prohibition and structure.

But the superego is also the source of guilt and self-punishment. Zbigniew participates in his own private trial every day — not through confession, but through compulsive order. Every rule, every arrangement, every careful decision is penance disguised as governance.

The well turns this into paranoia. Control becomes compulsion. Governance becomes siege mentality. The superego, overloaded, starts destroying what it was meant to protect.

- Contains Rezeń through authority
- Manages the cover-up through structure
- Paranoia amplifies as control slips
- When the superego fails, the id walks free

## The Pattern

The same structural positions, different people:

| Role | 1947 Massacre | 1954 Lynch |
|------|--------------|------------|
| **Id** | Stanisław Rezeń | Stanisław Rezeń |
| **Ego** | Edward Barnaś | Tadek Gajda |
| **Superego** | kpt. Henryk Ćwiek | Zbigniew Gajda |

The Id is constant — the drive never changes vessels. The Ego rotates — Edward succeeded, Tadek failed. The Superego rotates — institutional authority replaced by personal authority. And in both events, **the Superego falls first**: Ćwiek is killed, then Zbigniew cracks.

Edward and Tadek are the same position, different outcomes. One built a family on top of the horror. The other built a bottle. Both Egos trying to mediate between the drive and the guilt. The well shows what happens when mediation fails.

## The 1967 Collapse

The well's influence maps perfectly onto psychic collapse:

1. **Ego cracks first** — Tadek's drinking stops working. Half-truths leak. The mediator fails.
2. **Superego overcompensates** — Zbigniew tightens control, sees conspiracies, makes erratic decisions. The structure becomes rigid and brittle.
3. **Id breaks free** — Rezeń stops obeying. Shows up in the village. Starts drinking. The containment that held for 13 years dissolves.

The village is a single psyche, and the players are watching it have a breakdown.

## Thematic Resonance

The players dream too. The well shows them the id — their own capacity for violence. They wake up shaking. The question the scenario asks: *what do you do with the drive once you've felt it?*

- Edward Barnaś buried it (repression → peaceful life → death)
- Rezeń embraced it (id triumphant → monster)
- Tadek tried to drown it (failed ego → collapse)
- Zbigniew tried to control it (superego → paranoid authority)
- The players choose
