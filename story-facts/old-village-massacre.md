# The %OLD_VILLAGE% Massacre

**Type:** Core story secret

## What Happened

During Operation Vistula (1947), a KBW unit commanded by [kpt. Henryk Ćwiek](../characters/kbw-officer.md) arrived at %OLD_VILLAGE% to forcibly deport its Lemko inhabitants. The unit included [Edward Barnaś](../characters/soldier.md) as a soldier and [Stanisław Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md) as a civilian auxiliary recruited for his knowledge of local terrain.

The villagers rebelled — refused to leave, resisted the soldiers. Dmytro Kosach — a UPA guerrilla fighter hidden in the village, [Paraskewia Chyłak](../characters/hag.md)'s lover — **shot kpt. Ćwiek dead.** The soldiers, enraged by their captain's killing, turned on the village. The massacre wasn't chaos — it was retribution. They killed the entire rebelling population.

The three participants map onto Freud's structural model — see [The Freudian Triangle](freudian-triangle.md). The Superego (Ćwiek) died first. His death didn't leave a vacuum — it unleashed fury. Every soldier's Id broke free through rage. The Ego (Edward) was swept along. The Id (Rezeń) found what it had been waiting for.

## The Cover-Up

The cover-up doesn't protect the soldiers from justice — it protects them from their own chain of command. What happened at %OLD_VILLAGE% was a **military failure:**

- They didn't detect the UPA presence before entering the village
- They lost their commanding officer during a routine deportation
- After [kpt. Ćwiek](../characters/kbw-officer.md) died, nobody gave orders — the massacre was **uncontrolled**, not authorized
- They killed the entire village — women, children, elderly — not as counter-insurgency but as soldiers avenging their captain

The state wanted villages **cleared**, not massacred. Orderly deportation, train cars, paperwork. Not dead officers and wells full of bodies. Every soldier in that unit faced courts-martial for incompetence, not war crimes.

So the cover-up was simple:

- File the village as "evacuated successfully"
- Report [kpt. Ćwiek](../characters/kbw-officer.md) as KIA in a separate UPA engagement, or MIA
- The village is supposed to be empty anyway — nobody comes looking for deported Lemkos
- The forest takes over

**The double irony:** years later, [por. Witold Skowron](../characters/officer.md) and the state apparatus learn about the massacre and decide to bury it too — but for completely different reasons (political embarrassment, state crime). Two cover-ups stacked on top of each other, protecting different people from different consequences. The soldiers' silence and the state's silence overlap perfectly — which is why the secret held for twenty years.

For nearly 20 years, the secret has held.

## Why It Matters Now

The Solina Dam project will flood the area where %OLD_VILLAGE% stands. The government committee (the players) is sent to assess what will be lost. But the ruins of %OLD_VILLAGE% contain evidence of the massacre — remains, bullet marks, signs that this was not a peaceful evacuation.

The water is coming. If the dam fills, the evidence disappears forever. The second erasure completes the first.

## Thematic Weight

- The state that committed the crime is now sending bureaucrats to unknowingly bury it.
- The rain keeps falling — the land itself is trying to wash this away.
- The players must decide what they do with the truth, if they find it.
