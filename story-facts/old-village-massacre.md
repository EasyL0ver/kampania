# The %OLD_VILLAGE% Massacre

**Type:** Core story secret

## What Happened

During Operation Vistula (1947), soldiers arrived at %OLD_VILLAGE% to forcibly deport its Lemko inhabitants. The villagers rebelled — refused to leave, resisted the soldiers. The situation escalated and the soldiers opened fire, killing the entire rebelling population.

## The Cover-Up

The participating soldiers — including Edward Barnaś — made a pact of silence. The massacre was never reported, never documented. Officially, %OLD_VILLAGE% was evacuated like every other village in the region. No bodies were recorded, no incident filed.

For nearly 20 years, the secret has held.

## Why It Matters Now

The Solina Dam project will flood the area where %OLD_VILLAGE% stands. The government committee (the players) is sent to assess what will be lost. But the ruins of %OLD_VILLAGE% contain evidence of the massacre — remains, bullet marks, signs that this was not a peaceful evacuation.

The water is coming. If the dam fills, the evidence disappears forever. The second erasure completes the first.

## Thematic Weight

- The state that committed the crime is now sending bureaucrats to unknowingly bury it.
- The rain keeps falling — the land itself is trying to wash this away.
- The players must decide what they do with the truth, if they find it.
