# The Haunting of %OLD_VILLAGE%

**Type:** Story fact — village superstition / hidden truth

## What the Villagers Believe

The colonists of %NEW_VILLAGE% believe %OLD_VILLAGE% is **haunted**. Strange lights at night, sounds in the ruins, a feeling of being watched. Nobody goes there willingly. Parents warn children. Even Zbigniew Gajda avoids it. The ruins of the *cerkiew*, the overgrown cemetery with Cyrillic inscriptions, the crumbling *chyże* — it all feels wrong, cursed, poisoned.

This fear is convenient for everyone. It keeps people away from the well. It keeps people away from the evidence.

## The Truth

**It's Paraskewia Chyłak.** The old Lemko woman who has lived in the forest since 1947 sneaks into the ruins of %OLD_VILLAGE% — specifically to the remains of the *cerkiew* — to perform a traditional Lemko ritual for appeasing the dead and preventing angry spirits from returning.

In Lemko folk belief, the dead who are not properly mourned — whose funerals were never held, whose graves were never tended, whose names were never spoken — become restless. Their spirits haunt the living, bring illness, spoil crops, poison the land. The only remedy is ritual: fire, incense, offerings of food, prayer in the old tongue, the opening of symbolic doors so the dead may pass through and find peace.

Paraskewia Chyłak has been performing these rites for **20 years** — alone, in a ruined church, for an entire village of massacred people who were never buried properly. She is the only person still tending to their souls.

## What the Players Might See

- **Candle light** flickering in the ruins of the *cerkiew* at night.
- **The smell of incense** or burning herbs near %OLD_VILLAGE%.
- **Small offerings** — bread, honey, grain — left at the church threshold or near the cemetery.
- **Footprints** in the mud around the ruins, too small for a man.
- **Singing** — faint, in a language nobody in %NEW_VILLAGE% speaks anymore.

## Why It Matters

- The "haunting" is the first clue that something is wrong with %OLD_VILLAGE% — it's not just empty, it's *tended*.
- Paraskewia Chyłak is performing the ritual because she knows the dead are there — she may have witnessed the massacre or its aftermath.
- The villagers' fear keeps them away, which has inadvertently protected the evidence in the well for 20 years.
- If the players investigate the "ghost," they find Paraskewia Chyłak — and she is the key to everything.
