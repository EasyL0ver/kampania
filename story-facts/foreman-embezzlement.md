# %FOREMAN%'s Embezzlement — Red Herring

**Type:** Story fact / red herring / investigation trap

## What It Is

%FOREMAN% (%STEFAN%) has been skimming from the PGR farm for years. Redirecting supplies, inflating worker counts on paper, pocketing the difference. Small-scale, steady, careful. He does this **without %WOJEWODA%'s knowledge** — the sołtys trusts him to run the farm and doesn't check the books closely.

## How It Works

- **Phantom workers:** The ledger lists more laborers than actually work the farm. %FOREMAN% collects their wages.
- **Supply diversion:** Fertilizer, seed, feed, and tools ordered in bulk — a percentage quietly disappears. Sold privately or bartered in neighboring villages.
- **Inflated expenses:** Repair costs, transport fees, veterinary bills — all padded. The difference goes into %FOREMAN%'s pocket.
- **%ZOFIA% helps:** She inflates kitchen supply orders — more flour, sugar, lard than the workers actually eat. The surplus is theirs.

It's not a fortune. It's a slow, steady trickle — enough to live a little better than a farm overseer should, to put something aside, to buy things for %STASZEK% that other village kids don't have.

## What Players Might Notice

- **The ledger doesn't add up.** If they inspect PGR records as part of their census/damage assessment work (which is their cover story), the numbers are visibly wrong. More workers on paper than in the fields. Supply costs that don't match what's actually on the farm.
- **%FOREMAN% lives too well.** His clothes are slightly better. %ZOFIA%'s kitchen has supplies other households don't. %STASZEK% has a decent coat. Small things, but noticeable next to %BARBARA%'s poverty.
- **Workers grumble.** %JOZEK% or %PIOTR% might mention that supplies are always "short" despite what was supposedly ordered. They assume it's the state being stingy. They don't suspect %FOREMAN% — he's the boss.
- **%HALINA% at the store** notices %FOREMAN% occasionally sells or trades goods that look like they came from the farm. She gossips about it but nobody cares enough to act.

## Why It Looks Suspicious

A government committee doing census and damage assessment would naturally look at PGR records. When they find discrepancies, the investigation reflex kicks in:

- Why is %FOREMAN% lying about worker numbers? Is he hiding someone? Protecting someone?
- Where are the missing supplies going? Into the forest? To someone in hiding?
- Is %WOJEWODA% in on it? Is this how he funds the cover-up?
- Why does %FOREMAN% get nervous when the committee asks about the books?

**The answer to all of it:** a man stealing grain and pocketing wages. Mundane PGR corruption, replicated in every state farm across Poland.

## %FOREMAN%'s Reaction When Pressed

- **Stage 1 — Deflection.** "The books are the books, comrade. You'd have to ask the district office about allocations." Bureaucratic runaround.
- **Stage 2 — Nervousness.** If players push, he gets visibly uncomfortable. Starts watching them. Asks %ZOFIA% if they've been in the kitchen. Checks his ledger.
- **Stage 3 — Loyalty pivot.** If truly cornered, he runs to %WOJEWODA% — not to confess, but to warn him that the committee is "poking around the farm books." This triggers %WOJEWODA%'s paranoia for entirely the wrong reason. %WOJEWODA% assumes the committee found something about the lynch or the massacre. %FOREMAN% thinks it's about his skimming. Neither understands the other's fear. The miscommunication makes both men act guilty and evasive — which makes the players think they're onto something big.

## Connections to Larger Story

- **%WOJEWODA% doesn't know.** If the players expose the embezzlement to him, it creates a genuine surprise — and a distraction. %WOJEWODA% now has a farm crisis to deal with on top of everything else. Does he punish %FOREMAN%? Cover for him? Use it as leverage?
- **%FOREMAN% doesn't know about the lynch.** He arrived in %NEW_VILLAGE% after 1954. His guilt is financial, not moral. He has zero information about the core mystery.
- **%ZOFIA% might crack differently.** If her husband's skimming is exposed, she might try to trade gossip for leniency — offering up village rumors she's overheard in the kitchen. Some of those rumors might actually touch the lynch. The information is real, but it comes from a self-interested source trying to save her husband, not from genuine witness testimony.

## For the GM

The embezzlement is a lever with multiple uses:
- **Time sink:** Investigating the books burns an action and leads nowhere on the main mystery.
- **Paranoia amplifier:** %FOREMAN% running to %WOJEWODA% makes the whole village tighten up — everyone thinks the committee knows more than they do.
- **Bargaining chip:** If players discover the fraud but don't report it, they gain leverage over %FOREMAN% (and through him, access to the farm, workers, %BARBARA%'s routine).
- **Comedy of errors:** Two men panicking about two different secrets, each making the other look guiltier. Classic misdirection.
