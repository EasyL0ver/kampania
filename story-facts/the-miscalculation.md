# The Miscalculation — %NEW_VILLAGE% Will Flood

**Type:** Core story event / inciting incident (1967)

## What Happened

%PROFESSOR% discovers that the hydrological calculations for the Solina reservoir were wrong. The original projections indicated only %OLD_VILLAGE% and the low-lying areas would be submerged. But the calculations underestimated the flood zone — compounded by an **exceptionally heavy 1967 rain season**, the water will swallow **%NEW_VILLAGE% as well**.

The village must be evacuated **now**.

## Historical Basis

⚠️ *Fictionalized but grounded in real events:*
- Locals reported the actual flooding exceeded original projections.
- Intense rainfall during the final filling phase (Sept 1967) pushed water levels beyond predictions.
- Graves and structures that weren't dealt with in time were submerged unexpectedly.
- PRL suppressed any admission of miscalculation.

## Story Function

This is the **inciting incident** — the reason the committee (players) is sent, and the pressure cooker that forces everything to the surface:

1. **Time pressure** — the water is rising, there's no time for careful process.
2. **Panic** — the colonists face sudden displacement. History repeating.
3. **The well** — any evacuation or preparation work near %OLD_VILLAGE% risks exposing the remains.
4. **Conflicting interests** — some villagers want to flee, some want to protect secrets, some want the truth.
5. **The rain** — it never stops. It's the ticking clock made physical.
