# %WIFE% and %JUNIOR%'s Secret Investigation — Red Herring

**Type:** Story fact / red herring / parallel investigation

## What It Is

%WIFE% has been quietly investigating the events of 1954 for years. Not systematically — she has no training, no resources, no allies. Just a woman who can't stop noticing. But in the last year or two, she gained something she never had before: **%JUNIOR%**.

He's 21 now. Old enough to be trusted. Old enough to ask questions. And old enough to have noticed on his own that something is deeply wrong with this family.

## How It Started

%WIFE% never sat %JUNIOR% down and told him. It happened gradually — a comment here, a question there. %JUNIOR% grew up watching his father's locked silences, his uncle's drinking, his aunt %MATRONA%'s controlled performances. He started asking his mother: *"Why does uncle %WUJAS% drink so much?"* and *"What happened to the family that used to live in %CIOTKA%'s house?"*

%WIFE% didn't answer directly. But she stopped deflecting. She'd say things like *"Nobody talks about that"* or *"Ask your father and watch his face."* That was enough. %JUNIOR% started watching. Then noticing. Then asking more.

Now they have an unspoken pact. She watches the house — %WOJEWODA%'s papers, his meetings with %OFFICER%, the locked silences between the siblings. %JUNIOR% works the outside — listening to %WUJAS% when he's drunk, poking around the village, visiting %OLD_VILLAGE% when nobody's watching.

## What They've Found

Not much. Enough to be dangerous, not enough to understand:

- **%WIFE%'s collection:** A stained shirt from 1954 she never threw away. A button she found in the yard that didn't match any of %WOJEWODA%'s clothes. Newspaper clippings about missing persons that never matched anyone. Notes in her handwriting — dates, observations, fragments of overheard conversations. Hidden in a flour tin in the pantry.
- **%JUNIOR%'s fragments from %WUJAS%:** Drunken mumbles. *"She didn't deserve it."* A name — %JAGNA% — spoken once, never repeated. %WUJAS% grabbing %JUNIOR%'s arm one night and saying *"Don't ever be like us"* before passing out. Nothing concrete. Everything damning in tone.
- **Fresh tracks at %OLD_VILLAGE%:** %JUNIOR% has been visiting the ruins. He's seen the well, the cemetery, the cerkiew. He doesn't know what he's looking for. He's felt the wrongness of the place without understanding it.
- **A partial timeline:** Between them, they've roughly established that something happened in 1954, that it involved %SOLDIER%'s family, and that all four siblings were somehow connected. They don't know what. They don't have proof. Just a shape in the dark.

## Why It's a Red Herring

When the committee arrives and starts investigating, they'll encounter signs of %WIFE% and %JUNIOR%'s parallel investigation:

- **Fresh footprints at %OLD_VILLAGE%** that aren't %HAG%'s. Someone has been there recently.
- **%JUNIOR% asking %NEIGHBOUR% strange questions** — the players hear about this second-hand.
- **%WIFE% acting suspiciously** — hovering near conversations, watching the committee, clearly wanting to approach but pulling back.
- **%JUNIOR% disappearing into the forest** — same pattern as the bimber crew, same pattern as a conspirator.
- **The hidden collection** — if players search %WOJEWODA%'s house, they might find %WIFE%'s flour tin before they find anything of %WOJEWODA%'s. A stained shirt and notes that look like a cover-up dossier.

Players will spend time trying to figure out who this second investigator is. Are they being followed? Is someone destroying evidence ahead of them? Is %JUNIOR% working for his father? Is %WIFE% a co-conspirator maintaining a blackmail file?

**The truth:** A mother and son who've been doing exactly what the players are doing — just worse, longer, and with no authority behind them.

## The Danger

%WIFE% and %JUNIOR%'s investigation is the most dangerous thing in the village — not for the players, but for *them*:

- **If %WOJEWODA% finds out**, his wife and son have been building a case against him. He'll see betrayal, not justice. What he does next depends on how cornered he feels.
- **If %MATRONA% finds out**, she'll move to neutralize it. She's done this before — she knows how to handle people who become problems.
- **If %BUTCHER% finds out**, %JUNIOR% has been visiting %OLD_VILLAGE% and asking about 1954. The man who tried to strangle a toddler to eliminate witnesses won't hesitate.
- **If %OFFICER% finds out**, state security has a new problem — civilians digging into events the state wants buried.

The players' investigation and %WIFE%/%JUNIOR%'s investigation are on a collision course. When they meet, it can go several ways:

- **Alliance:** The players gain two inside sources with 13 years of observation. %WIFE%'s fragments + the committee's authority = real progress. But it puts all of them at greater risk.
- **Catastrophe:** The players inadvertently expose %WIFE%'s investigation to %WOJEWODA% by asking the wrong question, showing the stained shirt, or mentioning %JUNIOR%'s visits to %OLD_VILLAGE%. Now the family knows there's a traitor inside the house.
- **Mistrust:** %WIFE% decides the committee can't be trusted (they're too close to %WOJEWODA%, too friendly with %OFFICER%) and actively works against them — hiding evidence, warning %JUNIOR% to stop, retreating into silence.

## %JUNIOR%'s Role

%JUNIOR% is not just helping his mother — he's becoming his own person through this investigation. The spoiled sołtys's kid who drank with %WUJAS% and slept with %BARBARA% is now carrying a secret that could destroy his entire family. He's growing up fast.

His closeness to %WUJAS% is now double-edged: he genuinely likes his uncle, AND he's mining him for information. Every drinking session is both real bonding and covert interrogation. %JUNIOR% hates this — using the uncle who loves him as a source.

His visits to %OLD_VILLAGE% are reckless. He doesn't know what's in the well. He doesn't know about %HAG%. He's stumbling around a place full of secrets he's not ready for, leaving tracks that others can follow.

## For the GM

This subplot serves multiple purposes:

- **Red herring:** Players waste time identifying the "second investigator" and sorting friend from foe.
- **Time pressure:** %JUNIOR%'s reckless visits to %OLD_VILLAGE% risk alerting %WOJEWODA%, %BUTCHER%, or %MATRONA%. If the players don't find %WIFE% and %JUNIOR% quickly, the family might find them first.
- **Emotional payoff:** When the players finally connect with %WIFE%, it's one of the most powerful moments in the story — two investigations meeting, a woman who's waited 13 years for someone to believe her.
- **Tragedy potential:** If the players fumble the connection — expose %WIFE% to %WOJEWODA%, or arrive too late — this subplot becomes the story's deepest wound. The one person trying to do the right thing, destroyed by the people who came to help.
