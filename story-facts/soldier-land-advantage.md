# %SOLDIER%'s Land Advantage

**Type:** Story fact / motive element

## What Happened

When Polish colonists began settling the emptied Bieszczady in the early 1950s, %SOLDIER% had an edge no one else did — he already knew the area. He'd been stationed there during Akcja Wisła as part of the deportation (and massacre) forces.

He used this knowledge to claim the best piece of land in %NEW_VILLAGE%, arriving before others could scout the terrain.

## Why It Matters

This deepened the resentment against %SOLDIER% from multiple angles:

- **The siblings** (%WOJEWODA%, %WUJAS%, %MATRONA%, %CIOTKA%) — Lemko descendants watching a man who helped destroy their people now living fat off stolen land.
- **Other settlers** — suspicion and jealousy toward someone who seemed to have insider knowledge or connections.
- **The irony** — %SOLDIER% profited directly from the massacre. The land was only available because its owners were dead by his hand.

## Connection to the Lynch

One more reason for the village to turn on %SOLDIER%. He wasn't just a stranger with a dark past — he was visibly thriving on it.
