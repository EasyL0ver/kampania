# Performing the Ritual — Recovering or Continuing What Was Lost

**Type:** Story fact / player mechanic / endgame path

## The Problem

The well's influence is growing. %HAG%'s rites — her 20 years of prayers, offerings, and acknowledgement — are the only thing containing it. But the containment is weakening as the water rises.

**If %HAG% is killed** (see `story\event-butcher-kills-hag.md` — this is optional, not guaranteed), the containment breaks completely. The players must reconstruct the ritual from fragments.

**If %HAG% survives**, she can teach the players directly — but she's 77, exhausted, and her rites are failing against the rising water. She needs help. The players performing the ritual alongside her — or taking it over — may be the only way to hold the well in check through the final days.

Either way, the players must eventually stand at the well and do what %HAG% has done alone for 20 years: acknowledge the dead.

**The ritual can be reconstructed.** Not perfectly. Not the way she did it. But the dead may accept an imperfect version — not because the form is correct, but because the intent is real.

The ritual was never about the words. It was about acknowledgement.

## What the Ritual Requires

Three things, each held by a different source. The players must assemble all three:

### 1. The Form — How to do it

**Source: %HAG%'s cabin.**

Her cabin in the forest contains the physical components and the shape of the rite:
- Icons arranged in a specific pattern
- Candle placement — where and how many
- Offerings: bread, honey, grain, water from a stream (not the well)
- Incense herbs — dried bundles hanging from the rafters, identifiable by smell
- **A hand-drawn diagram** on a scrap of cloth or bark — not instructions in any language the players read, but a visual layout of the rite. Where to stand, where to place offerings, the pattern of movement around the well. Players can follow it like a map even without understanding the theology.

This is the easiest piece to find — if they know the cabin exists. %HAG% may have told them about it before her death. If not, finding it requires tracking her movements or getting directions from someone who knew her.

### 2. The Words — What to say

**Source: %BABCIA%.** (See `characters\babcia.md`)

%BABCIA% — %BARBARA%'s elderly relative — speaks more Lemko than Polish. She knows the **panakhyda** — the Lemko memorial prayer for the dead. She knows the old songs. She can teach the players the words, or speak them herself if brought to the well.

**The difficulty is not %BABCIA% — it's the chain of permission.**

%BABCIA% is frail. She can't cross the forest alone. She needs %BARBARA% to take her. And **%BARBARA% won't do it without %PRIEST%'s blessing.** She's a devout Catholic. Taking her grandmother to perform a Lemko pagan rite at a haunted well is heresy. The one authority she trusts is %PRIEST%. If he says no, she says no.

So the real obstacle isn't finding the words — it's **breaking %PRIEST%.** The man who's spent 13 years choosing silence must now actively endorse something his faith condemns. He must say: *"Go. Take her. Do what I cannot."* See `characters\priest.md` — "The Ritual — The Final Bottleneck" section.

**If %BABCIA% comes to the well herself** — an old Lemko woman, standing where her people were killed, speaking the prayers they were never given — it is the most powerful version of the ritual. But getting her there means: earning %BABCIA%'s trust → earning %BARBARA%'s cooperation → breaking %PRIEST%'s resistance → crossing the forest in the rain with a woman who can barely walk. Every step costs time the players may not have.

### 3. The Truth — What to acknowledge

**Source: The players themselves.**

This is the piece %HAG% always had and the players must find on their own. The ritual doesn't work as performance. It works as acknowledgement. The dead don't need correct pronunciation or proper icon placement. They need someone to stand at the well and **say what happened.**

- Name the dead. As many as can be named. The Lemko villagers. %SOLDIER%. His wife. %JAGNA%. %HAG% herself.
- Say what was done to them. Not in legal terms. In human terms. *They were killed. They were thrown in this well. Nobody spoke for them. Nobody mourned them.*
- Accept that it happened. Don't explain it. Don't justify it. Don't promise justice. Just acknowledge it.

**This is the psychoanalytic core.** The ritual is therapy. Name the trauma. Acknowledge it. Hold it. Refuse to let it stay buried. The exact words don't matter. The intention does. %HAG% understood this instinctively. The players must arrive at it through investigation, empathy, and courage.

## How Players Discover This Path

The ritual path should feel **earned, not obvious.** Possible routes:

- **%HAG% told them directly** — if they spoke to her before she died, she may have explained the rites, or at least the principle: *"The dead need to be named. The dead need to be told they are remembered."*
- **The cabin tells them** — finding %HAG%'s supplies and the diagram gives them the form. They still need the words and the understanding.
- **%BABCIA% tells them** — if they've built trust with her, she can explain the Lemko tradition of prayers for the unquiet dead. She may even say: *"Someone was doing it. I could hear her singing on the wind. The singing has stopped."*
- **%PRIEST% tells them** — despite his silence on the cover-up, %PRIEST% understands the theology. If the players describe the dreams, the well, the escalating dread — he may recognize what's happening and, grudgingly, point them toward the Lemko rites as something the Church wouldn't endorse but can't deny. *"There are older ways to tend to the dead. I don't believe in them. But something in that well might."*
- **Intuition from the dreams** — if the dreams are bad enough, the players may simply *know*. The dead are telling them what they need. Not in words. In pressure. In the feeling of standing at the well and knowing that someone needs to speak.

## Performing the Ritual

### The Setup
- At the well, at %OLD_VILLAGE%
- Night is best (traditional) but not required
- Candles, incense, offerings arranged per %HAG%'s diagram
- At least one person must speak — the words, the names, the truth

### What Happens

The ritual is not a spell. There is no flash of light, no supernatural confirmation. The story never commits to whether it "works" in a metaphysical sense. But:

- **The dreams ease.** Not immediately — but that night, for the first time since %HAG%'s death, the players sleep. Not well. But they sleep.
- **The atmosphere lifts.** The oppressive dread that's been crushing %NEW_VILLAGE% loosens. Not gone — but breathable.
- **The rain doesn't stop.** This is not a fairy tale. The flood is still coming. The dead are still dead. The well still holds its bones. But something has shifted. The wound isn't healed — but for the first time in 20 years, someone other than %HAG% has acknowledged it.
- **%BUTCHER%'s dreams return.** The compulsion was satisfied by %HAG%'s death. The ritual reopens the wound — properly this time. His calm breaks. He stops sleeping again. The well has new guardians, and they're undoing what he did.

### Imperfect Is Enough

The players will get the words wrong. The pronunciation will be terrible. The offerings might be substituted. The diagram is unclear in places. None of that matters. %HAG% did it perfectly for 20 years and even she was barely holding on. What the dead need isn't perfection. It's presence.

*"I am here. I see you. I know what happened. I will not forget."*

That's the whole ritual. Everything else is form.

## For the GM

This is the story's counter-move to the well's escalating influence. Whether %HAG% is alive or dead, the ritual is the players' chance to push back — not with violence, not with evidence, but with the same tool %HAG% used: acknowledgement.

**If %HAG% is dead:** The ritual is reconstruction — harder, more desperate, assembled from fragments. The players are replacing a 20-year relationship with raw intent.

**If %HAG% is alive:** The ritual is reinforcement — the players join her, adding their voices, their knowledge of the lynch victims she didn't know about, their willingness to name *all* the dead. This version is more powerful because %HAG%'s expertise guides the form while the players supply the missing truth.

It costs an action. It requires assembling pieces from multiple sources. It demands that the players stand at a well full of bones in the rain and speak to the dead. It's the hardest, strangest, most emotionally demanding thing the story asks of them.

And it's the only thing that works.
