# Well Influence — Per NPC

How [the well](the-well.md) affects each character as it strengthens.

## Zbigniew Gajda (Wojewoda)

Amplifies **paranoia and need for control.** Not guilt — he has none.

- Sees conspiracies — committee's questions feel pointed, Marek's absences deliberate
- Tightens access to phone. Finds reasons to keep people from %OLD_VILLAGE%. Orders feel like lockdown
- **Losing grip on Rezeń.** The arrangement frays. Rezeń stops listening, shows up in village, starts drinking. The dog is off the leash
- Sleep deteriorates. Sits up reviewing who he spoke to, what he said. Careful politician becomes a man counting enemies
- Decisions get erratic — overreactions, sudden hostility toward non-threats
- **Dream:** the well isn't a hole in the ground — it's a hole in his story, and everyone is looking down into it

## Tadek Gajda (Wujas)

Amplifies **guilt and self-destruction.** Already the weakest vessel — 13 years of drinking to forget.

- Drinking increases but stops numbing. He stays painfully, horribly lucid. The one thing that kept him functional fails
- Fragments leak out. Half-sentences at the wrong moment. Hania's name muttered in sleep. *"She didn't deserve it"* to whoever's next to him
- Sleep fills with the sound of the well — water dripping, echoing, rising. Wakes hoarse, as if shouting
- Volatility spikes. Swings between maudlin weeping and sudden rage. Violence closer to the skin

The well doesn't need to break Tadek. He was already broken. It just makes the cracks **audible**.

## Helena Rzepka (Matrona)

Erodes her **performance.** Doesn't shatter her — creates micro-cracks:

- Comments too sharp — flashes of contempt she'd normally suppress. Real woman surfaces before mask snaps back
- Intrusive thoughts. Catches herself staring at Emil without rehearsed warmth. Loses thread of conversation mid-sentence
- Over-corrects: more devout, more generous, more publicly perfect — reads as desperate to anyone paying attention
- Control over Emil tightens — more surveillance, pressure, reminders of what he owes her
- **Dream:** not the dead but **Hania** — alive, young, just looking at her. Not accusatory. Just *seen*

## Janina Gajda (Ciotka)

Amplifies **fear and paralysis:**

- Stops leaving the house — the world outside feels threatening
- Clings to Edek with increasing desperation. Senses something pulling him toward %OLD_VILLAGE%
- Prayers become frantic, repetitive, compulsive — not devotion but terror. God feels further away
- Flinches from her siblings — the family feels like a trap closing
- **Dream:** the night she saved Edek — except she doesn't make it in time. Reaches for the baby and her hands pass through him. Wakes and checks on the boy. Every night

## Edek Barnaś (Głupek)

The well draws him **physically and mentally.** He was supposed to die there — unfinished business.

- Wanders toward %OLD_VILLAGE% more often. Escapes Ciotka's watch. Found at the forest edge, staring toward the ruins
- Agitation grows: rocking, hitting himself, crying without cause. The village reads it as his condition worsening — it's not. Something is waking up
- **The well gives him back the truth** — not in words, but in fragments, feelings. Starts flinching from Wojewoda and Wujas too. Pulls away from Ciotka without understanding why
- At the well, he stands still. Agitation stops. Something recognises the place — the gravity. He was supposed to be down there

**The well doesn't give Edek dreams — it gives him knowledge he can't process.**

## Stanisław Rezeń (Butcher)

**The compulsion is winning.** The well's pull is stronger than his self-imposed discipline:

- **The wolves break the door open.** Wojewoda authorizes Rezeń to hunt wolves. First time in 13 years among people. Containment breaks
- **Doesn't go back.** Wolf job ends. Rezeń stays. He was called, authorized. The well's pull and the practical excuse merge
- **Starts showing up.** At the store, near the farm. The man who avoided everyone is suddenly *around*
- **Starts drinking.** The one rule he never broke. Everyone who remembers 1954 knows what alcohol did to those men
- Not aggressive — yet. Calm, almost pleasant. The practical self-awareness that kept him contained is dissolving

**The compulsion is to feed the well.** Not guard it — put something *in* it. His interest in players isn't territorial — it's predatory.

**He may kill Paraskewia.** If pressure drives both to the well on the same night. Not guaranteed — players can prevent it.

## Ryszard Dudka (Neighbour)

Amplifies **guilt of inaction.** Already heavy — now physical.

- Hears screaming at night — Hania's voice, drunken shouting, dogs. May be real or the well. Can't tell
- Sleeplessness. Sits at the window staring at Ciotka's house — the same window from that night
- Jumpiness, paranoia. Every knock is Rezeń. Every stranger's question an accusation
- Easier to crack but harder to *use* — coherence going. Rambles, circles, contradicts. Truth in shards mixed with panic
- **Dream:** lying in bed, hearing the screaming, reaching for the curtain — and this time, the curtain won't close

## Stefania Kopacz (Babcia)

The well **heals her mind.** Dementia reverses — lucidity returns day by day. Nobody notices.

- Prayers come back complete — every word, every inflection, every pause
- Memories return. Names, faces. Stops confusing people
- Cruelty sharpens from random to deliberate
- Grows restless. Stares at forest. Refuses to sleep. Listens for singing that has stopped

## Barbara Kopacz

The well has almost nothing to work with. No guilt, no connection to crimes. She's one of the few the well can't reach.

What the well *does* is darken the world around her:

- Notices Wujas drinking more, muttering, avoiding her
- Notices Babcia changing — singing, restlessness, sharpened memory
- Worries about Pawełek playing too far from the house
- Stays warm. Stays open. The village is falling apart and she's still making tea

## Pawełek Kopacz

No guilt, no trauma. The well has nothing to amplify — but it still calls. Children are curious.

- Wanders toward %OLD_VILLAGE%. Finds the well. Drinks the water
- **Falls ill.** High fever, convulsions, delirium. Floodwater contaminates everything — nothing supernatural required
- In fever-dreams mutters things a 4-year-old shouldn't know — *"the water under the ground," "the old woman"*

## ks. Władysław Pająk (Priest)

Erodes his **theological certainty** — the foundation his 13-year bargain rests on.

- Prayers feel hollow. Mass feels like performance. The confessional feels like a trap
- Becomes irritable, short with parishioners, distracted during services
- **Dreams:** the well itself. Stone walls descending into darkness. Water rising. Prayer in a language that isn't Latin and isn't Polish
- His justification — *"I chose the living over the dead"* — collapses. The dead aren't staying dead. The living aren't staying sane
- The well breaks him with **irrelevance.** His prayers aren't working. Something older than his theology is moving

This is what eventually makes him grant permission for the Lemko rite.

## Irena Gajda (Wife)

Amplifies **desperate need for answers.** Strips away 13 years of caution.

- Gets bolder — approaches players' investigation closer, takes more risks
- Pushes Marek harder — sends him to %OLD_VILLAGE% at bad times
- May accelerate the breakthrough — cracks the truth faster
- **Dream:** the night of 1954, but she's outside instead of inside. Can see everything. Can't move, can't shout

## Emil Rzepka (Painter)

The well reaches him through **his art.** Mostly innocent — no guilt to amplify.

- Starts painting the well. Not deliberately — it appears uninvited. A dark circle in landscapes. Stones that shouldn't be there
- The water in his paintings turns black. He doesn't notice at first. When he does, he can't stop
- The paintings are the well's way of being seen by someone with clean hands

## Michał Pytlak (Foreman)

The well gives him **inhuman fortitude.** No guilt to amplify, no wound to reopen.

- Doesn't sleep. Doesn't stop. Works sandbags until hands bleed, then works more
- 57 years old, outworking everyone. A strength that has no business being in a man his age
- Keeps him away from Zofia — the one person who sees what's happening
- **If players intervene:** he comes home, he's human again. But flood defense collapses
- **If nobody intervenes:** the well keeps him standing until work is done. Then lets go. What's left is a broken man looking at his dead wife

## Zofia Pytlak

The well drains her warmth. No guilt — instead, it takes.

- First tired. Then flat. Then empty. Then gone
- Food burns. Plates unwashed. She sits and stares. Light behind her eyes is off
- **Zofia kills herself.** Quietly. In the house. Nobody finds her in time
- The well's cruelest trick: gave her husband inhuman strength to keep him away from the one person who could save him

## Paraskewia Chyłak (Hag)

Not affected BY the well — she **contains** it. See [the-ritual.md](the-ritual.md) for full details.

- Performs Lemko rites for 20 years — fire, incense, offerings, prayer
- Names the dead, speaks to them, tells them to rest
- She is the treatment. Rezeń is the illness. The well is the patient

## por. Witold Skowron (Officer)

**None.** Does not arrive until the very end. Not present during the well's escalation.
