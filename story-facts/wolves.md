# The Wolves — Ambivalent Threat

**Type:** Story subplot / atmosphere / red herring / potential encounter

## The Naturalistic Reading

A small pack (5–7 wolves) ranges in the higher forest above [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../locations/old-village-ruins.md). The Bieszczady has always had wolves — but 20 years of depopulated Lemko villages removed the human presence that kept them at bay. The [PGR farm](../locations/pgr-farm.md)'s livestock is the closest easy prey for miles.

The 1967 rain season is the trigger. Exceptionally heavy rains flood the wolves' usual hunting grounds at higher elevation. Prey scatters. The pack moves downhill — toward the farm and its sheep.

Wolf predation on PGR livestock is a [documented problem](../historical%20context/14-wolves-bieszczady-1960s.md) in 1960s Bieszczady. The state response was culling — but in a remote village with no resident gamekeeper, the wolves answer to nobody.

## The Other Reading

The wolves do nothing supernatural. They're wolves — they range, hunt, howl. But the **people** interpret them through their own fears and traditions:

- Villagers already fear[Paraskewia Chyłak](../characters/hag.md). Now the wolves are back too. *"She controls them. She sends them."* That's **their fear**, not fact.
- [Edek Barnaś](../characters/glupek.md) hears the howling and goes still. Listens. Doesn't flinch. That's **his strangeness**, not wolf magic.

The ambivalence lives in how characters react to normal wolf behavior — not in wolves doing anything that requires a supernatural explanation. The rain is enough. The livestock is enough. Everything else is human projection.

## Mechanical Role

**The wolves are the mechanism that breaks [Stanisław Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md)'s containment.** Not the well's abstract influence — a practical crisis that forces [Zbigniew Gajda](../characters/wojewoda.md) to make a bad trade.

The chain:
1. Wolves attack PGR livestock for weeks before the game
2. [Ryszard Dudka](../characters/neighbour.md) — the village hunter — takes it upon himself to deal with it. He fails. Weeks of hunting, no kills. The pack is smart, the terrain is vast, one man with a rifle isn't enough.
3. [Zbigniew Gajda](../characters/wojewoda.md) doesn't want to authorize [Stanisław Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md). That means breaking 13 years of containment. He waits, hoping Dudka succeeds.
4. Day 1-2 of the game: fresh damage. Zbigniew assesses the dead livestock and makes the call. He uses the [Tadek](../characters/wujas.md) channel — tells [Tadek](../characters/wujas.md) to ask [Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md) to deal with the wolves.
5. [Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md) hears "we need you" and interprets it as **authorization**. He doesn't just hunt wolves at the forest edge — he shows up at the PGR, among workers, among people. First time in 13 years. With permission.
6. **Dudka is furious.** He failed, and the village's answer is to bring in a man he's 90% sure murdered someone. The bystander who couldn't protect the village in 1954 couldn't protect it now either. And now the killer gets the job.
7. **Zbigniew is furious.** He asked for pest control at the forest edge, not reintegration. But the damage is real, the committee is watching, and he can't pull Rezeń back without explaining why.
8. Rezeń won't go back. He was called. The containment is broken — not from neglect, but from **use**. Zbigniew made the mistake of sending a real request through the maintenance channel, and Rezeń used it as a door.

## Timeline

### Before the committee arrives (3–4 weeks)
First kills — a sheep here, a sheep there. [Michał Pytlak](../characters/foreman.md) reinforces the pens. [Ryszard Dudka](../characters/neighbour.md) takes it on himself — he's the village hunter, this is his job. Goes out with his rifle, tracks the pack, can't close. The wolves are smart, the terrain is vast, and one man isn't enough. [Zbigniew Gajda](../characters/wojewoda.md) knows about the problem but hopes Dudka handles it. The alternative is [Stanisław Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md), and that means breaking 13 years of containment.

### Day 1
Players arrive. Wolf damage is visible at the [PGR](../locations/pgr-farm.md) — bloodstains in the pen, patched fences, nervous animals. [Michał Pytlak](../characters/foreman.md) mentions it in passing. Dudka, if encountered, is tight-lipped about his failure.

Fresh kill overnight — another sheep. Zbigniew assesses the damage at the PGR. **Players CAN witness this if they're at the farm.** He stands in the pen, counts the dead, and realizes Dudka isn't going to solve this. The practical man makes the practical decision.

He tells [Tadek](../characters/wujas.md) to ask [Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md).

### Day 1–2
[Tadek](../characters/wujas.md) makes the visit. This is a different visit — not cigarettes and a butchering job. A real request from [Zbigniew](../characters/wojewoda.md). *"Zbyszek says deal with the wolves."* For the first time in 13 years, the village is calling [Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md).

Rezeń shows up at the PGR. Not at the forest edge — at the farm, among workers, among people. With his dogs. He assesses the damage, reads the tracks, starts working. He's good at it. Unnervingly good. **He doesn't leave.**

### Day 2+
The village reacts:
- **Dudka** is furious — see [The Wolf Hunt](../events/event-wolf-hunt.md)
- **Zbigniew** is furious — asked for pest control, got reintegration
- **Pytlak** doesn't understand the tension. He has a wolf problem and a man who can solve it. He's grateful.
- **Tadek** sees Rezeń at the PGR and something breaks. The man he's been managing from the edge house is *here*, among people. The go-between role is dead.

### Day 3–5
Wolves are dealt with (or driven off, or the weather shifts). Zbigniew says *"Go home."* Rezeń doesn't. He was called. He was authorized.

The howling continues — or doesn't. The GM decides based on atmosphere, not on a supernatural trigger. The wolves are weather. They don't respond to rituals or deaths. But if the howling happens to stop the night after the ritual — or the night after [Paraskewia Chyłak](../characters/hag.md) dies — nobody in the village will believe it's coincidence. **Let the players draw their own conclusions.**

## The Wolf Hunt

Full playable scene: [The Wolf Hunt](../events/event-wolf-hunt.md).

[Dudka](../characters/neighbour.md) has been failing for weeks. [Zbigniew](../characters/wojewoda.md) authorizes [Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md) via [Tadek](../characters/wujas.md). Rezeń shows up at the PGR. Dudka is furious. The scene is about the political earthquake, not the wolves.

## NPC Reactions

**[Michał Pytlak](../characters/foreman.md):** Another front in a losing war. Flood, dead worker, and now wolves. The losses are real — every sheep goes in the committee's damage report. He doesn't care about village politics. He needs the wolves gone. When Rezeń shows up, Pytlak is grateful. A useful pair of hands. That's all he sees.

**[Stanisław Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md):** The wolf authorization is a door. For the first time in 13 years, the village *called* him. Not a back-channel cigarette run — a real request, through the proper channel, from the foreman himself. He shows up at the PGR and does the work. Good at it. Unnervingly good. But the point isn't the wolves — the point is being *among people* again. At the farm gate. Talking to workers. Handling dogs where everyone can see. He won't go back to the edge. He was needed.

**[Zbigniew Gajda](../characters/wojewoda.md):** Made the call. Regrets it immediately. He asked for pest control at the forest edge — got reintegration into village life. Can't pull Rezeń back without explaining why the village's most competent tracker isn't allowed near people. Every hour Rezeń spends at the PGR is another hour of containment crumbling. The superego let the Id off the leash "just this once." There is no "just this once."

**[Ryszard Dudka](../characters/neighbour.md):** Furious. He spent weeks hunting the wolves — failed. The village's response isn't more support, better equipment, or patience. It's to bring in **the man Dudka is 90% sure murdered someone.** He heard Hania Barnaś die. He heard the screaming, the dogs. He saw shapes dragged toward the forest. And now the village is employing that man because Dudka wasn't enough. The bystander who couldn't protect the village in 1954 couldn't protect it now. His rage about the authorization feeds directly into his [escalation arc](../characters/neighbour.md) — gasoline for the mob violence ending.

**[Edek Barnaś](../characters/glupek.md):**Not afraid of the wolves. [Stanisław Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md)'s dogs terrify him — but when the howling starts, he goes still. Stands at the window and listens. Not distressed — calm. The contrast with his usual agitation is striking. Players can read it any way they want.

**[Paraskewia Chyłak](../characters/hag.md):** She's lived in their territory for 20 years. The pack knows her — she's part of the landscape. She's never been attacked. The village has seen her fires and heard her chanting at [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../locations/old-village-ruins.md) — rites for the dead, appeasing the unquiet. Nothing to do with wolves. But the village connects the dots: rituals + wolves that avoid her = *"She's summoning them."* This projection arms existing hostility and feeds the pressure that leads to [The Well Confrontation](../events/event-well-confrontation.md). → [hag-blamed-for-wolves](../clues/clues.md#hag-blamed-for-wolves)

## Connections to Existing Threads

### Containment-breaking mechanism
The wolves are the **concrete trigger** for [Rezeń's containment](../characters/butcher.md) breaking. Currently, Zbigniew contains Rezeń through [Tadek's visits](../events/event-wujas-visits-butcher.md) — cigarettes, odd jobs, a thread keeping the id at the edge. The wolf crisis forces Zbigniew to send a **real request** through that channel. Once Rezeń hears "we need you," the maintenance channel is dead. He doesn't need Tadek's visits anymore. He's been called.

The visits stop — not (only) because Tadek is too drunk, but because there's nothing left to maintain. The go-between role was hijacked for a real request, and the pretense is gone. See [Tadek Visits the Butcher](../events/event-wujas-visits-butcher.md).

### Dudka's escalation fuel
Dudka's anger about the authorization feeds directly into the [mob violence ending](../story/ending-punishment-lynch.md). He's been building rage since 1954. The wolf failure adds a new layer: not just guilt of inaction, but the humiliation of inadequacy. He tried to protect the village, failed, and the village turned to the man he believes is a murderer. A hunter with a rifle, watching a killer walk freely among villagers because *he wasn't enough* — that's a powder keg.

### Tadek's break
Tadek sees Rezeń at the PGR and something specific cracks. The man he's been managing from the edge house — carrying cigarettes to, keeping contained — is now among people, handling dogs, talking to workers. The arrangement Tadek maintained for 13 years is visibly dead. This accelerates his drinking spiral alongside the well's influence.

### Butcher's dogs vs. the wolves
Domesticated predators vs. wild ones. The dogs obey [Zbigniew Gajda](../characters/wojewoda.md)'s containment — just like [Stanisław Rezeń](../characters/butcher.md). The wolves answer to nobody. The parallel is thematic, not magical: tamed violence vs. wild violence.

### "The witch sent the wolves"
The village needs someone to blame. [Paraskewia Chyłak](../characters/hag.md) lives in the forest, the wolves don't touch her, and villagers have seen her fires and heard her chanting at [%OLD_VILLAGE%](../locations/old-village-ruins.md). She's performing rites for the dead — appeasing the unquiet, nothing to do with wolves. But the village connects the dots: rituals + wolves that avoid her = *"She's summoning them."* The projection arms existing hostility with a concrete grievance: she's not just creepy, she's **attacking them**. This feeds anti-hag pressure, and when [Rezeń kills her](../events/event-well-confrontation.md), nobody mourns. *"Good riddance."* Same silence as 1954.

### Dudka's surveillance
The wolf authorization puts Rezeń loose in the village — and intensifies Dudka's 13-year habit of watching him. Dudka sees Rezeń leave at night, notes the direction, but **won't follow past the tree line on his own.** Two things break the pattern: players tell him about Rezeń's nighttime well visits (info they don't understand but Dudka does), or players find the well and bring Dudka along for backup. See [The Well Confrontation — Prevention](../events/event-well-confrontation.md#prevention).

### Flood defense pressure
If players ignore the wolves → attacks continue → Pytlak diverts workers to guard livestock instead of sandbags → the engineering ending gets harder. Practical crisis with real resource cost.

### Action economy
Joining the hunt costs an action. Reinforcing the farm costs an action. Both are legitimate uses of the committee's time (livestock losses go in the damage report). But every hour hunting wolves is an hour not interviewing NPCs.

## For the GM

The wolves are **weather** — atmospheric, persistent, escalating. They're not a monster encounter. The players never fight a wolf. They hear the howling, see the damage, feel the presence. The wolves frame every night scene and every trip into the forest.

**The ambivalence is key — but it lives in the characters, not the wolves.** The wolves never do anything supernatural. They hunt, they howl, they range. What makes them ambivalent is that Głupek goes calm when they howl, villagers blame the hag, and the howling sounds different when you've been dreaming about the well. The wolves are a screen onto which every character projects their own fear, guilt, or knowledge. The GM never confirms anything. The wolves are wolves. Everything else is human.
