# The Village's Silence

**Type:** Story fact — social dynamics

## %SOLDIER%'s Unpopularity

%SOLDIER% was **wildly unpopular** in %NEW_VILLAGE%. The reasons stacked up:

- Took the best land using insider knowledge from his military service
- Seen as an outsider who profited unfairly
- The Lemko-descended residents (the siblings) had ethnic reasons to hate him
- General distrust — a former military man in a village of drifters and settlers

When he and his family disappeared, **nobody mourned**.

## The Unnamed Villagers

The rest of %NEW_VILLAGE%'s population falls into two categories:

1. **Arrived after the lynch** — don't know what happened. Accepted the story that %SOLDIER%'s family "left" or "disappeared." Didn't ask questions.
2. **Were there but chose silence** — knew or suspected something, but %SOLDIER% was so hated that nobody cared to investigate. Some may have seen or heard things that night. All of them looked the other way.

## The Result

The lynch wasn't just covered up by the three perpetrators — it was **absorbed by the village**. A collective, passive complicity. Nobody did it, nobody saw it, nobody asks about it. %SOLDIER%'s family simply ceased to exist, and the village moved on.

Only %GLUPEK% remains — a living reminder that everyone pretends not to understand.
