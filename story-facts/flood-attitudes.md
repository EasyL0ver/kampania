# Flood Attitudes — Per NPC

How each character reacts to the census committee and the coming flood.

## Zbigniew Gajda (Wojewoda)

Sees flooding before it's announced. Opportunity and threat: water destroys evidence, but also destroys everything he built. Pragmatic — protect his people first, secrets second. **Largely cooperative.** Opens doors, introduces people, translates the village for outsiders. Genuinely useful. Urges thorough census for compensation. Wants players to keep quiet about flooding — panic stops cooperation.

## Irena Gajda (Wife)

Agrees with Zbigniew — cooperate, census everyone, prepare for evacuation. Pragmatist married to a pragmatist.

## Marek Gajda (Junior)

The flood is the best thing that's ever happened to him — ticket out, fresh start. Cooperates enthusiastically: not civic duty, self-interest. Wants every fence post documented for maximum reimbursement so he can leave for the city.

## Tadek Gajda (Wujas)

**Doesn't care.** Too deep in the bottle and too far gone. Won't be hostile, won't be helpful. Just absent. Players will likely dismiss him early — that's the trap.

## Helena Rzepka (Matrona)

Follows Zbigniew's lead — cooperate, help, comply. The flood itself matters less to her than keeping the story intact.

## Janina Gajda (Ciotka)

The census terrifies her. Government people writing names, houses, land — paper trails that can't be answered cleanly. Whose house? Who is Edek's father? Why does a single woman have the best plot? She will lie — practiced, quiet, consistent. **She will never break for government strangers.** They are the people who could take Edek away.

## Edek Barnaś (Głupek)

Doesn't understand the committee, the census, or the flood. Strangers make him nervous. **But he needs to be in the census to survive.** Documenting him means answering questions that lead back to the lynch.

## Stanisław Rezeń (Butcher)

Doesn't care — no property, no family, no stake. **Looking forward to the flood** — the water will fill the well, cover it. Something in him says *yes*. Anticipation.

## Ryszard Dudka (Neighbour)

Angry at the government. The state is flooding them out. Hostile to players before anything else — not hiding a secret, furious about losing his home. Guilt stays buried under legitimate rage.

## Stefania Kopacz (Babcia)

Ready to die. Won't evacuate — not stubbornness, peace. Will nod, smile, and stay exactly where she is.

## Barbara Kopacz

Answers census questions warmly, volunteers extra. **Refuses to name Pawełek's father** — sweet about it, immovable. When she learns the flood is coming, she's scared — her house is going underwater. Single mother, 4-year-old, no money, nowhere to go. But even scared, she makes tea and asks if *you're* alright.

## Pawełek Kopacz

He's 4. Doesn't understand any of it.

## ks. Władysław Pająk (Priest)

**Unfriendly by default.** Government people. Communists, probably. Polite but cold. Unhelpful. The key to unlocking him is faith — genuine belief, respect for scripture, respect for his role. Without that, players get nothing. **Can call a lynch on the players** if they push too hard — a sermon, a word to the right people. The same authority that keeps the peace can break it.

## Emil Rzepka (Painter)

**Secretly wants Hania Barnaś in the census.** Won't say it — Helena would destroy him. But when the government starts writing names, something in him wants to say: *there was a woman named Hania Barnaś. She lived here. Write her down.* The census is the last record before the flood erases this place. If players get him truly alone — away from Helena — this is what comes out.

## Michał Pytlak (Foreman)

**Wants to stay and fight.** While others plan evacuation, he says no. We sandbag. We dig channels. We save the farm. A man who built something real looking at clipboards telling him it's going to be destroyed. He's the only voice saying *we can beat this*. Puts him at odds with the committee — every hour on paperwork is an hour not spent digging drainage.

## Zofia Pytlak

Shares Michał's stubbornness about staying. As the crisis deepens, she shifts to wanting him to *stop* — come home, let the water come, let go.

## Wanda Mazur (Widow)

Asks the committee to include her dead husband in the census. Not fraud — remembrance. *"He lived here. He worked here. He should be counted."* Thematic mirror for Emil's later request about Hania.

## Paraskewia Chyłak (Hag)

**Wants to survive.** Not a martyr. The flood will destroy her cabin. Won't ask for help — invisible for 20 years. But if players find her and explain the evacuation, she'll come. Writing her into the census gives her paperwork. Twenty years of isolation ended by a stranger keeping a promise.

## por. Witold Skowron (Officer)

Not a village resident. Arrives by car, doesn't stay. Knows the dam's purpose — the government chose this area for flooding deliberately, in part to cover evidence of the massacre.
